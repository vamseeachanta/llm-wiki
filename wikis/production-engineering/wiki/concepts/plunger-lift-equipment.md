# Plunger Lift Equipment

## Scope

The hardware inventory for a plunger-lift installation — surface and downhole. Equipment is simple compared with rod pump or ESP; this is part of why plunger lift has the lowest total cost.

## Surface equipment

- **Wellhead lubricator** — vertical section above the wellhead Christmas tree where the arriving plunger is caught and held during afterflow; provides sealing pressure containment
- **Catcher / striker** — mechanical or magnetic device at lubricator top that detects and catches plunger arrival
- **Motor valve** — pneumatically or electrically actuated valve in the flow line that opens (plunger trip) and closes (shut-in)
- **Surface controller** — PLC or specialty controller automating cycle timing (see [Plunger Lift Cycle Optimization](plunger-lift-cycle-optimization.md))
- **Flow-line meter** — measures gas + liquid production for cycle-time tuning

## Downhole equipment

- **Bumper spring** — at bottom of tubing string; absorbs plunger impact when falling and bounces it back upward
- **Standing valve** (optional) — check valve preventing fluid from flowing back down through bumper spring
- **Tubing nipple** — landing seat for bumper spring + standing valve assembly

## Plunger types

### Turbulent-seal (solid)

- Single solid metal plunger; relies on close clearance to tubing wall for seal
- Simplest; lowest cost
- Best for wells with clean gas (no sand or scale)

### Brush

- Has wire brushes around the body; conforms to tubing ID; tolerates some tubing irregularity
- Common general-purpose choice

### Pad

- Spring-loaded pads create dynamic seal against tubing
- Better seal at lower velocities; good for declining wells

### Bypass / two-piece

- Plunger has a hollow center with a bypass valve that opens during fall (fast drop) and closes during rise (sealed lift)
- Reduces cycle time by enabling faster plunger drop
- Higher cost; specialty use
