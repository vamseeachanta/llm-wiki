# ISO 17824 — Sand Control Screens

## Scope

ISO 17824, *Petroleum and natural gas industries — Downhole equipment — Sand screens*, is the international standards body's canonical specification for downhole **sand-control screens** intended for **open-hole gravel-pack** completions. It defines functional requirements, design verification expectations, and acceptance-test methodology for screen assemblies that retain formation sand (or, in gravel-pack service, retain the gravel that retains the formation sand) while permitting fluid production.

The standard pairs with **ISO 17825** (sister standard for cased-hole gravel-pack screens). The two-standard split mirrors the operationally-distinct service envelopes — open-hole pack placement is a different mechanical and hydraulic problem than cased-hole pack placement, and the screen-acceptance criteria differ accordingly.

ISO 17824 does **not** prescribe the operator's screen-family selection (standalone vs prepacked vs expandable vs premium-mesh). Its scope is bounded to the manufacturer-side qualification methodology that gives apples-to-apples comparison between screen products from different vendors. Operator selection logic lives in [Sand Control Screens](../concepts/sand-control-screens.md).

## Why operators read it

- **Vendor-comparison framework** — without a common qualification methodology, vendor-quoted retention-rating and collapse-rating numbers cannot be compared. ISO 17824 is the international anchor.
- **Acceptance-test reference** — completion engineers specifying gravel-pack screen assemblies often write acceptance tests against ISO 17824 verification thresholds.
- **Procurement-spec citation** — international operators (especially those bound by IOGP or national-oil-company procurement frameworks) cite ISO 17824 in the screen-procurement scope-of-work.
- **Litigation and regulator audit trail** — when post-completion sand-influx events occur and the screen is implicated, the ISO 17824 manufacturer qualification records become part of the failure-investigation chain of custody.
- **Cross-recognition with API equivalents** — most screen vendors qualify to ISO 17824 / 17825 *and* to API-equivalent practices simultaneously, so the standard is a procurement convergence point.

## Standard structure (high level)

The standard is structured around the lifecycle of a screen assembly from manufacture to qualification, with the structural intent stable across editions. The exact section labels and concrete numerical thresholds are not transcribed here (the standard is paywalled); the structural intent is:

- **Functional requirements** — definitions of screen retention behaviour, fluid-flow capacity, and mechanical integrity envelopes the screen must satisfy in service.
- **Design verification expectations** — the engineering documentation a manufacturer must produce to demonstrate that a candidate screen design will meet functional requirements, including material selection, weld-procedure qualification, and assembly-tolerance budgets.
- **Acceptance-test methodology** — the laboratory tests applied to qualification samples (collapse rating, tension rating, sand-retention performance against a reference particle-size distribution, flow-capacity through the filter element).
- **Documentation and marking** — the records that must accompany a screen joint into the field, including material certificates, dimensional inspection records, and the marking convention by which a screen joint can be traced back to its manufacturing batch.

Operators reading vendor data sheets should always confirm which ISO 17824 verification clauses the candidate screen has been qualified against; partial qualification (e.g. mechanical integrity tested but sand-retention performance not tested against the operator's specific PSD) is a known source of post-installation surprise.

## Cross-references

- **Sister standard** — ISO 17825 (cased-hole gravel-pack screens). Most operators specify both standards in any sand-control procurement that may include either open-hole or cased-hole sections.
- **Adjacent standards** — NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156 (sour-service material requirements applicable to screen base-pipe and weld metallurgy in CO2 / H2S service); API-equivalent screen-qualification practices are commonly cited alongside ISO 17824 in dual-standard procurement specs.
- **Vendor-archetype framing** — the major sand-control vendors (Halliburton, Schlumberger, Baker Hughes, Weatherford) and screen-specialist independents publish screen data sheets citing ISO 17824 / 17825 clauses; concept-level only, no proprietary screen-design content reproduced here.

## Notes

- ISO 17824 was first issued in 2009. Operators specifying screen-procurement acceptance tests should pin to a specific edition in their AFE / scope-of-work documents and confirm the current revision year via the ISO catalog.
- The standard's pairing with ISO 17825 (cased-hole gravel-pack screens) reflects a deliberate decision by the standards committee to keep the two service envelopes separate; operators running both open-hole and cased-hole gravel packs in a single field should specify both standards.
- ISO 17824 is paywalled. This wiki paraphrases the structural intent and references the standard for operators who already have a licensed copy. No verbatim text reproduced.
