# ALARP (As Low As Reasonably Practicable)

## Scope

Engineering / integrity asset management — see scope-boundary block in [index.md](../index.md). Risk-tolerability decision principle for safety-critical engineering systems.

## Definition

ALARP is the principle that residual risk shall be reduced as low as reasonably practicable, where "reasonably practicable" requires gross disproportion between the cost of risk-reduction measures and the benefit gained, judged against the magnitude of risk.

## Methodology / Mechanics

- Tolerability triangle: intolerable / tolerable-if-ALARP / broadly acceptable.
- Cost-benefit analysis with gross disproportion factor (GDF) graduated by risk level.
- Documented ALARP demonstration is a regulator-facing artifact.
