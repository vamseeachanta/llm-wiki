# Asset Lifecycle

## Scope

Engineering / integrity asset management — see scope-boundary block in [index.md](../index.md). This page is the **canonical authority** for the asset-lifecycle topic in this wiki; other pages (notably the ISO 55000 family overview) reference it rather than re-defining the term.

## Definition

The asset lifecycle is the end-to-end span of phases an asset passes through: concept, design, construction / acquisition, commissioning, operation, maintenance, modification, life-extension, decommissioning, and disposal.

## Methodology / Mechanics

- Each phase has decision-gates with explicit handover artifacts.
- Whole-life cost (CAPEX + OPEX + DECEX) is the integrating financial measure; whole-life risk is the integrating integrity measure.
- Late-life phases (life-extension, decommissioning) have outsized integrity-management consequences.
