# BSEE (2024) — Deepwater Dynamic Pipeline Riser Life Extension Perspectives and Process

Raw URL: https://www.bsee.gov/sites/bsee.gov/files/2024-10/BSEE%20Deepwater%20Riser%20Life%20Extension%20Perspectives%20and%20Process.pdf

Publisher: Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement (BSEE), Pipeline Section, US Department of the Interior.

Author: Angie D. Gobert (BSEE Pipeline Section).

Format: 12-slide conference presentation deck (960 × 540 pts, PDF v1.4, ~480 KB). Presented at the DNV Technology Week — Life Extension Session, October 22, 2024, New Orleans, Louisiana.

Document character: regulator-staff conference presentation. **Not** a Notice to Lessees (NTL), **not** a formal guidance document, **not** a regulation. Captures BSEE Pipeline-Section perspectives on the existing 30 CFR 250 framework and the life-extension review process for deepwater dynamic pipeline risers under their jurisdiction.

## Scope

The document covers **dynamic pipeline risers** in the US Outer Continental Shelf — specifically:

- Steel Catenary Risers (SCR).
- Steel Lazy-Wave Risers (SLWR).
- Unbonded flexible pipe risers.
- Free-Standing Hybrid Risers (FSHR).

The presentation's working definition is a riser "having a length of the riser not affixed to the production facility." This is a production / export riser scope; the document does **not** address drilling marine risers (separate regulatory regime under Subpart D / drilling rules), static / top-tensioned production risers, or land/onshore pipeline risers.

## Relevance to this wiki

The presentation maps onto the asset-management wiki's existing concept structure on three axes:

1. **Life extension methodology** — the document outlines the US OCS regulatory expectations for extending a dynamic riser's approved life beyond its initial 20-year design horizon. Direct cross-reference to [[life-extension]] and the broader [[integrity-management-cycle]].
2. **Integrity Management Program (IMP)** — BSEE's perspective on what an IMP for a deepwater dynamic riser must contain (assessments-from-inspection-triggers, inspection-data validation, record management, operational history) cross-references [[risk-based-inspection]] and [[fitness-for-service]].
3. **Regulator + CVA workflow** — the CVA (Certified Verification Agent) interaction model under 30 CFR 250.910(b) and 250.916 is the operational mechanism by which life-extension assessments enter the regulatory record. This is a US OCS-specific instantiation of the more general life-extension-decision pattern covered by [[life-extension]] and [[dnv-rp-g101]].

The presentation cites **API RP 1160** (managing system integrity for hazardous liquid pipelines) and **API RP 2RIM** (integrity management for offshore production assets) as the technical-IMP basis BSEE expects operators to apply.

## Key claims (as presented)

- **Dynamic-riser population (US OCS, 2024-10)**: approximately 250 dynamic risers installed; oldest are roughly 25 years old; approximately 186 in active service; approximately 60 are in some phase of, or have completed, the life-extension process. These are presenter-stated counts without sourcing, treat as 2024-Q4 ballpark.
- **CVA review is mandated for new floating-platform dynamic risers** under 30 CFR 250.910(b), via the Platform Verification Program, for both ship-shaped and non-ship-shaped buoyant offshore facilities.
- **CVA duties during the design phase** are governed by 30 CFR 250.916(a) — the CVA performs an independent design-assessment ensuring the riser is designed to withstand environmental and functional load conditions appropriate for the intended service life at the proposed location.
- **IMP content expected by BSEE for a deepwater dynamic riser** includes (per the presentation's enumeration): assessments triggered by inspection findings, inspection-data validation against assessment inputs, record and data management, operational history, corrosion data, local environmental data, and storm data.
- **Wall thickness evaluation requirement**: must be evaluated by inline inspection (ILI) **or** other direct assessment — neither approach is mandated exclusively.
- **Life-cycle elements** (presented in cycle-diagram form on the deck): Monitoring → Inspection → Maintenance & Repair → Engineering Analysis & Life Extension Application. Specific element examples cited: pressure / flow-rate / fluid-composition for Monitoring; ILI / external-visual / ultrasonic / corrosion-coupons / valve-testing for Inspection; VIV-suppression replacement for Maintenance.
- **Life-extension process** (8-step sequence as presented):
  1. Initial life: up to 20 years; riser approved for operation.
  2. During operation: company collects and evaluates riser data (environmental, loading, material).
  3. 1–3 years before end of life: company submits life-extension plan + CVA nomination to BSEE.
  4. BSEE reviews plan: approves OR requests modification.
  5. CVA performs independent review of the company's assessment plan and independent analysis of remaining riser life.
  6. CVA makes life-extension recommendation in CVA report to BSEE.
  7. BSEE reviews CVA report: accepts OR requires additional analysis by CVA.
  8. Company submits pipeline modification application; BSEE approves up to 10-year life extension based on CVA recommendation.
- **Maximum extension granted**: up to 10 years beyond the initial 20-year approved life, contingent on CVA recommendation. Total max riser operational life under this framework is therefore 30 years, though the deck does not state whether sequential 10-year extensions are permissible.
- **Forthcoming regulatory updates** (as of 2024-10): Subpart A (General) industry-standards-incorporated-by-reference update is past comment-period and moving toward final publication; Subpart J (Pipelines) update is being prepared for NPRM; Subpart Q (Decommissioning) is in early draft.

## Limitations of this source

- **Not authoritative regulation text.** This is a conference presentation by a BSEE staff engineer at a DNV industry event. The 30 CFR text remains the binding instrument. Operators must consult the regulation directly for compliance; this deck is a perspective summary.
- **No numeric acceptance criteria beyond life-duration caps.** The deck does not provide riser-fatigue-life-margin thresholds, residual-wall-thickness rejection criteria, or any other quantitative gating value an analyst would need to perform an actual life-extension calculation. Practitioners must rely on the cited API RP 1160 / API RP 2RIM / API RP 17G / DNV-OS-F201 / DNV-RP-F206 for normative content.
- **Sparse on data-quality expectations.** The deck enumerates the data classes expected (corrosion, environmental, storm, wall thickness) but does not specify minimum sampling cadence, sensor accuracy bands, inspection-coverage percentages, or how data gaps from earlier in service life should be treated.
- **Scope-limited.** Dynamic pipeline risers only — does not cover drilling marine risers (Subpart D), static production risers, or non-deepwater installations. Practitioners working on static-riser life extension or drilling-riser life extension cannot use this document as primary guidance.
- **Jurisdiction-limited.** US Outer Continental Shelf only. Operators in UK / Norway / Brazil / Australia / SE Asia waters work under different regulators (HSE, PSA, IBAMA, NOPSEMA, etc.) with different review-process structures, even when applying the same API / DNV standards.
- **Statistics are presenter-stated only.** Riser-population counts (~250 installed, ~186 in service, ~60 in life extension) are unsourced in the deck. Treat as 2024-Q4 BSEE-internal-record approximations, not as audited figures.
