# Research: competitor-market — 2026-04-23

## Key Findings

- **Sesam (DNV) 2026 H1: DNV 2026 Edition (effective 1 July 2026) restructures floating offshore column-stabilized/TLP standards (OS-C103/105/106); time-domain FOWT analysis methods (Direct Load Generation, Load Reconstruction, Response Reconstruction) remain focus. Zero subsea pipeline, on-bottom stability, or cathodic protection module announcements for 2026 H1. Sesam's 30+ module suite maintains enterprise positioning via Veracity cloud platform (enterprise negotiated licensing). Market consolidation signal: DNV strategic focus on FOWT + fish farm composites (new in 2026 edition), not oil & gas subsea expansion.** ([DNV 2026 Edition structural standards](https://www.dnv.com/news/2026/dnv-rules-2026-hearing-period-now-open/), [Sesam for floating structures](https://www.dnv.com/services/sesam-modules-for-floating-structures-2411/), [Sesam for offshore wind](https://www.dnv.com/services/sesam-for-offshore-wind-modules-2442/))

- **SACS Offshore Structure (Bentley) 2026: Cloud-first differentiation stable at $13,328/year subscription; Azure parallelization (10x speedup for 100+ load case batches) remains competitive moat. Zero 2026 module announcements identified; latest updates focus on dynamic analysis workflow enhancements (retained frequency-domain results, vessel damping forces, anisotropic constraint stiffness). Subscription pricing unchanged from 2024 baseline — no 2026 price increase signaled. Enterprise segment (100+ load cases, 10+ simultaneous analyses) benefits most; commodity calculator segment (single-case design) favors SkyCiv/TheNavalArch cost advantage.** ([SACS Offshore Structure](https://www.bentley.com/software/sacs-offshore-structure/), [SACS pricing G2](https://www.g2.com/products/sacs-offshore-structure/pricing))

- **OrcaFlex 11.6 series (Orcina) 2026: Stable version series with no 2026 breaking changes anticipated. Version 11.6 enhancements (vessel child object coupling, expanded frequency-domain analysis, Python variable loads for parametric sensitivity, constraint anisotropy 6×6 matrix) are production-ready and enable parametric design iteration. Python API (11.6c documented) supports automated load case generation and batch processing. Market signal: OrcaFlex remains industry baseline for dynamic riser/mooring analysis; no competing tools at comparable capability + price point ($500–2000/year commercial license).** ([OrcaFlex software](https://www.orcina.com/orcaflex/))

- **OpenFAST v5.0 (February 2026 update): Adds marine turbine buoyancy loads, generalized floating design evaluation, wave energy developer support. Remains renewable energy focused (wind/wave turbines, not subsea risers/cables). Free, open-source ecosystem (paired with Blue Kenue hydrodynamic preprocessing) creates low-cost entry point for floating platform loads + hydrodynamics. No threat to OrcaFlex's moat in dynamic riser analysis; complementary preprocessing workflow (OpenFAST vessel loads → OrcaFlex dynamic analysis → aceengineer fatigue assessment).** ([OpenFAST documentation](https://openfast.readthedocs.io/), [OpenFAST marine energy](https://www.nlr.gov/water/open-fast), [Blue Kenue hydrodynamic mesh](https://nrc.canada.ca/en/research-development/products-services/software-applications/blue-kenuetm-software-tool-hydraulic-modellers))

- **SkyCiv 2026 updates: Load Combination Generator rebuilt (load groups, patterns), Foundation Design + Connection Design enhancements, Canadian S16:24 support (February 2026), Offshore + Marine modules support DNV + ISO standards. Zero 2026 announcements for specialized subsea domains (VIV, cathodic protection, API 579-1 fitness-for-service); commodity calculator space remains generic structural checks (beams, plates, connections). Pricing model: free tier + paid cloud subscriptions. Competitive position: cost-advantage on generic design checks; no domain expertise moat.** ([SkyCiv monthly highlights March 2026](https://skyciv.com/product/skyciv-monthly-highlights-march-2026/), [SkyCiv offshore](https://skyciv.com/industries/offshore-oil-and-gas/), [SkyCiv marine](https://skyciv.com/industries/marine-engineering/))

- **ANSYS 2026 R1 (January 2026): Direct Morph Workflow (GPU mesh morphing), GPU resource prediction, Sherlock electronics reliability integration, MSUP workflow efficiency gains. Ocean Loading training course available; no new offshore/subsea design modules announced. ANSYS remains FEA platform of choice for coupled structural analysis (AQWA hydrodynamics + Mechanical fatigue/buckling); API unchanged from 2025. Market signal: ANSYS prioritizes meshing/morphing automation + electronics-structural coupling (industry trend toward digital twins), not subsea-specific modules.** ([ANSYS 2026 R1 webinar](https://www.ansys.com/webinars/ansys-2026-r1-structures), [ANSYS Mechanical](https://www.ansys.com/products/structures/ansys-mechanical))

- **Flexcom (Wood Group) 2026 H1: No public announcements identified. Recent development focused on floating wind turbine coupling (OpenFAST integration) + pipe-in-pipe riser modeling. Specialized niche tool; no competing alternatives in flexible riser FEA space. Strategic trajectory suggests offshore wind (floating) focus rather than oil & gas subsea expansion. Silent on 2026 roadmap.** ([Flexcom overview](https://www.woodplc.com/solutions/expertise/flexcom), [Flexcom pipe-in-pipe theory](https://flexcom.fea.solutions/theory_pipe.html))

- **DNV 2026 Edition (effective 1 July 2026) + 2026 H2 roadmap: Restructured floating offshore standards (OS-C103/105/106), new requirements for fish farm composites/polymers (DNV-RU-OU-0503), revised electrical systems (OS-D201), floating offshore wind FOWTF in-service requirements rewritten (DNV-RU-OU-0512). Next edition hearing opens March 2026, 77 documents scheduled for July 2026 publication. Standards publication cadence: **6-month cycle** (January edition effective, July edition hearing, next January effective cycle repeats). Implication: calculation modules must track standards changes continuously; aceengineer's standards traceability positioning gains competitive advantage if DNV 2026/2026 H2 curves are current by ship date.** ([DNV 2026 hearing](https://www.dnv.com/news/2026/dnv-rules-2026-hearing-period-now-open/), [DNV July 2025 edition](https://www.dnv.com/news/2025/now-available-july-2025-edition-of-dnv-class-rules-and-documents-for-ship-and-offshore/), [DNV offshore technical guidance](https://www.dnv.com/maritime/Offshore/technical-guidance-otg/))

- **No new entrants in offshore/subsea engineering software identified (2026-04-09 through 2026-04-23). Market consolidation thesis holds: 7.4% CAGR growth ($750M → $818M 2025→2026) concentrated in established platforms (DNV Sesam, Bentley SACS, Orcina OrcaFlex, NREL OpenFAST, Wood Group Flexcom). Startups focus on general software development outsourcing, not domain-specific engineering tools. Barrier to entry: deep subsea engineering expertise (VIV, cathodic protection, DNV compliance, fatigue assessment) + 10+ year code maturity + client validation. Opportunity window for independent developers (like aceengineer) remains open as competitors consolidate toward platform/cloud infrastructure rather than specialized domain modules.** (See all sources above)
