# OCIMF Tandem Mooring & Offloading Guidelines for Conventional Tankers at F(P)SO Facilities

OCIMF first-edition (2009) guidance for tandem mooring and offloading between conventional shuttle tankers and Floating (Production) Storage and Offloading installations. Curated source summary derived from the bounded OCR preview produced by [#2521](https://github.com/vamseeachanta/workspace-hub/issues/2521); the full standard is not ingested into git or this wiki.

## Scope (curated from source summary)

The visible table of contents in the OCR preview surfaces these high-level topical areas:

- Document scope and purpose
- Applicable codes and standards
- F(P)SO offloading philosophy
- Subsea mooring arrangements supporting tandem operation
- Basis of design for tandem mooring configurations
- Tandem mooring configuration overview

The standard targets shuttle-tanker offloading at floating production / storage facilities (F(P)SO), where the export tanker is held in tandem astern of the host facility during cargo transfer rather than connected to a multi-buoy or jetty arrangement.

## Equipment (curated from source summary)

The OCR preview enumerates the following equipment elements covered by the guideline:

- Single-hawser systems and dual-hawser systems
- Chafe chain (line-end abrasion mitigation in the fairlead)
- Fairlead (mooring-line bend management at the host facility)
- Weak link (load-limiting element preventing over-tension propagation upstream)
- Hawser handling (deployment and recovery procedures)
- Hawser storage (long-term storage, including retirement criteria)
- Hawser retirement criteria

These equipment topics are referenced verbatim from the OCR'd table of contents; engineering specifics, dimensions, MBL/SWL ratios, and inspection cadences must be consulted directly from the source PDF on disk.
