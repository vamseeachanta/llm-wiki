# Hybrid Riser Tower / SLOR / Bundled Tower

## Scope

This page covers the hybrid riser-tower architecture: a near-vertical rigid bundle anchored at the seabed, terminated by a near-surface buoyancy can, and connected to the host vessel by short flexible jumpers. Variants include the Single-Line Offset Riser (SLOR), grouped-SLOR, and integrated production bundle (IPB). It is NOT a single-pipe SCR page — see [[riser-steel-catenary-design]]. It is NOT a flexible-only page — see [[riser-flexible-design]].

## Key Concepts

- **Single-Line Offset Riser (SLOR)** — single vertical riser line, individual top buoyancy can, individual flexible jumper to vessel.
- **Grouped SLOR** — multiple SLORs sharing a common buoyancy structure or bundled within a guide-frame.
- **Integrated production bundle (IPB)** — multiple production lines, gas-lift lines, control umbilicals, and heating lines bundled inside a single carrier pipe with a shared buoyancy can.
- **Top buoyancy can** — provides axial tension; sized to the bundle wet weight plus a tension margin.
- **Foundation** — suction-pile or gravity foundation anchoring the bundle base; resists net uplift from buoyancy minus bundle weight.
- **Flexible jumper** — short catenary jumper from the buoyancy can to the vessel turret or porch; isolates vessel motion from the rigid bundle.
- **Tower thermal management** — bundle insulation, heated annulus, or pipe-in-pipe vacuum maintains flow assurance temperature.
- **Installation sequencing** — bundle assembly often onshore or near-shore; tow-out plus upend to vertical at site.
