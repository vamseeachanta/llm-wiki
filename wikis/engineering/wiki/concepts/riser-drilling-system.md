# Drilling Riser System

## Scope

This page covers the marine drilling riser system: top-side telescopic joint, riser-joint stack, lower marine riser package (LMRP) and blowout preventer (BOP) interface, air-gap, weak-point disconnect, and tensioner-recoil response. It is NOT a production-riser page — see [[riser-top-tensioned-design]] and [[riser-steel-catenary-design]]. It is NOT a VIV-fatigue mechanics page — see [[viv-riser-fatigue]].

## Key Concepts

- **Telescopic / slip joint** — vertical-stroke compensator between the riser and the diverter housing, accommodating vessel heave.
- **Riser joint stack** — modular joints (typically 75 ft) with auxiliary lines (choke, kill, booster, hydraulic supply) clamped to the main pipe.
- **Buoyancy modules** — syntactic-foam jackets reducing wet weight and the required top tension.
- **LMRP / BOP** — lower marine riser package latches onto the BOP stack on the wellhead; emergency disconnect separates LMRP from the BOP.
- **Air-gap** — vertical clearance between the still-water level and the underside of the diverter housing; sized for storm wave crest plus a safety margin.
- **Weak-point design** — designated structural weak point (often the flex-joint at the LMRP) that fails predictably in a drive-off or drift-off scenario.
- **Disconnect / recoil** — after emergency disconnect, the tensioned riser rebounds upward; recoil control limits maximum acceleration and prevents derrick contact.
- **Operability envelope** — vessel-offset and weather window inside which connected operations remain within stress and angle limits.
