# Flexible Riser — Architecture and Design

## Scope

This page covers unbonded flexible riser architecture: composite layer functions, end-fittings, ancillaries (bend stiffener, bend restrictor, mid-water arch, buoyancy modules), and annulus integrity. It is NOT a configuration-topology page — see [[riser-configurations]] for lazy-wave / steep-wave / pliant-wave geometry. It is NOT a metallic-riser page — see [[riser-steel-catenary-design]] and [[riser-top-tensioned-design]].

## Key Concepts

- **Unbonded layer stack** — typical layers are inner carcass, internal pressure sheath (polymer), pressure-armour (Z-shape interlocked), tensile armours (helically wound, two layers cross-laid), anti-wear and outer sheath.
- **Carcass** — interlocked stainless strip preventing bore collapse under external pressure during depressurisation.
- **Pressure-armour** — interlocked metallic profile resisting hoop stress from internal pressure.
- **Tensile armours** — counter-helical layers carrying axial tension; controlled torsional balance is a design constraint.
- **Polymer sheaths** — fluid-barrier and outer-protection layers; ageing and gas permeation drive service-life limits.
- **End-fitting** — anchors all metallic layers and provides the pressure seal; the most stress-intense and costly subassembly.
- **Bend stiffener** — polyurethane cone limiting bending curvature at the connector; fatigue-critical ancillary.
- **Bend restrictor** — articulated locking sleeves limiting bend at seabed and PLEM transitions.
- **Mid-water arch** — fixed support providing the buoyancy section in lazy-S and lazy-wave shapes.
- **Annulus integrity** — vent system and gas-monitoring detect outer-sheath breach; flooded annulus accelerates tensile-armour fatigue and corrosion.
- **Failure modes** — outer-sheath rupture, tensile-armour birdcaging, pressure-armour fatigue, polymer ageing, end-fitting pull-out.
