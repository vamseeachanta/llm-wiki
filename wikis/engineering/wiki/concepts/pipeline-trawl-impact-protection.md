# Pipeline Trawl Impact Protection

## Scope

Pipeline protection design against fishing-trawl-gear interaction (impact, hooking, pull-over) and dropped-object events. This page covers protection methods (rock-dump, mattress, trenching, burial) and impact-energy bands. It does NOT cover free-span VIV fatigue (see [[free-span-viv-fatigue]]) or general corroded fitness-for-service (see [[pipeline-integrity-assessment]]).

## Key Concepts

- **Trawl-board impact** — direct first-contact event from heavy steel trawl boards crossing or striking a pipeline; impact-energy bands feed dent-and-puncture limit-state checks.
- **Pull-over** — sustained drag of trawl gear across a pipeline; pull-over force and duration drive lateral displacement and local stress demand.
- **Hooking** — gear becomes snagged on pipeline appurtenances (PLETs, anodes, tees); rare but high-consequence event.
- **Dropped-object protection** — supply-vessel-dropped containers, pipe joints, or hoist objects; protection criteria from impact-energy bands.
- **Rock-dump** — graded rock berm sized for both protection and on-bottom-stability augmentation; common at PLET landings and crossings.
- **Concrete mattress** — articulated concrete blocks placed by ROV-deployable frames; quick local protection.
- **Trenching/burial** — most reliable protection, integrated into the pipelay schedule per [[pipeline-installation-methods]].
- **Risk-based selection** — protection method chosen against fishing-gear density and dropped-object exposure inherited from [[pipeline-route-selection]].
