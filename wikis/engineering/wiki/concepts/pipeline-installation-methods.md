# Pipeline Installation Methods

## Scope

Surface and subsea pipeline installation techniques for offshore field development. This page covers S-lay, J-lay, reel-lay, and tow-out (surface, mid-depth, off-bottom, bottom). It does NOT cover commissioning/hot-tap or onshore pipeline construction in detail.

## Key Concepts

- **S-lay** — pipeline assembled horizontally on a lay vessel and lowered through a stinger into an S-curve; mature for shallow-to-moderate water depths and standard for large-diameter trunklines.
- **J-lay** — pipeline assembled vertically in a tower and lowered as a J-curve; preferred for deep water where S-lay stinger geometry becomes infeasible.
- **Reel-lay** — pipeline pre-fabricated onshore, spooled onto a reel, and unwound at sea; high lay rate; diameter limited by reel curvature plastic-strain budget.
- **Tow-out methods** — surface tow, mid-depth tow, off-bottom tow, and bottom tow; pre-fabricated sections (bundles, towheads) towed by tugs to site for tie-in.
- **PLET / PLEM landing** — pipeline-end-termination structures landed at the start, end, or mid-line; constrains [[pipeline-end-expansion-spool-design]].
- **Burial after lay** — jet-trenching, ploughing, or rock-dump for protection or [[pipeline-upheaval-buckling]] cover; integrated into the lay-spread schedule.
