# Riser Configurations — Topology Taxonomy

## Scope

This page is the topology hub for offshore production-, drilling-, and export-riser configurations. It enumerates configuration archetypes and lists the differentiating geometric and motion features. It is NOT a design-state or fatigue page: SCR design lives on [[steel-catenary-riser-design]], TTR on [[top-tensioned-riser-design]], flexible architecture on [[flexible-riser-design]], hybrid towers on [[hybrid-riser-tower]], drilling-system architecture on [[drilling-riser-system]], and VIV-fatigue mechanics on [[viv-riser-fatigue]].

## Key Concepts

- **Free-hanging catenary** — single span from vessel hang-off to seabed touchdown, gravity-tensioned, used for SCR (steel) and free-hanging flexible.
- **Lazy-wave** — distributed buoyancy modules along the mid-section create a wave shape, decoupling vessel motion from touchdown-point response.
- **Steep-wave** — buoyancy section near the seabed bend, anchor at the seabed, riser climbs steeply to the vessel.
- **Lazy-S** — single mid-water arch (buoy or fixed) supports the riser at one mid-depth point.
- **Steep-S** — riser anchored at the seabed bend, climbs to a single arch, then to the vessel.
- **Pliant-wave** — variant of lazy-wave with seabed anchor on the riser side of touchdown.
- **Tethered (Chinese lantern, tethered-S)** — multiple symmetric tethers fix the riser geometry against vessel offset.
- **Hybrid riser tower** — rigid vertical bundle (Single-Line Offset Riser, SLOR, or grouped tower) terminated by a near-surface buoy with flexible jumpers to the vessel — see [[hybrid-riser-tower]].
- **Top-tensioned riser** — vertical riser with active tensioner at the vessel keeping the line under axial tension — see [[top-tensioned-riser-design]].
