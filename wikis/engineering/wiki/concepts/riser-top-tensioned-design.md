# Top-Tensioned Riser (TTR) — Design State

## Scope

This page covers the design state of a Top-Tensioned Riser (TTR) used on tension-leg platforms (TLPs), spars, and dry-tree semi-submersibles. Topics: top-tension envelope, tensioner stroke, taper joint, stress joint, keel joint, and single- vs dual-casing layout. It is NOT a VIV page — see [[viv-riser-fatigue]]. It is NOT a drilling-riser page — see [[drilling-riser-system]].

## Key Concepts

- **Top-tension envelope** — minimum tension to avoid effective compression and Euler buckling; maximum tension bounded by tensioner capacity and material limits.
- **Tensioner stroke** — vertical travel allowance accommodating vessel heave, tide, payload change, and thermal length change.
- **Taper joint** — gradual wall-thickness step that reduces stress concentration at the keel transition.
- **Stress joint** — tapered titanium or steel joint at the seabed connector providing rotational compliance.
- **Keel joint** — wear-resistant section spanning the spar keel guide where contact-bending alternates with axial pull.
- **Single-casing TTR** — production tubing inside a single outer riser pipe; shared with completion access.
- **Dual-casing TTR** — production casing inside an outer drilling-riser annulus; allows simultaneous production and well intervention.
- **Air can** — passive top-tension provider on spar TTRs (buoyancy can replacing active tensioner).
