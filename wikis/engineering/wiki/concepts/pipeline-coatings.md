# Pipeline Coatings

## Scope

Subsea pipeline external coating systems: anti-corrosion barrier, thermal insulation, concrete weight coating, and field-joint coating. This page names coating types and selection drivers; it does NOT enumerate corroded fitness-for-service prose (see [[pipeline-integrity-assessment]]) or vendor-specific application thresholds.

## Key Concepts

- **FBE (fusion-bonded epoxy)** — single-layer thermosetting epoxy applied as powder; dominant baseline anti-corrosion barrier.
- **3LPE / 3LPP** — three-layer polyethylene or polypropylene over an FBE primer with adhesive interlayer; mechanical-protection plus barrier; PP variant tolerates higher service temperature than PE.
- **Wet-insulation systems** — polypropylene-syntactic foams or solid PP layers applied directly to the line; deepwater HT/HP service.
- **Pipe-in-pipe (PIP)** — inner production pipe + annulus insulation + outer carrier pipe; reserved for very-high-temperature or arctic flow-assurance duty.
- **Concrete weight coating (CWC)** — densified concrete shell providing submerged-weight for [[pipeline-on-bottom-stability]] in shallow water; thickness driven by hydrodynamics, not anti-corrosion need.
- **Field-joint coating (FJC)** — coating applied at lay-vessel weld station to bridge mill-coated joints; weak-link in the long-term integrity envelope.
- **Selection drivers** — service temperature, water depth, soil aggressiveness, installation method per [[pipeline-installation-methods]] (reel-lay strain capacity vs S-lay/J-lay handling).
