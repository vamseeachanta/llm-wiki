# Riser Installation Methods

## Scope

This page covers the offshore-installation methods used to lay and connect risers (rigid and flexible) at the host vessel. Topics: reel-lay, J-lay, S-lay-with-stinger, top-down deployment, abandonment-and-recovery (A&R), and pull-in. It is NOT a configuration page — see [[riser-configurations]]. It is NOT a global-analysis page — see [[riser-global-analysis-load-cases]].

## Key Concepts

- **Reel-lay** — riser pre-spooled on a vertical or horizontal reel onshore; straightened and laid via vertical-tower or aft-ramp deployment; high lay rate, suitable for rigid pipelines and small-OD risers.
- **J-lay** — vertical-tower deployment, single-joint or quad-joint welds, suitable for deepwater rigid risers and SCRs.
- **S-lay** — horizontal firing line with overboard stinger forming the over-bend, sag-bend at touchdown; common for shallow-to-mid water pipelines and risers.
- **Top-down deployment** — flexible riser deployed from the vessel reel, end-fitting first, with bend-stiffener pre-installed; applies to flexible production risers.
- **Pull-in** — riser end pulled through the host vessel turret, porch, or pull-tube using winch and messenger wire.
- **Abandonment and recovery (A&R)** — controlled lay-down of the riser end on the seabed for weather-window or bad-weather waiting, with retrieval winch on standby.
- **Lay tension control** — lay tension and departure angle govern the touchdown stress and the over-bend stress; tension monitoring is real-time.
- **Bend-stiffener pre-installation** — flexible riser bend-stiffener installed before pull-in to protect the connector during deployment.
- **Installation analysis cases** — normal-lay tension, lay-tension upset, A&R hang-off, weather hold-off, and pre-lay verification.
