# Pipeline On-Bottom Stability

## Scope

Lateral and vertical stability of as-laid pipelines under wave-induced and current-induced hydrodynamic loading. This page covers absolute and generalised stability methods plus concrete-weight-coating sizing. It does NOT re-cover free-span dynamic response (see [[free-span-viv-fatigue]]) or corroded fitness-for-service (see [[pipeline-integrity-assessment]]).

## Key Concepts

- **Absolute stability** — pipeline is sized so peak hydrodynamic load never exceeds soil resistance for a chosen return-period storm; conservative and quick to apply.
- **Generalised lateral stability** — calibrated displacement-allowable method that permits limited cyclic embedment-displacement cycles within a tolerable berm-formation envelope.
- **Concrete weight coating** — the dominant submerged-weight lever for shallow-water pipelines; thickness driven by hydrodynamic-coefficient × significant-wave-height combinations rather than steel grade.
- **Soil resistance** — friction model is depth-of-embedment-dependent; tied to [[pipeline-soil-interaction]] axial/lateral models.
- **Span formation as failure mode** — too-rough seabed produces unacceptably long spans, kicking back into the [[free-span-viv-fatigue]] design loop.
- **Construction-as-laid vs operating-condition checks** — empty-pipe stability during installation is the often-controlling load case.
