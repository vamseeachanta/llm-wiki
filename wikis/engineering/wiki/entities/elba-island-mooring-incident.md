# Elba Island LNG Mooring Incident (2006)

While the LNG tanker Golar Freeze was discharging at Southern LNG terminal on Elba Island in the Savannah River, Georgia, a river pilot passed the terminal at approximately 14 knots. The passing vessel's wake knocked the Golar Freeze loose, breaking mooring lines and the fuel hose, creating fire/explosion risk. The tanker pulled away from the pier.

## Consequences

- Dock shut down for ~36 hours for USCG and FERC investigation
- **$35 million remediation**: Southern LNG carved out a dedicated slip for two LNG carriers to remove them from the main river channel
- **USCG mandate**: Passing ships must be escorted at slow speed by tugs whenever an LNG tanker is moored

## Key Lesson

Passing vessel wake effects at even moderate speeds (14 knots) can generate sufficient hydrodynamic forces to part mooring lines at river berths. Terminal location relative to navigation channels is a critical design factor.
