## Summary

BSI (British Standards Institution) is the UK national standards body and a primary publisher of British Standards (BS), BS EN (European-aligned), and BS ISO (internationally-aligned) technical specifications used across offshore O&G. The 80 deduplicated catalog entries are dominated by the BS 13xxx / 14xxx / 15xxx / 16xxx / 17xxx ranges — most of which are British re-publications of the ISO 13xxx oil-and-gas equipment series (drilling equipment, downhole equipment, subsea umbilicals, flexible pipe, wellhead/Christmas-tree equipment, life-cycle costing) — together with the durable British classics BS 7608 (fatigue assessment of steel structures), BS 7910 (assessment of flaws in metallic structures), BS 7448 (fracture-mechanics toughness tests), BS 8010 (pipeline code), BS 5400 (steel/concrete/composite bridges), BS 4360 (structural steel sections), and BS 9001/9002 (quality systems). The 1980–2014 publication-date span is heavily clustered in 2000–2004 and runs from the 1980 BS 5400 Pt 10 fatigue rules through the 2014 BS 7608 update.

## Verification

- Document count cross-checked against `_catalog.json` `statistics.by_organization.BSI = 80`.
- Bucket purity caveat: ~20 of 80 entries are NOT BSI publications. The folder includes 4 ABS Guide PDFs (`ABS GUI 00`, `ABS GUI 002`, `ABS GUI 115` 2003 + 2010), 11 FAOS-series joint-industry publications (`FAOS_Commentary.pdf`, `Pub101_FPSO_DLA.pdf`, `Pub104_OffshoreSFA_Guide.pdf`, `Pub115_FAOS.pdf`, `Pub123_Riser_GuideMar08.pdf`, etc.), 7 generic `Corrigenda.pdf` / `RCN1.pdf` files (the catalog reports duplicate filenames with distinct `content_hash`), and 5 loose `Guide*.pdf` / `*Colors*.pdf` documents. The mis-bucketed FAOS/ABS items should reroute on the next consolidation pass.
- DRM status: see `/mnt/ace/O&G-Standards/drm_protected_files.txt` for any BSI files flagged as DRM-protected.
- OCR availability: `_inventory.db` provides per-page OCR text for all PDFs (consume via SQLite, do not copy into wiki). One `.doc` file (`bs4360.doc`, 243 KB) is non-PDF and may not be OCR-indexed.

## Recommended follow-ups

1. **Per-code standards/ pages**: codes with multi-edition coverage and durable engineering-citation value warrant a `wiki/standards/bsi-<code-id>.md` page consolidating revision history. Highest-value: BS 7608 (1993/1993+1995/2014), BS 7910 (1999/2005/2013), BS 7448 Pt 1+4 (1991/1997), BS 10204 (1991/2004), BS 10423 (2001/2004), BS 13628 Pt 2/3/5/8 subsea series (2001–2002), BS 15663 Pt 1/2/3 life-cycle costing (2000–2001).
2. **Topical concept pages**: cross-link this source into existing concepts/ for fatigue assessment (BS 7608), flaw acceptance / engineering critical assessment (BS 7910), fracture-mechanics testing (BS 7448), subsea flexible pipe (BS 13628 Pt 2), umbilicals (BS 13628 Pt 5), wellhead/Christmas-tree equipment (BS 10423), and HSE risk assessment (BSI 17776 — the British re-publication of ISO 17776).
3. **Catalog hygiene**: the 4 ABS Guide PDFs should move to a future `/mnt/ace/O&G-Standards/ABS/` slice (ABS already has an ABS folder at the top level — verify and rehome). The FAOS-series files are ABS / Joint Industry publications that belong with that slice as well. The 7 generic `Corrigenda.pdf` filenames need reverse-lookup against `content_hash` to identify which standard each amends.
4. **BS-vs-ISO equivalence index**: the BS 13xxx / 14xxx / 15xxx / 16xxx / 17xxx range is largely the British re-publication of the ISO 13xxx oil-and-gas equipment series. A comparison table cross-linking each BS document to its ISO equivalent would close the redundancy gap and clarify which is the citation-of-record for given audiences.
