# ISO 19901-7 — Stationkeeping systems for floating offshore structures

## Scope

Part 7 of the ISO 19901 series ("Specific requirements for offshore structures") covering stationkeeping systems. Specifies design requirements for stationkeeping on:

- Mobile offshore units (MODUs) — semisubmersible drilling rigs, drillships;
- Floating production systems (FPSOs, FSOs, FPSs, semisubmersible production units);
- Tension-leg platforms operating in the passive-mooring portion of their installation life;
- Spars in their normal mooring configuration.

Coverage spans passive catenary mooring, taut-leg mooring, thruster-assisted mooring, and dynamic positioning. Strength and fatigue limit-state acceptance is provided for line components (chain, wire rope, synthetic fiber rope), anchors (drag-embedment, suction, pile), connectors, and fairlead/winch interfaces. Consumes metocean inputs from ISO 19901-1 and geotechnical inputs from ISO 19901-4.

## Key sections

- **Clause 1-3 — Scope, normative references, terms** — applicability boundary and interface to ISO 19900 series.
- **Clause 4 — Symbols and abbreviated terms.**
- **Clause 5 — General requirements** — design philosophy, design situations, design service life.
- **Clause 6 — Design conditions** — metocean conditions imported from ISO 19901-1; intact, damaged, transient, and survival design conditions.
- **Clause 7 — Mooring system analysis methods** — quasi-static, frequency-domain, time-domain selection; low-frequency response treatment.
- **Clause 8 — Strength acceptance (ULS)** — partial safety factors keyed to consequence-class L1/L2/L3 and equivalent characteristic-tension methodology.
- **Clause 9 — Fatigue acceptance (FLS)** — T-N curve methodology for chain and wire, fatigue safety factors.
- **Clause 10 — Component requirements** — chain, wire rope, fiber rope, connectors; manufacturing and certification.
- **Clause 11 — Anchors** — drag, suction, pile anchor design and proof-load.
- **Clause 12 — Thruster-assisted mooring and DP** — coupling between passive mooring and active station-keeping.

**Cross-wiki bridge (maritime-law):**

- [SOLAS 1974](../../../maritime-law/wiki/standards/solas-1974.md) — **bidirectional bridge**: ISO 19901-7 governs station-keeping (passive catenary mooring, taut-leg mooring, thruster-assisted mooring, and dynamic positioning) for floating offshore structures including MODUs, FPSOs, FLNG, and FSRUs. SOLAS Chapter II-1 governs ship subdivision, damage stability, and watertight integrity for SOLAS-registered vessels. The two standards converge on **mobile offshore units operating as ships under flag-state jurisdiction** — particularly FLNG and FSRU classification, where the floating asset is simultaneously a moored offshore structure (ISO 19901-7 station-keeping basis) and a SOLAS-registered ship (Chapter II-1 stability and Chapter VII Part C IGC Code cargo containment). **IACS UR M53** (mobile offshore units) and the **MODU Code** (IMO Code for the Construction and Equipment of Mobile Offshore Drilling Units, Resolution A.1023(26)) harmonize the two regimes; class-society rules for column-stabilized units, ship-type FPSOs, and disconnectable-turret FPSOs invoke ISO 19901-7 for mooring-component sizing while preserving SOLAS Ch. II-1 stability margins through the operating envelope. Damage-stability disconnect-and-departure scenarios for cyclone-prone FPSOs (NW Australia, Gulf of Mexico hurricane season) sit at the intersection — the disconnect mechanism is sized to ISO 19901-7 transient design conditions while the post-disconnect transit complies with SOLAS Ch. II-1 intact + damaged stability.
