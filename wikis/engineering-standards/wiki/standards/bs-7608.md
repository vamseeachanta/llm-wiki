## Scope

BS 7608 is BSI's flagship guide for **S-N-based fatigue design and assessment of steel products**, including welded and non-welded details, and is the UK's primary fatigue-design code outside pressure-equipment and offshore-specific codes. Originally written to support the offshore oil-and-gas and structural-steel industries during the 1980s/1990s North Sea expansion, the standard has since been **widely adopted for non-pressure components** in cranes, bridges, lifting equipment, transmission towers, wind-turbine support structures, and general fabricated steelwork.

The guide covers nominal-stress S-N curves for parent metal and welded joints, hot-spot stress methodology for welded details where geometric stress concentration cannot be captured by classification, weld-class catalogues (Class B / C / D / E / F / F2 / G / W in the UK weld-class hierarchy), Palmgren-Miner cumulative-damage summation under variable-amplitude spectra, sea-water and cathodic-protection environmental modifiers, and design-S-N-curve safety-factor calibration including reliability-based partial safety factors.

## Comparison with DNV-RP-C203 and parallel codes

BS 7608 is the UK national counterpart to the offshore-fatigue codes [[dnv-rp-c203]] (DNV) and structural-fatigue annexes in Eurocode 3 EN 1993-1-9. The three families are technically aligned but differ in defaults, S-N curve labelling, and environmental-class definitions:

| Dimension | BS 7608 (UK) | DNV-RP-C203 (offshore) | Eurocode EN 1993-1-9 (EU) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary surface | Steel products generally — bridges, cranes, lifts, wind | Offshore welded steel structures | Civil/structural steelwork |
| S-N classification | UK Class B-W ladder | DNV B1, B2, C, C1, C2, D, E, F, F1, F3, G, W1-W3, T | EC Class 36-160 (detail-category labels) |
| Environment | Air / seawater / seawater+CP / freely-corroding | Air / seawater / seawater+CP / freely-corroding | Air (corrosion treated separately) |
| Hot-spot stress | Annex (recommended for complex welds) | Annex (mandatory for tubular joints) | Annex (informative) |
| Variable-amplitude | Palmgren-Miner | Palmgren-Miner + spectrum-block reduction | Palmgren-Miner |
| Welded T-section / tubular | Limited (topside applications) | Comprehensive (offshore tubular joints) | Limited |

BS 7608 and DNV-RP-C203 are typically used in tandem on offshore/onshore-mixed projects, with BS 7608 governing topside / onshore steel and DNV-RP-C203 governing offshore submerged structure. They are **not interchangeable on a per-detail basis** — class labels and environmental modifiers are internally calibrated within each code, and an Eurocode Class 90 is not numerically identical to a BS 7608 Class D despite occupying similar curve regions.

## Edition history

- **BS 7608:1993** — first publication consolidating earlier BSI fatigue guidance into a single guide.
- **BS 7608:2014** — major revision, updated weld classifications, added environmental-class refinements, expanded variable-amplitude guidance.
- **Amendment 1 (2015)** — A1 amendment to BS 7608:2014; corrections and clarifications.

The BS 7608:2014+A1:2015 edition is the current published edition at time of writing. Consult BSI Knowledge for any further amendments before normative citation.

## Key clauses (informative pointer)

The 2014 edition's clause structure is the working surface for most calc modules:

- **Clauses 1-3** — Scope, normative references, terms and definitions.
- **Clauses 4-6** — Material and weld-quality requirements; design data; assessment procedures.
- **Clauses 7-8** — S-N curve framework; weld-class assignments; environmental modifiers.
- **Clauses 9-10** — Variable-amplitude loading; Palmgren-Miner cumulative damage; non-proportional loading.
- **Annexes** — Hot-spot stress methodology; reliability-based design factors; weld-class atlas; specimen test data referenced for class derivation; corrosion-fatigue treatment.

## Practitioner application

- Crane, bridge, and lifting-equipment designers requiring UK-jurisdiction fatigue qualification.
- Wind-turbine support-structure (jacket, monopile, tripod) topside fatigue analysts pairing BS 7608 (topside) with DNV-RP-C203 (submerged) on the same project.
- Welding engineers feeding [[bs-7910]] Annex M fatigue-crack-growth assessments — BS 7608 supplies the S-N input that anchors the FCG initiation point.
- Class societies — LR, ABS, DNV, BV — accepting BS 7608 fatigue assessments for ship-mounted equipment, port cranes, and offshore-loading-arm topsides.
