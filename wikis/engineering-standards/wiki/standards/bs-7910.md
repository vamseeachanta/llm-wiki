## Scope

BS 7910 is BSI's flagship guide for **engineering critical assessment (ECA)** and **fitness-for-service (FFS)** evaluation of crack-like and non-crack-like flaws in metallic structures and weldments. It supplies the methods to determine whether a known or postulated flaw is acceptable for continued service under static, cyclic, and elevated-temperature loading, covering Failure Assessment Diagram (FAD) methodology at three levels of analytical conservatism (Level 1 / 2 / 3), planar and non-planar flaw assessment in plates, pipes, and pressure vessels, fatigue crack growth via Paris-law integration of stress-intensity-factor ranges, leak-before-break (LBB) demonstration, residual-stress treatment for as-welded and post-weld-heat-treated weldments, and creep / creep-fatigue crack growth for high-temperature plant.

## Revision history

- **PD 6493:1991** — predecessor BSI Published Document; superseded by BS 7910:1999.
- **BS 7910:1999 (with Amendment 1)** — first edition; titled "Guide on methods for assessing the acceptability of flaws in fusion welded structures".
- **BS 7910:2005 (with Amendment 1)** — title broadens to "metallic structures" (drops the "fusion welded" restriction).
- **BS 7910:2013** — major revision; revised FAD options, updated residual-stress profiles, expanded creep clause.
- **BS 7910:2013+A1:2015** — Amendment 1 to 2013 edition.
- **BS 7910:2019+A1:2023** — current published edition; consult BSI Knowledge for any subsequent amendments.

## Key sections

The 2019 edition's normative annex set is the working surface for most calc modules:

- **Annex A** — Failure Assessment Diagram (FAD); Level 1 / 2A / 2B / 3 curves; ordinate K<sub>r</sub>, abscissa L<sub>r</sub>, cut-off L<sub>r</sub><sup>max</sup>.
- **Annex K** — Tabulated as-welded and post-weld-heat-treated residual-stress distributions for plate, T-butt, nozzle, pipe seam and girth, and repair welds.
- **Annex M** — Fatigue crack growth: Paris-law constants for ferritic/austenitic steels and aluminium alloys; threshold ΔK<sub>th</sub>; environmental modifiers; integration procedure.
- **Annex N** — Probabilistic FAD / reliability framework; Monte Carlo and FORM/SORM treatment; partial-safety-factor calibration.
- **Annex T** — Leak-before-break: through-wall-flaw stability and leakage-rate prediction.
- **Annex J** — Creep crack initiation and growth; C\* parameter framework.

> Annex letters are normative within BS 7910:2019; verify against the publisher edition for normative work.

## Practitioner application

- Offshore O&G operators and EPCs running ECA on pipeline girth welds, riser welds, and floating-production-unit hull welds, typically pairing BS 7910 Level 2 with [api-1104](api-1104.md) Annex A for North-American pipeline construction welds.
- Pressure-equipment FFS analysts disposing of in-service crack-like flaws under [api-579-1-asme-ffs-1](api-579-1-asme-ffs-1.md) Part 9 alongside BS 7910 Level 2/3, switching codes by jurisdiction and operator preference.
- Fatigue-driven inspection-planning specialists feeding BS 7910 Annex M FCG output into [dnv-rp-c210](dnv-rp-c210.md) probabilistic-inspection scheduling for offshore steel structures.
- Nuclear safety-case engineers running parallel BS 7910 / R6 assessments on pressure-retaining components.

## Industry adoption

BS 7910 is the dominant ECA/FFS code in EU/UK offshore O&G fabrications and is widely adopted in international offshore projects alongside its US counterpart [api-579-1-asme-ffs-1](api-579-1-asme-ffs-1.md). The two codes are broadly aligned in technical philosophy (FAD-based two-criteria fracture assessment, residual-stress superposition, fatigue-crack-growth integration) but diverge in scope, structure, and method options — practitioners must not mix-and-match annexes across codes since residual-stress profiles, reference-stress solutions, and FAD curves are internally calibrated within each code. Class societies (DNV, ABS, BV, LR) accept BS 7910 ECA/FFS deliverables on offshore-structure scope.
