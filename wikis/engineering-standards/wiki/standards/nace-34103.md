# NACE 34103 (AMPP-published) — Refinery Crude Unit Practical Damage Mechanisms

## Scope

Field-engineer-focused field guide to the damage mechanisms specific to crude distillation units — the atmospheric tower, the vacuum tower, and their ancillary systems (overhead condenser, side-draw circuits, desalter, preheat train, transfer lines). The publication is structured around the operational reality of running a crude unit rather than as an encyclopedic catalogue of mechanisms. The mechanisms emphasized are the ones that govern crude-unit availability and inspection planning in practice:

- **Naphthenic-acid corrosion (NAC)** — TAN-driven organic-acid attack at high-velocity points in vacuum-tower side draws, vacuum transfer-line elbows, and atmospheric-column flash-zone internals (see [sulfidation-and-naphthenic-acid](../concepts/sulfidation-and-naphthenic-acid.md)).
- **High-temperature sulfidation** — H2S and organic-sulfur attack on hot-circuit piping and column internals above ~230 °C; H2-free McConomy regime for crude unit (no hydrogen partial pressure to invoke modified Couper–Gorman).
- **HCl + chloride-induced corrosion in the overhead condenser** — hydrolysis of MgCl2 / CaCl2 salts surviving the desalter, dew-point HCl attack at the water condensation front, under-deposit ammonium-chloride corrosion when the water wash is mistimed or undersized.
- **Wet-CO2 corrosion** — secondary contributor in the overhead system when CO2 is present in the crude or stripping steam.
- **Microbiologically influenced corrosion (MIC)** in cooling-water service to the overhead condenser shell side and the crude-tower pump-around exchangers.
- **Ammonium-bisulfide (NH4HS) corrosion** — overhead-system mechanism when both H2S and NH3 partition into the aqueous phase; aggravated at high NH4HS concentration and high velocity (Kp factor).

The publication is the **operations-focused companion** to API RP 571 — RP 571 is the umbrella mechanism encyclopedia for the entire refining industry; 34103 narrows to the crude unit and adds the field-monitoring and case-study material that lets a refining engineer act on the umbrella catalogue.

## Key sections

The publication is organized around the **crude-unit equipment map** rather than around mechanism families — this is the structural choice that distinguishes it from RP 571's mechanism-first organization. Typical section structure (general to NACE Practical Damage Mechanism guides; specific clause numbering must be verified against the publisher edition):

- **Crude-unit operating envelopes by stream** — atmospheric tower top vs. middle vs. bottoms vs. overhead vs. side draws (kerosene, diesel, AGO); vacuum tower flash zone vs. wash zone vs. side draws (LVGO, HVGO) vs. bottoms; preheat train hot-side vs. cold-side; desalter inlet/outlet.
- **Per-stream damage-mechanism + alloy + monitoring + mitigation tables** — each stream block lists the credible mechanisms (typically 1–4 per stream), the typical alloy ladder for that stream (carbon steel through 5Cr / 9Cr / 12Cr / 316L / higher Mo CRA), the inspection technique and CML density, and the operational mitigations available (water-wash design, neutralizer/inhibitor injection, velocity limits, feedstock-blending constraints).
- **Real-world case-study format** — the publication's distinguishing feature versus RP 571 is the case-study presentation: each case follows a **mechanism → equipment → alloy choice → monitoring → outcome** narrative drawn from refinery field experience, with photographs of the failure morphology and the inspection technique that detected (or missed) it. Case studies cover both successful upgrades and failed metallurgy choices.
- **Velocity and residence-time considerations for NAC** — explicit guidance on how shear-rate distribution drives NAC localization on vacuum-tower side draws and transfer lines, including the rule-of-thumb velocity bands at which the alloy ladder steps up. Companion to API RP 581 quantitative thinning damage-factor treatment for asset-specific rate prediction.
- **Overhead-system corrosion control** — water-wash design (rate, distribution, dew-point margin), neutralizer selection (ammonia vs. amine vs. caustic) and the trade-off against under-deposit ammonium-chloride corrosion, filming-amine inhibitor program. The section that field operators consult most frequently in day-to-day operations.
- **Inspection-programme guidance** — CML placement bias toward high-velocity points, the diagnostic separation of sulfidation rate from NAC rate using feedstock TAN and sulfur trends, the role of probe corrosion coupons and electrical-resistance probes in the overhead system, and the integration with API 510 / API 570 inspection cycles.

## Where it complements API RP 571

The two documents are **scope-complementary**, not redundant:

- **API RP 571** is the encyclopedic mechanism catalogue. Every refining damage mechanism (sulfidation, NAC, HCl, NH4HS, MIC, polythionic-acid SCC, chloride SCC, hot-hydrogen attack, creep, embrittlement families) is described once in canonical form. RP 571 is the umbrella reference for **any** refinery unit — FCC, hydrotreater, hydrocracker, reformer, alky, coker — not just crude.
- **NACE 34103** narrows to the **crude-unit operating reality**. Mechanisms are presented through the lens of crude-unit streams and equipment, with the field-monitoring and case-study material that RP 571 deliberately does not carry (RP 571 stays at the mechanism-description level to keep its umbrella scope tractable).

The practical division of labour:

- A refining engineer with a crude-unit corrosion problem reaches for **34103 first** (operational context, case studies, monitoring guidance) and **RP 571 second** (canonical mechanism description for any mechanism that the case study points to).
- For any other unit (FCC, HDS, hydrocracker, reformer, alky, coker, sulfur recovery, sour-water stripper), **RP 571 is the umbrella** and the analogous Practical-Damage-Mechanism publications are the unit-specific companions where they exist (see [[#Sibling publications]] below).

This split is also visible in the standards lineage downstream: API RP 581 embeds the modified Couper–Gorman thinning damage factor (umbrella, all units) and references RP 571 for mechanism descriptions; 34103 sits to the side as the crude-unit operations supplement that informs CML placement and inspection-interval decisions for the specific equipment in front of the inspector.

### Sibling publications

The NACE Practical-Damage-Mechanism family covers the major refinery process units. Each is a **scope-parallel** companion to RP 571 for its respective unit; promotion to first-class standards pages in this wiki is gated by source availability and concept-page demand.

- **NACE Publication 34104** — *Refinery FCC Practical Damage Mechanisms.* Sibling field guide for the fluid-catalytic-cracking unit (main fractionator slurry circuit, regenerator, reactor, flue-gas system). Future-promotion candidate; not currently a first-class standards page.
- **NACE Publication 34105** — *Refinery HDS Practical Damage Mechanisms.* Sibling for hydrodesulfurization / hydrotreater units; H2-bearing hot circuits where the modified-Couper–Gorman regime supersedes the McConomy regime documented in 34103. Future-promotion candidate.
- **NACE Publication 34106** — *Refinery Reformer Practical Damage Mechanisms.* Sibling for catalytic-reformer units; emphasizes high-temperature hydrogen attack and platinum-catalyst-section integrity. Future-promotion candidate.

These three siblings cover the **crude-unit-downstream process units** that consume crude-unit cuts. Together with 34103 they form the unit-by-unit field-monitoring layer above the unit-agnostic RP 571 mechanism catalogue.
