## Scope

ISO 15156-3 / NACE MR0175-3 is **Part 3 of the joint sour-service-materials qualification standard** governing **cracking-resistant corrosion-resistant alloys (CRAs) and other alloys** for use in petroleum and natural gas production equipment exposed to H2S-bearing (sour-service) environments. Part 3 is the CRA companion to:

- **Part 1** ([ampp-mr-0175-pt1](ampp-mr-0175-pt1.md)) — general principles for materials selection, environmental severity classification, and qualification framework.
- **Part 2** ([ampp-mr-0175-pt2](ampp-mr-0175-pt2.md)) — cracking-resistant carbon and low-alloy steels and the use of cast iron.
- **Part 3** (this page) — CRAs, including 13Cr martensitic stainless, 22Cr / 25Cr duplex and super-duplex stainless, austenitic stainless, nickel-based, and cobalt-based alloys.

Part 3 uses **partial-pressure-of-H2S, chloride-concentration, and temperature envelopes** per material group (rather than the pH-pH2S region zoning used in Part 2 for carbon and low-alloy steels) to define the qualification window for each CRA family. The standard tabulates pre-qualified compositional and condition envelopes for the major commercial CRA grades and provides a fitness-assessment route for materials that fall outside the pre-qualified tables. Cold-work caps, manufacturing-route restrictions (forged vs. cast vs. wrought), and weldment-specific qualification overlays apply per material group.

## Revision history

- **NACE MR0175 single-document era (1975-2002)** — CRA provisions were carried in the single-document NACE MR0175 (revised on a roughly biennial cycle); see [ampp-mr-0175-1995](ampp-mr-0175-1995.md) for the historical 1995 single-document edition.
- **2003 — ISO 15156-3 1st Edition** issued as part of the international harmonization that split the single-document MR0175 into three parts; on disk at `/mnt/ace/O&G-Standards/ISO/15xxx/ISO_15156_Pt_3_1st_Ed_(2003)_Cracking-resistant_CRAs_(corrosion_resistant_alloys)_and_other_alloys.pdf`.

- **2015 — Revision** with selected CRA-table updates (duplex, super-duplex envelopes).
- **2020 — 3rd Edition** (publisher-current); jointly published as ISO 15156-3:2020 and NACE MR0175-3:2020 (later AMPP MR0175 Part 3 post-2021 corporate rebrand). Not on disk; calc-callers needing the 2020 edition must procure from the publisher portal.

## Practitioner application

- **Subsea production-equipment CRA selection** — wellhead bodies and trim, Christmas tree components, manifolds, jumpers, flowlines (CRA cladding or CRA solid-wall) per [api-spec-6a](api-spec-6a.md) / API Spec 17D.
- **Topsides production-piping CRA selection** — duplex / super-duplex separator and gas-treatment piping for sour service.
- **Downhole-tubular CRA selection** — 13Cr / super-13Cr tubing, 22Cr / 25Cr completion strings.
- **Refining sour-service equipment** — hydroprocessing reactor internals, sour-water-stripper trays, amine-unit equipment where CRAs are selected over carbon/low-alloy steel.
- **HISC-under-CP overlay** — duplex / super-duplex CRAs additionally bind to [dnv-rp-f112](dnv-rp-f112.md) when subsea-and-cathodically-protected; the MR0175-3 envelope qualifies SSC; F112 qualifies HISC.

## Industry adoption

ISO 15156-3 is the **dominant international CRA sour-service standard**; downstream API, DNV, NORSOK, and operator specifications cite it for CRA qualification. Operator specifications (Shell DEP, BP ETP, Chevron, ExxonMobil GP, Equinor TR) routinely call MR0175-3 for sour-service CRA selection. Cross-jurisdictional acceptance is universal among major NOCs and IOCs; ISO and NACE/AMPP publish identical normative text.
