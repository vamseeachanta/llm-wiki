# DNV-RP-G101 — Risk Based Inspection of Offshore Topsides Static Mechanical Equipment

## Scope

DNV-RP-G101 is the DNV recommended practice for **risk-based inspection (RBI) of static mechanical equipment on offshore topsides** — pressure vessels, piping, heat exchangers, atmospheric and low-pressure tanks, separators, scrubbers, flare/relief systems, and pressure-relief devices installed on fixed jacket platforms, jack-ups, semisubmersible MODUs, FPSOs, FLNGs, FSRUs, and floating-production tendon and spar units. It is the offshore-topsides specialisation in the wider RBI ecosystem dominated onshore by [api-rp-580](api-rp-580.md) (qualitative methodology) and [api-rp-581](api-rp-581.md) (quantitative implementation).

The recommended practice covers semi-quantitative and quantitative RBI methodology adapted to offshore-topsides constraints; damage-mechanism screening for offshore-specific patterns (CO<sub>2</sub> corrosion, microbiologically influenced corrosion, sour-service cracking, corrosion under insulation, atmospheric salt-laden corrosion, hydrate-related erosion); consequence-of-failure (COF) modelling that incorporates offshore-specific impact categories — helideck-occupancy hazard, evacuation-route compromise, marine-environment release, weight-and-cost penalties for unscheduled offshore intervention, and TR (Temporary Refuge) integrity; inspection-effectiveness grading on offshore NDT methods accounting for access constraints, weather windows, and topside congestion; and integration with the wider DNV offshore standards family (DNV-OS, DNV-RP).

## Revision history

- **DNV-RP-G101:2002** — first publication; initial offshore-topsides RBI methodology.
- **DNV-RP-G101:2010** — second edition; expanded damage-mechanism catalogue and inspection-effectiveness grading.
- **DNV-RP-G101:2021-07** — current edition at time of writing; harmonized with DNV-RP-C210 (offshore-structural-fatigue RBI) and modernized COF treatment.

## Practitioner application

- Offshore-platform integrity-management engineers planning multi-year inspection campaigns on topside static mechanical equipment for fixed jacket platforms, jack-ups, semisubmersibles, FPSOs, FLNGs, and FSRUs.
- FPSO / FLNG / FSRU integrity-management teams running RBI on hydrocarbon process trains, gas-treatment trains, water-injection systems, and utility systems under offshore-specific COF constraints.
- Offshore EPCs (Aker Solutions, TechnipFMC, Saipem, McDermott, Subsea7, Worley) preparing inspection-and-monitoring philosophy documents for greenfield-platform deliverables, where DNV-RP-G101 provides the design-stage RBI framework on topside static mechanical equipment.
- Class societies — DNV, ABS, BV, LR — accepting DNV-RP-G101-based RBI arguments on classed offshore units, often in parallel with class-society-specific integrity-management notations.
- Operators running cross-jurisdictional asset portfolios pairing DNV-RP-G101 (offshore-topsides) with [api-rp-580](api-rp-580.md) / [api-rp-581](api-rp-581.md) (onshore process equipment) for portfolio-wide RBI coverage.

## Industry adoption

DNV-RP-G101 is the dominant offshore-topsides RBI recommended practice in North Sea (Norway, UK), Brazilian Basin, West Africa, Australia North-West Shelf, and Mediterranean offshore operations. It is a peer to [api-rp-580](api-rp-580.md) / [api-rp-581](api-rp-581.md), with the choice typically driven by jurisdiction, operator preference, and class-society relationship rather than technical superiority — both code families produce comparable risk-ranked inspection schedules when applied to the same equipment population with consistent inputs. The 2021-07 edition was harmonized with [dnv-rp-c210](dnv-rp-c210.md) (offshore-structural-fatigue probabilistic-inspection RP) so that operators running an integrated platform integrity-management programme can use DNV-RP-C210 for jacket/topside-structural-fatigue inspection planning and DNV-RP-G101 for topside process-equipment inspection planning under a coherent reliability framework.
