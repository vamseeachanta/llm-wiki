# ISO 15156 / NACE MR0175 — H2S Sour Service Materials

## Scope

Materials qualification standard for petroleum and natural gas production equipment exposed to H2S-containing (sour-service) environments. ISO 15156 is jointly published as NACE MR0175 (legacy) and AMPP MR0175 (current); the three publishing identifiers carry the same normative text. Three parts:

- **Part 1 — General principles** for selection of cracking-resistant metallic materials, including environmental severity classification, qualification framework, and definitions of sour-service exposure (wet H2S partial pressure, in-situ pH, chloride, elemental sulfur).
- **Part 2 — Cracking-resistant carbon and low-alloy steels and the use of cast iron**, the workhorse pipeline-and-pressure-vessel material class for sour service. Includes hardness limits, heat-treatment constraints, and SOHIC/SSC region zoning.
- **Part 3 — CRAs and other alloys**, covering austenitic stainless, duplex/super-duplex, martensitic stainless (13Cr), nickel-based, and cobalt-based alloy compositional and condition envelopes.

This is the dominant international sour-service materials code; downstream API, DNV, and operator specifications cite it for SSC/HIC qualification of metallic equipment in H2S service.

## Edition history

NACE MR0175 traces back to **1975** as a standalone NACE single-document edition; subsequent NACE editions ran on a roughly biennial cycle through the 1990s. The international harmonization with ISO began with the **1st Edition (2001/2003)** as a three-part split (Part 1 in 2001; Parts 2 and 3 in 2003), which is the edition retained on disk in `/mnt/ace/O&G-Standards/ISO/15xxx/`. The 2009 NACE re-issue (the second edition under the joint NACE/ISO arrangement) is on disk under the NACE folder. The publisher-current edition is the **2020 3rd Edition**, jointly published as ISO 15156:2020 and NACE/AMPP MR0175-2020. The 2021 NACE-to-AMPP corporate rebrand carried the document number unchanged.

## Key elements

(High-level inventory of the qualification framework — no normative thresholds reproduced.)

- **Environmental severity classification**: pH–pH2S diagram zoning of in-service exposure into severity regions; Part 2 introduces the four-region (0/1/2/3) sour-service classification for SOHIC/SSC qualification of carbon and low-alloy steel; Part 3 uses partial-pressure-and-chloride envelopes for CRA qualification.
- **Test methods** (acceptance criteria companion to NACE TM-series test specifications):
  - Sulfide stress cracking (SSC): NACE TM-0177 tensile (Method A), bent-beam (Method B), C-ring (Method C), 4-point bend (Method D).
  - Hydrogen-induced cracking (HIC) / stepwise cracking: NACE TM-0284 with Solutions A and B.
  - Slow strain-rate testing: ISO 7539-7 for SCC characterization.
- **Material-class qualification**:
  - Carbon and low-alloy steels (Pt 2): Rockwell C hardness caps, weld-zone hardness limits, heat-treatment condition restrictions, fitness-for-service assessment route, and cast-iron limited use.
  - CRAs (Pt 3): compositional envelopes for 13Cr martensitic, 22Cr / 25Cr duplex and super-duplex, austenitic stainless, nickel-based, and cobalt-based alloys; cold-work and partial-pressure / chloride / temperature limits per material group.
- **pH-pH2S diagrams**: graphical region maps that combine partial pressure of H2S, in-situ pH, chloride concentration, and temperature into qualification envelopes per material class.
- **Fitness assessment**: Part 1 outlines the qualification-vs-laboratory-data evidence routes for materials not pre-listed in the standard's tables.
