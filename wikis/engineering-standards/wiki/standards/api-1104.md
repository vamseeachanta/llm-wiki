# API 1104 — Welding of Pipelines and Related Facilities

## Scope

API 1104 is the canonical North American standard for the **arc and oxyacetylene welding of carbon and low-alloy steel piping** used in compression, pumping, and transmission of crude petroleum, petroleum products, fuel gases, carbon dioxide, and nitrogen. It governs welding procedure specifications (WPS) and procedure qualification records (PQR) for shop and field welds, welder performance qualification by destructive (mechanical) and bend testing, production weld acceptance under either workmanship (Section 9) or alternative (Annex/Appendix A) acceptance criteria, non-destructive examination (RT, UT/AUT, MT, PT, visual), repair welding limits, and in-service welding for hot-tap and repair-sleeve welds onto pressurized pipelines (Appendix B).

The standard is invoked normatively by **49 CFR 192/195** (US PHMSA pipeline safety regulations), **ASME B31.4 / B31.8** (pipeline construction codes), **CSA Z662** (Canadian cross-border consistency), **DNV-OS-F101** (Section 12 references), and most pipeline EPC general specifications.

## Key sections

The section numbering below tracks the 20th-edition structure; pin the edition in any downstream `Citation` you emit since structure and clause numbering shift across editions.

- **Section 5** — Welding procedure qualification; essential variables (base material, filler classification, joint design, position, electrical characteristics, preheat/interpass, PWHT) define the WPS envelope.
- **Section 6** — Welder performance qualification by destructive testing (tensile, nick-break, bend specimens).
- **Section 9** — Workmanship-based weld acceptance keyed to flaw type, length, and aggregated length per weld length.
- **Section 11** — Radiography and UT operations, including manual pulse-echo and Automated UT (AUT) for mechanized welds.
- **Section 10** — Repair welds; acceptable repair limits and requalification rules.
- **Appendix A** — Alternative acceptance criteria via Engineering Critical Assessment (ECA); the recognised North American hook into [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) UK and [api-579-1-asme-ffs-1](api-579-1-asme-ffs-1.md) US FFS methods.
- **Appendix B** — In-service welding; procedure qualification accounting for accelerated cooling and hydrogen-cracking risk under flowing-product conditions.

## Practitioner application

- Pipeline EPCs (Bechtel, McDermott, Saipem, Worley, Wood) qualifying line-pipe girth-weld procedures and welders for greenfield and expansion projects under 49 CFR 192/195 jurisdiction.
- Pipeline operators (transmission, gathering, distribution) maintaining in-service WPS portfolios and managing repair / hot-tap operations under Appendix B.
- ECA practitioners applying Appendix A alternative acceptance to defects detected by AUT in mechanized welds, where workmanship limits are exceeded but fitness-for-purpose can be demonstrated via [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) Level 2 fracture-mechanics assessment.
- Class societies (DNV, ABS, LR, BV) accepting API 1104 weld-procedure qualification on pipeline-adjacent floating-production-system risers and topside production piping.

## Industry adoption

API 1104 is the global default for onshore and offshore pipeline girth-weld qualification in the North American regulatory perimeter and is widely adopted internationally. It is paired with **ASME BPVC Section IX** for pressure-vessel welding at compressor stations, pump stations, and tie-in facilities — operators commonly maintain dual-qualification matrices. The standard is a peer to **DNV-OS-F101** (offshore subsea pipeline systems) and **ISO 13847** (international welding of pipelines for petroleum and natural-gas industries); jurisdiction selection drives code choice rather than technical superiority.
