# API Recommended Practice 2SIM — Structural Integrity Management of Fixed Offshore Structures

> **code_id:** `api-rp-2sim` · **publisher:** API · **revision:** 2nd ed (2019); current published edition.

## Scope

API RP 2SIM is the **structural integrity management (SIM) practice** for fixed offshore structures in service — primarily steel jacketed platforms in shallow-to-moderate water, but also caisson, well-protector, and minimal-platform structures. Where [api-rp-2a-wsd](api-rp-2a-wsd.md) is the design code for new fixed platforms, RP 2SIM governs **everything that happens after first oil**: in-service inspection planning, condition assessment, evaluation of existing platforms against current criteria when modified or extended, decommissioning evaluation, and the structural-integrity programme that ties it all together. RP 2SIM was created in 2014 to consolidate and supersede the existing-platform-assessment guidance previously embedded in [api-rp-2a-wsd](api-rp-2a-wsd.md) §17 — separating "design new" (RP 2A-WSD) from "manage existing" (RP 2SIM) into clean, complementary practices.

## Key sections

The headings below summarise the practice's structural backbone — clause text, exposure-category tables, and inspection-interval tabulations are **not reproduced** per metadata-only governance.

- **SIM framework.** Data → evaluation → strategy → programme cycle; analogous in spirit to RBI but with offshore-structure-specific damage mechanisms and consequence framings.
- **Exposure categories.** Categorisation of platforms by life-safety and consequence of failure (manned/unmanned, high/medium/low consequence) — drives inspection scope and assessment criteria.
- **In-service inspection planning.** Underwater inspection (general visual, close visual, NDE — flooded-member detection, cathodic-potential surveys), above-water inspection, scour-and-foundation surveys; inspection intervals tied to exposure category and prior-finding history.
- **Condition assessment.** Damage taxonomy: fatigue cracking at tubular joints, corrosion (atmospheric, splash-zone, submerged, internal), dropped-object damage, vessel-impact damage, scour, foundation settlement, marine growth.
- **Evaluation of existing platforms.** Triggers for re-assessment (change in use, life extension, missed inspection, observed damage); analytical methods including pushover analysis, ultimate-strength assessment, and platform-specific reliability methods.
- **Acceptance criteria.** Acceptance basis differs from [api-rp-2a-wsd](api-rp-2a-wsd.md) new-design WSD criteria; RP 2SIM uses ultimate-strength and reserve-strength-ratio (RSR) criteria more analogous to ULS / ALS in [iso-19902](iso-19902.md).
- **Mitigation and remediation.** Strengthening, modification, repair, monitoring; decommissioning evaluation as a SIM outcome.
- **Decommissioning.** Evaluation triggers and structural-integrity considerations for end-of-life and abandonment.

## Practitioner application

API RP 2SIM is the practice cited by:
- **Offshore structural engineers** managing in-service jacket platforms in the US Gulf of Mexico and other API-jurisdiction regions.
- **Inspection planners** scoping annual / quinquennial Level I-IV underwater surveys on fixed structures.
- **Operators** authoring SIM management plans required under BSEE 30 CFR 250 Safety and Environmental Management Systems (SEMS).
- **Life-extension engineers** evaluating whether a platform originally designed to a 25-year life can safely operate beyond design life.
- **Decommissioning engineers** evaluating structural-integrity status feeding the platform-removal plan.

FFS assessment under [api-std-579](api-std-579.md) / [api-579-1-asme-ffs-1](api-579-1-asme-ffs-1.md) is consumed component-by-component within the RP 2SIM evaluation framework — for example, FFS Part 9 crack-like-flaw assessment of a fatigue crack at a tubular joint feeds into the platform-level RP 2SIM ultimate-strength evaluation.

## Industry adoption

Adopted as the de-facto SIM practice for fixed offshore platforms in US Gulf of Mexico operations; cited by BSEE in offshore-operator audits. Internationally, RP 2SIM is one of three SIM-doctrine references commonly cited alongside [iso-19902](iso-19902.md) §14 and ISO 19901-9 (structural integrity management of offshore structures); operators in non-US jurisdictions select the framework that maps to their flag-state regulator.

The two regimes converge on **integrity-management methodology**: both reference **IACS UR M53** (mobile offshore units) and **DNV-OS-C401** hull-integrity envelopes for cyclical inspection planning, damage-mechanism cataloguing (fatigue cracking at structural details, corrosion in splash/submerged zones, scour/foundation interaction), and condition-assessment-driven re-inspection-interval logic. Disconnectable-turret FPSOs in NW-Australia cyclone corridors and Gulf-of-Mexico hurricane corridors sit at the explicit intersection — RP 2SIM ultimate-strength / RSR criteria size mooring-and-hull integrity envelopes while SOLAS Ch. II-1 intact and damage-stability margins govern post-disconnect transit. The **MODU Code (IMO Resolution A.1023(26))** provides the harmonization precedent that lets fixed-platform SIM doctrine map onto ship-shape mobile-unit life-extension cases.
