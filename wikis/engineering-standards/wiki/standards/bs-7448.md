## Scope

BS 7448 is the UK fracture-mechanics toughness-testing series, defining laboratory test methods for the three principal toughness parameters used downstream by [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) and [api-579-1-asme-ffs-1](api-579-1-asme-ffs-1.md) fitness-for-service assessments: plane-strain fracture toughness K<sub>Ic</sub>, the J-integral J, and the crack-tip opening displacement (CTOD, δ). The series provides the UK national companion to ASTM's parallel test methods [astm-e399](astm-e399.md) (K<sub>Ic</sub>) and [astm-e1820](astm-e1820.md) (unified K/J/CTOD), and is the predecessor of the ISO 12135 international unified series.

## Multi-part structure

The standard is published in four parts, each addressing a different specimen-and-loading configuration:

- **Part 1 (1991, A1:2008)** — Method for determination of K<sub>Ic</sub>, critical CTOD, and critical J values of metallic materials. The base test method, used with single-edge-notch bend (SENB) and compact-tension (CT) specimens machined from parent metal.
- **Part 2 (1997, A1:2003)** — Method for determination of K<sub>Ic</sub>, critical CTOD, and critical J values of welds in metallic materials. Extends Part 1 to welded-joint specimens, including notch-position rules (parent metal, weld metal, fusion line, HAZ), residual-stress conditioning, and additional qualification gates for weldment toughness.
- **Part 3 (2005)** — Method for determination of fracture toughness of metallic materials at rates of increase of stress intensity factor greater than 3.0 MPa·m<sup>0.5</sup>·s<sup>-1</sup> (dynamic / rapid-loading regime). Used for impact-loading toughness inputs to dynamic-fracture assessments.
- **Part 4 (2005)** — Method for determination of fracture resistance curves and initiation values for stable crack extension in metallic materials. R-curve / J-Δa testing for ductile-tearing-controlled assessments under [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) Level 3 and ASME FFS-1 Part 9 ductile-tearing routes.

Parts are typically cited together as the BS 7448 series; an assessment may invoke Part 1 for parent metal, Part 2 for weldment toughness, and Part 4 for tearing-resistance R-curves on the same project.

## Comparison with ASTM and ISO test methods

BS 7448 sits in a triangle with the ASTM and ISO equivalents. The three families are technically aligned but differ in defaults, qualification gates, and weldment-specific provisions:

| Dimension | BS 7448 (UK) | ASTM E399 / E1820 (US) | ISO 12135 (international) |
|---|---|---|---|
| K<sub>Ic</sub> single-parameter | Part 1 | [astm-e399](astm-e399.md) | ISO 12135 (unified) |
| CTOD / J unified | Part 1 (CTOD-prominent UK heritage) | [astm-e1820](astm-e1820.md) (J-prominent) | ISO 12135 (parallel methods) |
| Weldment toughness | Part 2 (explicit UK provisions) | E1820 + ISO 15653 weld appendix | ISO 15653 (companion) |
| Dynamic loading | Part 3 | E399 + dynamic supplements | ISO 26843 (companion) |
| R-curve / J-Δa | Part 4 | E1820 R-curve protocol | ISO 12135 R-curve clause |
| ECA / FFS consumer | [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) | [api-579-1-asme-ffs-1](api-579-1-asme-ffs-1.md) | Either, jurisdiction-dependent |

Practitioners must not mix-and-match clauses across families within a single assessment — each code's qualification gates (specimen size, fatigue-precracking, side-grooving, validity criteria) are internally calibrated against its consuming FFS code. ISO 12135 was issued as the harmonised international successor and is increasingly adopted; BS 7448 remains in active use for UK programmes and legacy citations.

## Edition history and PD-6493 lineage

The BS 7448 series superseded the legacy BSI Published Document **PD 6493** (which itself bundled flaw-assessment methodology with toughness-testing guidance until the 1990s split). When PD 6493 was reorganised, the flaw-assessment methodology became [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) (issued 1999), and the toughness-testing methodology became BS 7448 Parts 1-4 (issued 1991-2005). This split mirrors the US division between flaw-assessment codes (API 579 / ASME FFS-1) and test methods (ASTM E399 / E1820 / E1921 / E647).

The BS 7448 series itself is now partly superseded internationally by ISO 12135:2021, but the BSI catalogue retains all four parts as active UK national standards. BS 7448 Parts 1 and 2 carry amendments (A1) issued in 2008 and 2003 respectively. Part 3 and Part 4 (both 2005) have not been amended at time of writing.

## Practitioner application

- Materials-test laboratories conducting UK-jurisdiction toughness qualification (offshore-fabrication weld procedures, pressure-vessel material qualification, pipeline girth-weld ECA inputs).
- Welding engineers preparing CTOD or J test campaigns under [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) Level 2 or Level 3 ECA workflows, where Part 2 provides the weldment-specific test protocol.
- Class societies — DNV, LR, BV, ABS — accepting BS 7448 toughness data as input to offshore structural certification (DNV-OS series rules typically accept BS 7448, ASTM E1820, or ISO 12135 interchangeably).
- Pipeline EPCs preparing girth-weld qualification campaigns for sour-service, low-temperature, or strain-based-design projects, where CTOD testing per Part 2 feeds the [bs-7910](bs-7910.md) Annex A FAD.
