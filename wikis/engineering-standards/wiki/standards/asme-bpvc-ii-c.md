# ASME BPVC Section II Part C — Specifications for Welding Rods, Electrodes, and Filler Metals

**code_id:** `asme-bpvc-ii-c` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **publisher:** ASME (American Society of Mechanical Engineers) &nbsp;·&nbsp; **revision:** 2023 BPVC cycle

## Scope

ASME BPVC Section II Part C is the **filler-metals volume of the ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code Section II (Materials)**, supplying the SFA-prefixed welding-consumable specifications (collectively known as the SFA specifications) covering arc-welding electrodes, bare welding rods and wires, filler metals for shielded metal-arc welding (SMAW), gas tungsten-arc welding (GTAW), gas metal-arc welding (GMAW), flux-cored arc welding (FCAW), submerged-arc welding (SAW), electroslag welding, brazing alloys and fluxes, and related consumables for carbon, low-alloy, stainless, nickel-alloy, copper-alloy, aluminum-alloy, and titanium materials.

The SFA specifications in Part C are technically equivalent to and harmonized with the AWS A5.x specification family (AWS A5.1 = SFA-5.1 for SMAW carbon-steel electrodes, AWS A5.4 = SFA-5.4 for stainless SMAW, etc.). Part C also defines the **F-Number** filler-metal grouping system used by [asme-bpvc-ix](asme-bpvc-ix.md) for welding-procedure essential-variable control during procedure qualification.

## Revision history

ASME BPVC Section II Part C is published on the standard ASME BPVC 2-year revision cycle:

- **2010 / 2013 / 2015 / 2017 / 2019 / 2021** — sequential 2-year cycle editions; each adds new SFA specifications, withdraws obsolete ones, and harmonizes against the latest AWS A5.x revisions.
- **2023** — current BPVC cycle.

Consult the ASME publications catalog for the BPVC edition in force at time of citation.

## Key sections

The SFA specifications are organized by welding process and material family (verify against the publisher copy in any normative work):

- **SFA-5.1** — SMAW carbon-steel electrodes (AWS A5.1).
- **SFA-5.4** — SMAW stainless-steel electrodes (AWS A5.4).
- **SFA-5.5** — SMAW low-alloy electrodes (AWS A5.5).
- **SFA-5.9** — Bare stainless-steel rods and electrodes (AWS A5.9).
- **SFA-5.11** — SMAW nickel-alloy electrodes (AWS A5.11).
- **SFA-5.14** — Bare nickel-alloy rods and electrodes (AWS A5.14).
- **SFA-5.17** — Carbon-steel SAW solid wires and fluxes (AWS A5.17).
- **SFA-5.18** — Carbon-steel GMAW/GTAW filler metals (AWS A5.18).
- **SFA-5.20** — Carbon-steel FCAW electrodes (AWS A5.20).
- **SFA-5.22** — Stainless-steel FCAW electrodes (AWS A5.22).
- **SFA-5.28** — Low-alloy GMAW/GTAW filler metals (AWS A5.28).
- **SFA-5.29** — Low-alloy FCAW electrodes (AWS A5.29).
- **F-Number tables** — filler-metal groupings (F-No. 1 through F-No. 7 ferrous; F-Nos. 21–24 aluminum; F-No. 31 copper; F-Nos. 41–46 nickel; etc.) used by Section IX as essential variables.

## Practitioner application

- Welding engineers and Certified Welding Inspectors qualifying WPS / PQR / WPQ documentation under [asme-bpvc-ix](asme-bpvc-ix.md) and the B31 piping codes; Section II Part C supplies the filler-metal classification and F-Number that anchor the procedure essential-variable matrix.
- Filler-metal manufacturers (Lincoln Electric, ESAB, Hobart, Bohler, Sandvik, etc.) certifying consumables to SFA specifications and marking/branding accordingly.
- EPCs and fabricators specifying weld consumables on procurement documents — typically dual-cited as "SFA-5.1 / AWS A5.1, classification E7018" and similar.
- Metallurgical and corrosion engineers selecting weld consumables for sour-service, low-temperature, hydrogen-service, and dissimilar-metal-weld applications, working from SFA-specification chemistry and mechanical-property limits.

## Industry adoption

ASME BPVC Section II Part C is the canonical filler-metal-specification reference across all ASME-jurisdiction work in the US (BPVC pressure-vessel construction, B31 piping, Section IX welding-procedure qualification) and is widely adopted internationally. The SFA specifications are functionally interchangeable with the AWS A5.x family and are commonly dual-cited on procurement documents to satisfy both ASME-jurisdiction and AWS-only consumers. Class societies (ABS, DNV, BV, LR) accept SFA-specification consumables on offshore-fabrication scope when paired with appropriate Section IX procedure qualification.
