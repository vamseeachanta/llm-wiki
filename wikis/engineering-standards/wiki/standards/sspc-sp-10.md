# SSPC-SP 10 / NACE No. 2 — Near-White Metal Blast Cleaning

Bounded metadata-only resolver page for downstream `Citation(...)` callers under the calc-citation contract at `.claude/rules/calc-citation-contract.md`. Page contains no clause text, photographic-reference reproductions, or numerical inspection thresholds — the procedural detail must be read from a procured copy of the publisher source.

## Scope

SSPC-SP 10 (jointly NACE No. 2) specifies the requirements for **near-white metal blast cleaning** of unpainted or painted carbon-steel surfaces by abrasive blasting prior to the application of protective coatings. It is the surface-preparation grade required by most high-performance offshore, splash-zone, immersion, and refinery coating systems and is the dominant North-American counterpart to ISO 8501-1 grade **Sa 2½**. The standard prescribes:

- **Cleanliness criteria** — at least 95% of each unit area free of visible mill scale, rust, rust-stain, paint, and foreign matter, with light shadows, slight streaks, or discolorations permitted on the remaining 5%.
- **Inspection method** — visual comparison against the SSPC-VIS 1 photographic standards (or NACE TM 0175 visual references), with the surface viewed under specified lighting conditions.
- **Surface-profile requirement** — anchor-pattern profile depth specified separately by the coating manufacturer, typically measured per ASTM D4417 (replica tape, depth micrometer, or surface profilometer).
- **Pre-blast surface preparation** — removal of oil, grease, and soluble contaminants per SSPC-SP 1 prior to abrasive blasting.

The standard does **not** prescribe abrasive type, nozzle pressure, or blast equipment; those parameters are selected by the applicator to meet the cleanliness and profile criteria for the specified coating system.

## Practitioner application

- Coating contractors and applicators specifying surface preparation for offshore topsides, jackets, FPSO hulls, refinery vessels, tanks, and pipeline external coatings.
- Coating manufacturers — International, Hempel, Jotun, AkzoNobel, PPG, Sherwin-Williams — referencing SSPC-SP 10 in product datasheets as the minimum surface-preparation grade for high-performance epoxy, polyurethane, zinc-rich primer, and 3LPE/3LPP/FBE pipeline-coating systems.
- Owner-operator coating specifications (Shell DEP, BP ETP, Chevron, ExxonMobil GP) routinely call SSPC-SP 10 for splash-zone, immersion, and high-corrosivity-category service.
- Inspection chain typically pairs SSPC-SP 10 with SSPC-VIS 1 (visual reference photographs), ASTM D4417 (profile measurement), and SSPC-Guide 15 / ISO 8502-6 Bresle test (residual-salt screening).

## Industry adoption

SSPC-SP 10 / NACE No. 2 is the global default near-white surface-prep grade for high-performance protective coatings on carbon steel. Sister surface-prep standards form a complete cleanliness gradient:

- **SSPC-SP 5 / NACE No. 1** — White Metal Blast Cleaning (100% removal; the highest grade, equivalent to ISO 8501-1 Sa 3). Used for severe immersion, chemical-resistant linings, and splash-zone hot service.
- **SSPC-SP 6 / NACE No. 3** — Commercial Blast Cleaning (66%+ clean, equivalent to ISO 8501-1 Sa 2). Used for lower-corrosivity industrial atmospheric service.
- **SSPC-SP 7 / NACE No. 4** — Brush-Off Blast Cleaning (visible mill scale, paint, rust permitted if tightly adherent; equivalent to ISO 8501-1 Sa 1). Used for low-criticality maintenance-overcoat scenarios.
- **SSPC-SP 11** — Power Tool Cleaning to Bare Metal. Used where abrasive blasting is not feasible (offshore in-service patch repair, congested refinery interior).
- **SSPC-SP 15** — Commercial-grade Power Tool Cleaning. Mid-grade power-tool alternative to SP 6.
- **ISO 8501-1 Sa 2½** — international counterpart equivalent to SSPC-SP 10 by harmonization clause; coating-manufacturer datasheets typically list both for jurisdictional cross-reference.
