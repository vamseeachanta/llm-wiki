# MV Dali (2024)

## Case overview

**Facts.** The MV *Dali* was a Singapore-flag, 9,962 TEU container vessel built 2015, owned by Grace Ocean Pte Ltd (Singapore), operated under technical management by Synergy Marine Group, and on time charter to Maersk Line A/S at the time of casualty. At approximately 01:27 EDT on 26 March 2024, while outbound from the Port of Baltimore, Maryland, in the Patapsco River, the vessel suffered a series of electrical-power-management failures, lost propulsion and steering control approximately 0.6 nautical miles from the Francis Scott Key Bridge, and struck the bridge's southwest support column at approximately 7.5 knots. The bridge — a 2,632-metre continuous-truss vehicular structure carrying I-695, opened in 1977 — collapsed almost in its entirety into the channel within seconds of impact. Six members of a roadway-maintenance crew working on the bridge at the time were killed; two were rescued. The Port of Baltimore's main shipping channel was blocked for 11 weeks, requiring complex wreckage-removal operations by US Army Corps of Engineers, US Navy SUPSALV, and contracted commercial salvors before partial channel restoration on 10 June 2024 and full restoration on 25 June 2024. Direct property and consequential damage estimates exceeded US$1.5 billion; the State of Maryland's bridge-replacement project was estimated at approximately US$1.7–2.0 billion.

**Issue.** (i) Whether Grace Ocean and Synergy Marine, as registered owner and operator-manager respectively, can avail themselves of the US Limitation of Liability Act of 1851 (46 U.S.C. §§ 30501 et seq., commonly "LOLA") to cap aggregate liability at the post-casualty market value of the vessel and pending freight (approximately US$43.7 million for hull alone, modified upward to roughly US$87 million accounting for double-hull tonnage in petitioners' filings). (ii) Whether the §30530 "privity or knowledge" cap-break gateway is satisfied by allegations of pre-casualty knowledge of electrical-power-management deficiencies. (iii) Coordination with OPA 90 (33 U.S.C. §§ 2701–2762) for the bunker fuel released, the Bunkers Convention 2001 (operational backstop given Singapore-flag), and the Wreck Removal Convention framework engaged on the partially-collapsed bridge structure. (iv) Multidistrict litigation (MDL) consolidation across personal-injury, property-damage, business-interruption, and government-claimant tracks.

## Procedural history

- **2 April 2024 — Limitation petition.** Grace Ocean and Synergy Marine filed a petition under 46 U.S.C. §§ 30501 et seq. in the US District Court for the District of Maryland (*In re the Petition of Grace Ocean Private Ltd. and Synergy Marine Pte Ltd.*), seeking either exoneration from or limitation of liability and proposing an interim limitation fund of approximately US$43.7 million.
- **April–May 2024 — Initial claimants and case-management.** Wrongful-death, personal-injury, US Government (Department of Justice on behalf of multiple agencies), Maryland Transportation Authority, City of Baltimore, business-interruption (cargo, freight-forwarding, port-tenant), and contingency-claimant filings began. The court entered an initial monition and case-management order.
- **24 September 2024 — US Department of Justice civil claim.** DOJ filed a US$103 million civil claim under the Rivers and Harbors Act 1899 and OPA 90 for channel-clearance, bunker-spill response, and federal-property-damage costs, asserting that Grace Ocean and Synergy were precluded from limitation by the §30530 "privity or knowledge" gateway based on alleged pre-casualty awareness of electrical-power-management deficiencies.
- **October 2024 — Maryland State and City of Baltimore claims.** Maryland filed claims for bridge-replacement costs and lost-toll-revenue; the City of Baltimore filed claims for port-disruption and infrastructure-impact costs.
- **2024–ongoing — Coast Guard Marine Board of Investigation and NTSB Marine Accident Investigation.** Parallel safety-investigation proceedings, with NTSB issuing preliminary findings on electrical-power-management causation in 2024 and continuing through anticipated 2025–2026 final reports.
- **MDL consolidation under consideration.** As of mid-2024, the Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation considered consolidation of related federal claims; coordination with the Maryland District Court limitation proceeding shaped the procedural architecture.

## Key legal holdings

- **US Limitation of Liability Act 1851 (LOLA) framework engagement.** LOLA permits a vessel-owner to limit liability for damages arising from a maritime casualty to the post-casualty value of the vessel plus pending freight, provided the casualty occurred without the owner's "privity or knowledge" (§30530). The Act applies to seagoing vessels and inland vessels alike and predates the modern IMO LLMC 1996 framework (the United States is not a party to LLMC 1996).
- **§30530 "privity or knowledge" cap-break gateway.** The leading test traces to *Coryell v Phipps* (US 317 1940) and a substantial subsequent body of circuit authority. The owner bears the burden of proving lack of privity or knowledge; corporate-owner privity is assessed against the knowledge of supervisory management (not the master or crew). Modern application in the wake of *In re Otal Investments* (2nd Cir. 2010) and other circuit authority gives substantial weight to ISM Code-mandated safety-management-system failures as a privity-or-knowledge basis.
- **Tension between LOLA cap and OPA 90 unlimited-liability framework.** OPA 90 §2704(c)(1) lifts its statutory cap for gross negligence or violation of regulatory requirement; the relationship between OPA 90 §2718 (state-law preservation) and LOLA limitation is doctrinally contested. *Dali* is positioned to test whether the OPA 90 framework operates wholly outside LOLA limitation or whether LOLA caps aggregate non-OPA-90 claims while OPA 90 governs pollution claims separately.
- **Bunkers Convention 2001 backstop.** As a Singapore-flag vessel in US waters, the Bunkers Convention 2001 strict-liability framework operationally interacted with OPA 90 and LOLA; Singapore as flag state issued the Bunkers Certificate of Insurance underlying the operational P&I cover.
- **First major modern test of LOLA against bridge-collapse damages.** Prior bridge-collision LOLA proceedings (*In re Maris Petroleum* 1980, various subsequent district-court cases) involved smaller damage scales; *Dali* is the first modern major test against multi-billion-dollar government-claimant exposure with double-hull tonnage and post-OPA-90 framework integration.

## Doctrinal significance

- **First major US 1851 LOLA test against modern post-OPA-90 + Bunkers framework.** *Dali* is the doctrinal anchor for resolving the LOLA / OPA 90 / Bunkers Convention interaction at modern incident scale. Pre-*Dali*, the interaction was discussed in academic and practitioner commentary but lacked a major court-tested data point.
- **Cap-quantum-versus-damages disconnect at unprecedented scale.** The approximately US$87 million LOLA cap (post-double-hull modification) compared against US$1.5 billion+ direct damages and US$2 billion+ State of Maryland bridge-replacement exposure illustrates the modern LOLA framework's structural disconnect with 21st-century damage scales — a doctrinal pressure point analogous to (and now exceeding) the *Exxon Valdez* OPA 90 driver and *Costa Concordia* LLMC pressure.
- **Privity-or-knowledge gateway and ISM Code intersection.** The DOJ's framing of pre-casualty electrical-power-management knowledge as a privity-or-knowledge basis is the leading post-2010 application of ISM Code safety-management-system documentation as a cap-break evidentiary lever, building on *In re Otal Investments* and the wider 2nd / 5th / 9th Circuit body of LOLA authority.
- **Potential legislative response.** US Congressional commentary post-casualty (Senate Commerce Committee and House Transportation & Infrastructure Committee 2024 hearings) revisited proposals for LOLA reform that have circulated since *Exxon Valdez* (1989), *Deepwater Horizon* (2010), and *Costa Concordia* (2012); *Dali* is positioned to be the calibration data point for such reform.
- **Multi-claimant coordination architecture.** The interaction of the limitation-fund proceeding (concursus, all claims must be filed in the limitation court) with anticipated MDL coordination, OPA 90 Oil Spill Liability Trust Fund mechanics, and parallel state and federal claims is a major procedural test for modern multi-claimant US maritime litigation.

## Practitioner impact

- **For container-line operators and time-charterers.** *Dali* reinforces the operational risk profile of confined-waterway transits, particularly under power-management-system stress; chartered-tug-escort decisions, harbour-pilot coordination, and pre-departure systems-readiness review have been scrutinised against the *Dali* benchmark.
- **For owner / operator-manager structures.** The Grace Ocean / Synergy Marine owner-manager split is a typical modern container-line operating structure; *Dali* will likely produce substantial authority on whether operator-manager privity-or-knowledge attaches to owner for LOLA purposes, an issue of broad practitioner relevance.
- **For P&I clubs and hull underwriters.** The case is now a standard reference in P&I cover review for container-line and confined-waterway transits, particularly on the LOLA / OPA 90 / Bunkers interaction and on US-domestic claim-aggregation exposure beyond conventional towers.
- **For state and federal government claimants.** DOJ's claim methodology — combining channel-clearance, bunker-response, and federal-property-damage components under both OPA 90 and Rivers and Harbors Act 1899 — provides a template for analogous government-claimant filings in future major US-waters casualties.
- **For port authorities and bridge owners.** Post-*Dali* infrastructure-protection reviews (vessel-strike risk modelling, fender-system upgrades, traffic-management protocols on approach to major bridges) accelerated across US ports and federal-channel infrastructure.
- **For the US salvage and wreck-removal sector.** US Army Corps of Engineers + US Navy SUPSALV + commercial-salvor (Donjon Marine, Resolve Marine, Skanska) coordinated operations on the partially-collapsed bridge and the entrapped vessel are a reference for future major-bridge-collapse and confined-channel-clearance operations.
- **For the legislative-reform community.** Practitioners should track 2024–2026 LOLA reform proposals; substantive cap-modernisation legislation, even if not enacted in the near term, is a foreseeable downstream consequence of *Dali*-driven public attention.

## Subsequent application

- **DOJ § 2704(c) gross-negligence framing.** The DOJ's framing of OPA 90 cap-loss against alleged power-management-system deficiencies builds on *Deepwater Horizon* (E.D. La. MDL 2179) and *Athos I* (3rd Cir. 2008) authority; *Dali* will likely produce substantial appellate authority on the interaction between OPA 90 § 2704(c) cap-loss and LOLA § 30530 cap-break.
- **Subsequent bridge-strike and harbour-collision incidents.** Practitioners will cite *Dali* in advising on liability exposure, LOLA / OPA 90 framework engagement, and government-claim coordination for analogous incidents in US waters and in foreign comparative-jurisdiction analyses.
- **NTSB / Coast Guard Marine Board findings.** The forthcoming final-investigation reports will substantially shape litigation framing on causation (electrical-power-management, classification-society oversight, ISM Code compliance, pre-departure coordination).
- **Port-infrastructure-protection updates.** Post-*Dali* federal and state infrastructure-protection budget allocations (FY 2025+ federal infrastructure law amendments and state-level bridge-protection programmes) reflect the casualty's policy impact.
- **Maritime law reform discourse.** Academic and practitioner commentary cite *Dali* as the leading pressure-point case on LOLA modernisation, alongside the older *Exxon Valdez* + *Deepwater Horizon* + *Costa Concordia* arc of cap-pressure events.
