# Salvage — LOF, SCOPIC, and the 1989 Salvage Convention

## Scope

Salvage is the maritime law of voluntary, successful rescue of property at peril at sea by a non-contracting third party, who earns a reward proportionate to the danger averted and the value saved. This page covers the salvor-claim doctrine (private rescue for reward); it does NOT cover general average (see `./general-average.md`) or wreck removal (Nairobi Convention 2007 — deferred to a future page).

## Doctrine

- Three classical elements (Schoenbaum Ch. 16; Mandaraka-Sheppard Vol 2 Ch. 8): **(1)** real maritime peril, **(2)** voluntary service (no pre-existing duty), and **(3)** success in whole or in part — captured in the maxim "no cure, no pay".
- Codification: **International Convention on Salvage 1989** (IMO; entered into force 1996) replaces the 1910 Brussels Convention. Article 13 lists the criteria for fixing the salvage reward (salved value, skill and efforts, danger, time, expenses).
- **Article 14 special compensation** introduces a partial exception to "no cure, no pay" for environmentally threatening casualties — salvors recover expenses (and up to 30% / 100% uplift) even where the property is lost, where they prevented or minimised damage to the environment.
- Standard contract surface: **Lloyd's Open Form (LOF 2020)** — the dominant industry contract; arbitration in London under Lloyd's Salvage Arbitration Branch.
- **SCOPIC clause** (Special Compensation P&I Club Clause, current revision 2020) — invoked by salvors to substitute the LOF Article 14 mechanism with a fixed-tariff regime backed by the property underwriters' P&I clubs; reduces uncertainty for both parties.
- Civil-law jurisdictions reach equivalent outcomes via maritime-code provisions (e.g., French Code des transports L5132-1 et seq.).

## Cases

- **MV Ever Given (2021)** — SMIT Salvage and Nippon Salvage refloated the vessel after a six-day Suez grounding; salvage award negotiated separately from the canal authority's detention claim. See `../entities/mv-ever-given-2021.md`.
- **The Sea Empress (1996)** — salvage operations during the Milford Haven grounding became the subject of subsequent litigation between salvors and port authority. See `../entities/sea-empress-1996.md`.
- **The Nagasaki Spirit (1997) AC 455** — UK House of Lords decision interpreting "expenses" under Article 14 of the 1989 Salvage Convention.
