# Environmental Liability in Maritime Law

The legal framework for holding parties accountable for marine pollution incidents, including oil spills, chemical releases, and ecological damage.

## Evolution

| Era | Key Development | Precedent |
|-----|----------------|-----------|
| 1967+ | CLC 1969 created strict liability regime | [[torrey-canyon-1967]] |
| 1978+ | Trans-boundary damages established | [[amoco-cadiz-1978]] |
| 1990 | OPA 90 — unlimited gross negligence (US) | [[deepwater-horizon-2010]] |
| 1996 | Port authority criminal liability | [[sea-empress-1996]] |
| 1999 | Corporate criminal liability, 'préjudice écologique' | [[mv-erika-1999]] |
| 2020 | MARPOL prosecution + crew welfare | [[mv-wakashio-2020]] |

## Compensation Mechanisms

1. **CLC 1992** — Strict liability for tanker spills (insured, capped)
2. **IOPC Funds** — Supplemental compensation when CLC limits are exceeded
3. **Bunker Convention 2001** — Non-tanker oil pollution
4. **HNS Convention 2010** — Hazardous substances
5. **National regimes** (e.g., OPA 90 in US — unlimited for gross negligence)
