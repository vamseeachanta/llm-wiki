# STCW 1978 — Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers (paired-slug resolver)

> **Paired-slug resolver page.** This is the `stcw-1978` slug used by citing pages that refer to the convention by its founding-treaty year. The full doctrinal synthesis lives at the canonical sibling page **[STCW Convention](../standards/stcw-convention.md)** under the `stcw-convention` slug, which is pinned to the consolidated **STCW 1978 + 1995 Amendments + 2010 Manila Amendments** edition.
>
> The two slugs name the **same instrument** — the IMO "fourth pillar" convention establishing minimum global training, certification, and watchkeeping standards for seafarers. `stcw-1978` references the original founding treaty year (London, 1978-07-07; entry-into-force 1984-04-28); `stcw-convention` is the slug pinned to the consolidated post-Manila edition currently in force. Per the W214 paired-slug pattern (api-17a / api-17h) and W217 (bs-7608 / bs-7608-fatigue-design), both slugs are retained for backward link compatibility and citation-style flexibility.

## Why this page exists

Citing pages in the maritime-law concepts and standards tiers reference this convention as `stcw-1978` (the founding-year slug) rather than `stcw-convention` (the consolidated-edition slug). Without this paired-slug resolver, link verifiers report `stcw-1978` as a missing target. This page closes the gap and forwards readers to the canonical synthesis. It is **not** a distinct treaty page — there is one STCW instrument, and the canonical doctrinal content lives at the sibling page.

## Why two slugs

Two stable citation styles exist in maritime-law literature and practice:

- **`stcw-1978`** — anchors the founding-treaty year and is the prevailing style in legal-academic and regulatory-history contexts where the original convention's entry-into-force date matters.
- **`stcw-convention`** — anchors the consolidated post-Manila convention and is the prevailing style in operational, training-curriculum, and flag-state-administration contexts where the currently-in-force edition matters.

Both styles appear across the wiki's citing pages (ISM Code, MLC 2006, SOLAS 1974, et al.). Per the workspace paired-slug pattern, the resolver page (this file) carries minimal metadata + a forwarding pointer; the canonical page carries the full synthesis. Operationally, both pages should be treated as equivalent citation targets.
