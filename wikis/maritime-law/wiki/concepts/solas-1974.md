# SOLAS 1974 — International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea

## Scope

SOLAS is the principal IMO instrument governing the safety of merchant ships. The 1974 Convention superseded earlier SOLAS texts (1914, 1929, 1948, 1960) and is the broadest in scope, covering construction, equipment, operation, and certification. **Entry into force**: 25 May 1980. The 1988 Protocol (HSSC) and 1978 Protocol introduce harmonised survey and certification regimes.

## Structure

- **Chapter I** — General provisions, surveys, certificates
- **Chapter II-1** — Construction (subdivision, stability, machinery, electrical)
- **Chapter II-2** — Construction (fire protection, detection, extinction)
- **Chapter III** — Life-saving appliances and arrangements
- **Chapter IV** — Radiocommunications (GMDSS)
- **Chapter V** — Safety of navigation
- **Chapter VI** — Carriage of cargoes
- **Chapter VII** — Carriage of dangerous goods (incorporates IMDG Code)
- **Chapter VIII** — Nuclear ships
- **Chapter IX** — Management for the safe operation of ships (incorporates the **ISM Code** — see `./ism-code.md`)
- **Chapter X** — Safety measures for high-speed craft
- **Chapter XI-1** — Special measures to enhance maritime safety
- **Chapter XI-2** — Special measures to enhance maritime security (incorporates ISPS Code)
- **Chapter XII** — Additional safety measures for bulk carriers
- **Chapter XIII** — Verification of compliance (mandatory IMSAS)
- **Chapter XIV** — Safety measures for ships operating in polar waters (Polar Code)

## Key Mechanisms

- **Statutory survey and certification** — flag state (or its Recognised Organisation) issues the Cargo Ship Safety Construction, Equipment, and Radio Certificates and the Passenger Ship Safety Certificate.
- **Goal-Based Standards (GBS)** — performance-oriented standards for the construction of bulk carriers and oil tankers since 2016.
- **Cyber-resilience** amendments to Chapter II-1 take effect under the SOLAS amendment chain.
- **Tacit-amendment procedure** — amendments adopted by MSC enter into force unless rejected by a defined number of parties.
- **Designated Person Ashore** is required by ISM Code (SOLAS Ch. IX) — see `./ism-code.md`.
