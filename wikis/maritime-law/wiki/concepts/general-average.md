# General Average — York-Antwerp Rules

## Scope

General average is the ancient maritime doctrine under which an extraordinary sacrifice or expenditure intentionally and reasonably made to preserve a maritime adventure (ship plus cargo plus freight) from peril is shared rateably among all parties whose property was saved. This page covers the doctrine and its modern codification in the York-Antwerp Rules; it is NOT a page on salvage (compensated rescue by third parties — see `./salvage.md`) nor on marine insurance (which indemnifies the contributing interests — see `./marine-insurance.md`).

## Doctrine

- The defining requirements (per Rule A, York-Antwerp Rules 2016): an extraordinary sacrifice or expenditure, voluntarily and reasonably made, in time of peril, for the common safety of the maritime adventure.
- Contributions are levied on the values saved (ship, cargo, freight at risk) in proportion to the saved values at the time and place the adventure ends.
- Codification surface: **York-Antwerp Rules** — non-statutory rules promulgated by the Comité Maritime International (CMI), incorporated by reference in nearly every voyage charter, time charter, and bill of lading; current versions are YAR 1994, YAR 2004, and YAR 2016. US admiralty (Schoenbaum Ch. 17) and UK practice (Mandaraka-Sheppard) treat the YAR as the operative rule-set; civil-law systems reach the same outcome via national maritime codes (e.g., French Code des transports L5421-1 et seq.).
- Adjustment is performed by an independent **average adjuster** (in the UK, members of the Association of Average Adjusters) who issues a statement allocating loss across the contributing interests.
- Modern context: **container losses, salvage and Suez-canal blockage incidents** routinely trigger general average declarations, with adjustments running for years.

## Cases

- **MV Ever Given (2021)** — owner Shoei Kisen declared general average days after the Suez grounding; Egyptian authorities' separate canal-toll claim was settled outside the GA process. See `../entities/mv-ever-given-2021.md`.
- **MSC Flaminia (2012)** — fire and explosion triggered general-average sacrifices and contributions allocated against shippers; entity page `../entities/msc-flaminia-2012.md`.
