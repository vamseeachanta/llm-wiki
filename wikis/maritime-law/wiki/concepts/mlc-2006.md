# MLC 2006 — Maritime Labour Convention

## Scope

The Maritime Labour Convention 2006 is the **International Labour Organization** (not IMO) instrument consolidating sixty-eight earlier ILO maritime labour conventions and recommendations into a single comprehensive instrument. It is often called the "seafarers' bill of rights" and applies to all ships ordinarily engaged in commercial activities, with limited exceptions. **Entry into force**: 20 August 2013, twelve months after ratification by 30 member states representing at least 33 per cent of world tonnage.

## Structure

- **Articles** — fundamental rights and principles
- **Title 1** — Minimum requirements for seafarers to work on a ship
- **Title 2** — Conditions of employment
- **Title 3** — Accommodation, recreational facilities, food, and catering
- **Title 4** — Health protection, medical care, welfare, and social security protection
- **Title 5** — Compliance and enforcement (including flag-state inspections, port-state control, and labour-supplying responsibilities)

## Key Mechanisms

- **Maritime Labour Certificate** and **Declaration of Maritime Labour Compliance (DMLC Parts I and II)** — issued by the flag state to ships of 500 GT or more engaged in international voyages.
- **Seafarers' Employment Agreement (SEA)** — written individual employment contract required for every seafarer; minimum content prescribed by Title 2.
- **Financial security for abandonment, death, and long-term disability** — added by the 2014 amendments (in force 2017); ensures repatriation costs and back-wages where the shipowner abandons the seafarer.
- **Port-state inspection** under Title 5 — port states inspect foreign-flag vessels for MLC compliance and may detain on substantial deficiencies.
- **Special Tripartite Committee (STC)** — ILO body that adopts amendments via tripartite (governments, shipowners, seafarers) negotiation; cadence is independent from IMO MSC/MEPC.
