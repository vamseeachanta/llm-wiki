# MARPOL 73/78 — International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships

## Scope

MARPOL is the principal IMO instrument addressing operational and accidental pollution of the marine environment from ships. The 1973 Convention as modified by the 1978 Protocol (and a further 1997 Protocol introducing Annex VI) applies to all vessels operating in marine waters of states party to the Convention, with state-flag enforcement supplemented by port-state control. **Entry into force**: 2 October 1983 (Annexes I and II); subsequent annexes phased in through 2005 (Annex VI).

## Structure

- **Annex I** — Regulations for the Prevention of Pollution by Oil
- **Annex II** — Regulations for the Control of Pollution by Noxious Liquid Substances in Bulk
- **Annex III** — Prevention of Pollution by Harmful Substances Carried by Sea in Packaged Form
- **Annex IV** — Prevention of Pollution by Sewage from Ships
- **Annex V** — Prevention of Pollution by Garbage from Ships
- **Annex VI** — Prevention of Air Pollution from Ships (added by 1997 Protocol)

## Key Mechanisms

- **Special Areas and Emission Control Areas (ECAs)** — designated regions with stricter discharge or emission limits, enforced by the relevant flag and port states.
- **IOPP and IAPP certificates** — International Oil Pollution Prevention and International Air Pollution Prevention certificates issued by the flag state (or its Recognised Organisation) following statutory survey.
- **Annex VI sulphur cap** — global limit on sulphur content in marine fuel oil applies outside ECAs since 1 January 2020 (the IMO 2020 sulphur cap).
- **Energy Efficiency mechanisms** — EEDI (Energy Efficiency Design Index) for new ships, EEXI and CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator) for existing ships under MARPOL Annex VI Chapter 4.
- **Tacit-amendment procedure** — amendments adopted by MEPC enter into force on a fixed schedule unless rejected by a defined number of parties; this is how MARPOL is kept current without re-ratification.
