# MARPOL 73/78 — International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships

## Scope

MARPOL 73/78 is the foundational IMO treaty regulating ship-source pollution. Six annexes partition the regime by pollutant class: oil (Annex I), noxious liquid substances carried in bulk (Annex II), harmful substances in packaged form (Annex III), sewage (Annex IV), garbage (Annex V), and air pollution (Annex VI). The "73/78" naming reflects that the 1978 Protocol absorbed the 1973 Convention before either entered force — they are treated as a single instrument and amended together. MARPOL is the most-amended IMO treaty: the IMO Marine Environment Protection Committee (MEPC) revises the annexes on roughly an annual cadence under the tacit-amendment procedure.

## Adoption + entry-into-force

- **Adopted:** London, 1973-11-02 (Convention) and 1978-02-17 (Protocol).
- **Entry-into-force:** 1983-10-02 (Annexes I and II — the mandatory annexes).
- **Subsequent annexes:** added through 1988–2008 as parties-of-states accumulated to trigger entry-into-force for each optional annex (Annex III in 1992, Annex IV in 2003, Annex V in 1988, Annex VI in 2005 via the 1997 Protocol).
- **States parties:** 160+, covering effectively the entire world fleet by tonnage.

## Six Annexes

Annexes I and II are mandatory for all parties; Annexes III–VI are optional but in practice ratified by virtually all major flag states.

- **Annex I — Oil.** Oily-water separators, Oil Discharge Monitoring and Control System (ODMCS), Oil Record Book, segregated ballast tanks, and the double-hull tanker phase-in (1995–2015) under MEPC.95(46).
- **Annex II — Noxious Liquid Substances in Bulk.** Categorization (X/Y/Z/OS), discharge criteria, and tank-cleaning prewash requirements; tied to the IBC Code for chemical-tanker construction.
- **Annex III — Harmful Substances in Packaged Form.** Packaging, marking, labelling, documentation, and stowage; aligned with the IMDG Code.
- **Annex IV — Sewage from Ships.** Discharge criteria by distance-from-land and treatment-plant performance; Special Areas for sewage (e.g., Baltic).
- **Annex V — Garbage from Ships.** Total prohibition on plastics discharge; Special Areas with stricter regional discharge restrictions; Garbage Record Book and Garbage Management Plan.
- **Annex VI — Air Pollution from Ships.** Sulfur cap of 0.50% globally and 0.10% inside Emission Control Areas (ECAs) since 2020; NOx Tier I/II/III for marine diesel engines; Energy Efficiency Design Index (EEDI) for new builds; Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) operational rating; future amendments include the Mediterranean Sulfur ECA and Arctic Polar Code interactions.

## Notable enforcement / case law

- **Erika (2000)** — TotalFinaElf single-hull tanker breakup off Brittany, France; drove MEPC acceleration of the double-hull phase-in schedule and informed the EU Erika I/II/III packages.
- **Prestige (2002)** — single-hull tanker casualty off Galicia, Spain; accelerated the global single-hull tanker phase-out under MEPC.95(46) and triggered EU Directive 2005/35/EC on ship-source pollution.
- **Hebei Spirit (2007)** — South Korean oil-spill litigation; large-scale Annex I and CLC/IOPC interaction with national criminal-prosecution overlay.
- **The "Volga" (2002)** — ITLOS prompt-release case touching flag-state versus port-state jurisdiction in the broader Part XII enforcement architecture under which MARPOL operates.

- [[api-rp-571]](../../../engineering-standards/wiki/standards/api-rp-571.md) — **bidirectional bridge**: API RP 571 (Damage Mechanisms Affecting Fixed Equipment in the Refining Industry) is the technical-content reference invoked when MARPOL incident investigations and survey non-conformance findings need a mechanism-level metallurgical catalogue for tanker hull, cargo-tank, and slop-tank failures. **Annex I (oil)** and **Annex II (noxious liquid substances in bulk)** inspection regimes — including the IOPP/NLS certificate survey programme and the double-hull tanker fitness-for-service evaluation under MEPC.95(46) — overlap directly with RP 571 entries for sour-water corrosion, CO2 corrosion, sulfide stress cracking, naphthenic-acid corrosion, microbiologically-influenced corrosion (MIC), and erosion-corrosion in cargo-handling and ballast-water circuits. Cargo-tank atmospheric corrosion under Annex I crude-oil-washing residues, Annex II category-X/Y prewash-cycle attack on tank linings, and slop-tank bottom-side MIC are the highest-frequency RP 571 mechanism hits in the casualty record (Erika 2000, Prestige 2002, Hebei Spirit 2007 each involved hull-integrity damage mechanisms catalogued in RP 571). Use the pairing for incident-investigation digests that bridge regulatory non-conformance to mechanism-level evidence.
- [[api-17j]](../../../engineering-standards/wiki/standards/api-17j.md) — **bidirectional bridge**: API Spec 17J's design, materials, fabrication, and integrity-management requirements for **unbonded flexible pipe** in offshore subsea production directly affect oil-pollution-prevention performance of subsea-tieback, FPSO, and FSO systems regulated under **MARPOL Annex I**. The dominant flexible-pipe failure modes — **carcass collapse** (annulus flooding, hydrostatic crush), **tensile-armor wire fracture** (corrosion-fatigue, HISC of duplex wires under cathodic protection per DNV-RP-F112), **end-fitting leakage** (epoxy-resin debonding, vent-port blockage), **outer-sheath rupture** (seawater ingress to annulus, accelerated tensile-armor corrosion), and **pressure-sheath aging** (CO2/H2S permeation, polymer plasticization) — feed pollution-incident root-cause analysis when subsea releases reach the marine environment. The API 17J integrity-management envelope and the MEPC casualty-reporting regime share root-cause-analysis vocabulary through this bridge; industry-loss databases (OGP, IADC, DNV) map flexible-riser failures onto Annex I reportable thresholds. The pairing also feeds **Annex VI** air-pollution accounting where flexible-riser failure triggers blowdown/flaring on host floating production systems. Use the pairing for casualty digests that bridge subsea-system specification non-conformance to MARPOL Annex I and Annex VI consequences.
- [[api-rp-17b]](../../../engineering-standards/wiki/standards/api-rp-17b.md) — **bidirectional bridge**: API RP 17B (Recommended Practice for Flexible Pipe Systems) is the **operations + integrity** sister to API Spec 17J's **design + qualification** envelope; together they bracket the flexible-pipe lifecycle relevant to **MARPOL Annex I** subsea-release reporting. RP 17B governs in-service fitness-for-service, inspection campaigns (annulus testing, vent-port monitoring, MEC/MFL armor-wire inspection), and end-of-design-life retirement decisions for flexible risers, flowlines, jumpers, and dynamic FPSO/FSO connections. The same failure-mode taxonomy that 17J addresses at the design stage (carcass collapse, tensile-armor wire fracture, end-fitting leakage, outer-sheath rupture, pressure-sheath aging) recurs in 17B at the operations stage with field-data-driven retirement criteria. **Casualty causation typically traces to the integrity-management gap, not the design specification** — making RP 17B the operationally salient reference for MARPOL Annex I incident investigations. The pairing also feeds **Annex VI** air-pollution accounting where in-service flexible-riser failure forces host-platform blowdown/flaring. Use the 17B pairing for in-service casualty digests; use the 17J pairing for as-built / qualification-failure digests; both bridges resolve to MARPOL Annex I + VI consequences.
