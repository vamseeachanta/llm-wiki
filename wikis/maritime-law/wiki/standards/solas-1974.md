# SOLAS 1974 — International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea

## Scope

SOLAS is the foundational IMO treaty for the safety of merchant ships at sea. The 1974 instrument is the successor-by-replacement to the original SOLAS adopted in 1914 in response to the *Titanic* loss, and to the intermediate 1929, 1948, and 1960 conventions; it is widely cited as the most important and most-amended of all maritime safety treaties.

It is mandatory for merchant vessels of 500 GT and above engaged on international voyages (with chapter-level exceptions for passenger ships, tankers, bulk carriers, gas carriers, and high-speed craft regardless of tonnage). Coverage spans ship construction (Ch. II-1 subdivision and stability; Ch. II-2 fire protection), life-saving appliances (Ch. III), radiocommunications (Ch. IV — GMDSS), navigation (Ch. V), carriage of cargoes (Ch. VI), dangerous goods (Ch. VII), nuclear ships (Ch. VIII), management for safe operation (Ch. IX — ISM Code mandate), high-speed craft (Ch. X — HSC Code mandate), maritime safety and security (Ch. XI-1 and XI-2 — ISPS Code mandate), bulk carrier safety (Ch. XII), verification of compliance (Ch. XIII — IMSAS), and polar operations (Ch. XIV — Polar Code mandate).

This page is pinned to the **SOLAS Consolidated 2022 (IMO publication)** edition and to the **MSC.521(106) (2022, e-navigation) / MSC.532(108) (2024, cybersecurity)** amendment cohort recorded in `amendment_status`.

## Adoption and entry-into-force

- **Adopted:** London, 1974-11-01, at the IMO International Conference on Safety of Life at Sea.
- **Entry-into-force:** 1980-05-25, after ratification by 25 States representing not less than 50% of world merchant shipping tonnage.
- **Successor relationship:** replaces SOLAS 1948 and SOLAS 1960; the 1914 and 1929 conventions are of historical interest only.
- **Protocols:** the 1978 Protocol (post-*Amoco Cadiz* tanker safety) and the 1988 Protocol (Harmonized System of Survey and Certification — HSSC) are integral and in force.
- **States parties:** 167+ contracting governments covering ~99% of world merchant shipping tonnage as of 2024.

## Amendment regime

SOLAS evolves through the IMO Maritime Safety Committee (MSC) under a **tacit-acceptance procedure**: amendments adopted by MSC enter into force on a defined date unless objected to by a specified fraction of contracting governments. This explains the velocity of SOLAS revision relative to other treaty regimes and is the reason `amendment_status` here is recorded at MSC-resolution granularity rather than as a linear edition number.

| Amendment / protocol | Year adopted | Year in force | Summary |
|----------------------|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Protocol of 1978 | 1978 | 1981 | Tanker safety; inert-gas, steering-gear, and survey upgrades after *Amoco Cadiz*. |
| Protocol of 1988 (HSSC) | 1988 | 2000 | Harmonized System of Survey and Certification across SOLAS, MARPOL, Load Lines. |
| Ch. IX (ISM Code mandate) | 1994 | 1998/2002 | Made the ISM Code mandatory in two phases (passenger/tanker first, then cargo). |
| Ch. XI-2 (ISPS Code mandate) | 2002 | 2004 | Maritime security regime adopted post-9/11; ISPS Code made mandatory. |
| Ch. XII (bulk carriers) | 1997+2004 | 1999+2007 | Additional structural and damage-stability rules after *Derbyshire* and successor losses. |

| MSC.521(106) | 2022 | 2024 | E-navigation and modernization-of-GMDSS amendments. |

## Chapters

Chapter *titles only* — Regulation text lives at the publisher catalog (link below).

- **Chapter I — General Provisions.** Application, definitions, surveys, certificates, port-state control on operational requirements.
- **Chapter II-1 — Construction (Subdivision and Stability, Machinery and Electrical Installations).** Damage stability, watertight subdivision, machinery redundancy.
- **Chapter II-2 — Construction (Fire Protection, Fire Detection and Fire Extinction).** Structural fire protection, detection systems, fixed extinguishing systems.
- **Chapter III — Life-Saving Appliances and Arrangements.** Survival craft, lifeboats, evacuation systems, drills.
- **Chapter IV — Radiocommunications.** Carries the **GMDSS (Global Maritime Distress and Safety System)** mandate.
- **Chapter V — Safety of Navigation.** Carriage of nautical charts, voyage data recorders (VDR), AIS, LRIT, ECDIS, e-navigation.
- **Chapter VI — Carriage of Cargoes and Oil Fuels.** Grain Code, Cargo Securing Manuals, IMSBC Code applicability for solid bulk cargoes.
- **Chapter VII — Carriage of Dangerous Goods.** Mandatory **IMDG Code**, **IBC Code** (chemical tankers), and **IGC Code** (gas carriers).
- **Chapter VIII — Nuclear Ships.**
- **Chapter IX — Management for the Safe Operation of Ships.** Mandatory **ISM Code** (Safety Management System, Designated Person Ashore).
- **Chapter X — Safety Measures for High-Speed Craft.** Mandatory **HSC Code**.
- **Chapter XI-1 — Special Measures to Enhance Maritime Safety.** Authorization of recognized organizations, enhanced surveys, IMO ship identification numbers.
- **Chapter XI-2 — Special Measures to Enhance Maritime Security.** Mandatory **ISPS Code** (post-9/11); ship and port-facility security plans, Ship Security Officer, Continuous Synopsis Record.
- **Chapter XII — Additional Safety Measures for Bulk Carriers.** Damage-stability and structural strength for bulk carriers ≥150 m.
- **Chapter XIII — Verification of Compliance.** Mandatory IMO Member State Audit Scheme (IMSAS).
- **Chapter XIV — Safety Measures for Ships Operating in Polar Waters.** Mandatory **Polar Code**.

## Major SOLAS-driven Codes

SOLAS is the parent instrument for an extensive system of subordinate IMO codes. Each is mandatory under the chapter cited and is itself a candidate for a dedicated standards-page in the relevant wiki domain:

- **IGC Code** — International Code for the Construction and Equipment of Ships Carrying Liquefied Gases in Bulk; mandatory under Ch. VII for gas carriers (LNG, LPG, ethylene, ammonia). Already first-class at [`lng-projects/wiki/standards/igc-code.md`](../../../lng-projects/wiki/standards/igc-code.md).
- **IBC Code** — International Code for the Construction and Equipment of Ships Carrying Dangerous Chemicals in Bulk; mandatory under Ch. VII for chemical tankers.
- **ISM Code** — International Safety Management Code; mandatory under Ch. IX for company-side and ship-side safety management.
- **ISPS Code** — International Ship and Port Facility Security Code; mandatory under Ch. XI-2.
- **IGF Code** — International Code of Safety for Ships Using Gases or Other Low-Flashpoint Fuels; mandatory under Ch. II-1 Part G for LNG- and methanol-fuelled vessels.
- **HSC Code** — High-Speed Craft Code (1994 and 2000 editions); mandatory under Ch. X.
- **Polar Code** — International Code for Ships Operating in Polar Waters; mandatory under Ch. XIV (and under MARPOL for environmental provisions).
- **IMDG Code** — International Maritime Dangerous Goods Code; mandatory under Ch. VII for packaged dangerous goods.
- **IMSBC Code** — International Maritime Solid Bulk Cargoes Code; mandatory under Ch. VI for solid bulk cargoes.

## Notable enforcement / case law and incident-driven amendments

SOLAS evolves substantially through a casualty-then-amendment loop; the most-cited incidents in this loop include:

- **Estonia 1994** — Roll-on/roll-off ferry sinking in the Baltic Sea with 852 fatalities; drove SOLAS Ch. II-1 damage-stability amendments and Ch. III life-saving-appliance amendments for ro-pax vessels (the "Stockholm Agreement" regional baseline preceded the global IMO uptake).
- **Costa Concordia 2012** — Italian flag passenger ship grounding off Isola del Giglio with 32 fatalities; drove the IMO "Costa Concordia Lessons Learned" review and resulting Ch. II-1 amendments on emergency lighting, damage-control drills, and passenger musters.
- **Sewol 2014** — Republic of Korea ferry capsize with 304 fatalities (mostly schoolchildren); drove regional and cross-flag domestic amendments and reinforced ISM-Code (Ch. IX) audit scrutiny on stability-data accuracy and crew training.
- **Erika 1999 / Prestige 2002** — Although primarily MARPOL/CLC casualties, both reinforced SOLAS Ch. II-1 enhanced-survey and Ch. XI-1 recognized-organization audit chains.
