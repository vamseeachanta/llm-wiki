# UNCLOS 1982 — UN Convention on the Law of the Sea

## Scope

UNCLOS is the foundational treaty governing all uses of the world's oceans and their resources. It defines maritime-zone rights (territorial sea, contiguous zone, exclusive economic zone, continental shelf, high seas, and the Area), navigation freedoms (innocent passage, transit passage, archipelagic-sea-lanes passage), the protection and preservation of the marine environment (Part XII), marine scientific research (Part XIII), the development and transfer of marine technology (Part XIV), and a comprehensive dispute-settlement regime (Part XV, including ITLOS and Annex VII tribunals).

It is widely referred to as the **"constitution for the oceans"** and is cited as authority by virtually every other maritime treaty — SOLAS, MARPOL, MLC, STCW, CLC, LLMC, the Salvage Convention, and the various carriage-of-goods regimes all rest on the maritime-zone and flag-state architecture set by UNCLOS.

## Adoption and entry-into-force

- **Adopted:** Montego Bay, Jamaica, 1982-12-10.
- **Entry-into-force:** 1994-11-16, after the 60th ratification (Guyana).
- **States parties:** 168+ as of 2024.
- **Notable non-parties:** the United States has signed (1994 agreement) but not ratified the convention itself; US executive practice nevertheless recognizes most provisions as reflective of customary international law.

## Amendment history

| Amendment | Year | Subject |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Part XI Implementation Agreement | 1994 | Modifies seabed-mining provisions to enable widespread Western (industrialized-state) ratification; treated as integral to the convention. |
| UN Fish Stocks Agreement | 1995 | Implementation of UNCLOS provisions on conservation and management of straddling and highly-migratory fish stocks. |

A standalone amendment to the convention text itself has not entered into force; the regime evolves chiefly through implementing agreements, ITLOS jurisprudence, and Annex VII arbitral awards.

## Key Parts

Section *titles only* — full text lives at the publisher catalog (link below).

- **Part I — Introduction.** Use of terms, scope.
- **Part II — Territorial Sea and Contiguous Zone.** 12-nm territorial sea; innocent passage; 24-nm contiguous zone for customs/fiscal/immigration/sanitary enforcement.
- **Part III — Straits Used for International Navigation.** Transit-passage regime.
- **Part IV — Archipelagic States.** Archipelagic baselines; archipelagic-sea-lanes passage.
- **Part V — Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ, 200 nm).** Coastal-state sovereign rights over living and non-living resources; navigation freedoms preserved for other states.
- **Part VI — Continental Shelf.** Sovereign rights for exploration and exploitation; CLCS submission process for outer-limit claims beyond 200 nm.
- **Part VII — High Seas.** Freedom of navigation, overflight, fishing, cable-laying, scientific research, construction of artificial islands.
- **Part VIII — Regime of Islands.** Article 121 — distinction between rocks (no EEZ/continental-shelf entitlement) and islands; central to the South China Sea Award.
- **Part IX — Enclosed and Semi-Enclosed Seas.** Cooperation duties (e.g., Mediterranean, Caribbean, Persian Gulf, South China Sea).
- **Part X — Land-Locked States.** Right of access to and from the sea; freedom of transit.
- **Part XI — The Area.** Deep-seabed beyond national jurisdiction; common heritage of mankind; International Seabed Authority (ISA).
- **Part XII — Protection and Preservation of the Marine Environment.** General obligation to protect; basis for MARPOL, OPRC, BWM, AFS, and London Convention frameworks.
- **Part XIII — Marine Scientific Research.** Coastal-state consent regime in EEZ/continental shelf.
- **Part XIV — Development and Transfer of Marine Technology.**
- **Part XV — Settlement of Disputes.** Choice-of-procedure (Article 287): ITLOS, ICJ, Annex VII arbitral tribunal, Annex VIII special-arbitration tribunal.
- **Annexes I–IX.** Highly-migratory species; ITLOS statute; CLCS; arbitration; conciliation; ISA; Enterprise; etc.

## Case law citing UNCLOS

- **South China Sea Arbitration (Philippines v. China), 2016** — Annex VII tribunal under Article 287 / Annex VII; rejected China's nine-dash-line as incompatible with UNCLOS; applied the Article 121 rocks-vs-islands distinction at scale.
- **Chagos Marine Protected Area Arbitration (Mauritius v. UK), 2015** — Annex VII tribunal on EEZ rights of dependent territories; later reinforced by the ICJ Chagos Advisory Opinion 2019.
- **The "Volga" (Russia v. Australia), 2002** — ITLOS prompt-release; flag-state jurisdiction and bond reasonableness under Article 73(2).
- **The M/V "Saiga" (No. 2) (Saint Vincent and the Grenadines v. Guinea), 1999** — ITLOS judgment on hot-pursuit limits and EEZ enforcement; held the genuine-link requirement does not authorize other states to refuse to recognize the flag.
- **Bay of Bengal Maritime Boundary cases** — *Bangladesh v. Myanmar* (ITLOS, 2012) and *Bangladesh v. India* (Annex VII tribunal, 2014); continental-shelf delimitation beyond 200 nm.
