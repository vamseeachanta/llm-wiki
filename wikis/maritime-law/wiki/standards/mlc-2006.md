# MLC 2006 — Maritime Labour Convention

## Scope

The Maritime Labour Convention 2006 is the **International Labour Organization** consolidated convention establishing a comprehensive seafarer-labor-rights regime covering recruitment, employment conditions, accommodation, food and catering, health protection and medical care, social security, and welfare. Compliance is verified through mandatory port-state-control inspections backed by the **Maritime Labour Certificate** plus the two-part **Declaration of Maritime Labour Compliance (DMLC Parts I and II)**.

MLC 2006 is widely referred to as **"the fourth pillar"** of international maritime regulation, sitting alongside SOLAS (safety of life at sea), MARPOL (pollution prevention), and STCW (training, certification, watchkeeping). Unlike the other three pillars — adopted under the IMO — MLC originates in the ILO, a distinct UN-system body whose tripartite structure (governments, shipowners' representatives, seafarers' representatives) governs the convention's amendment cycle.

## Adoption + entry-into-force

- **Adopted:** Geneva, at the 94th (Maritime) Session of the International Labour Conference, 2006-02-23.
- **Entry-into-force:** 2013-08-20, twelve months after the threshold of **30 ILO member states representing at least 33 per cent of world gross tonnage** was met.
- **States parties:** 100+ as of 2024, covering more than **90 per cent of world tonnage**, making MLC one of the most widely-implemented maritime instruments by tonnage coverage.
- **Tripartite governance:** the Special Tripartite Committee (STC) under the ILO Governing Body negotiates amendments; this cadence is independent of IMO MSC/MEPC sessions.

## Five Titles

The convention is structured as Articles + Regulations + a two-part Code (Part A mandatory standards, Part B non-mandatory guidelines), grouped into five Titles:

- **Title 1 — Minimum Requirements for Seafarers to Work on a Ship.** Minimum age, medical certification, training and qualification, recruitment and placement services.
- **Title 2 — Conditions of Employment.** Seafarers' Employment Agreement (SEA), wages, hours of work and hours of rest, entitlement to leave, repatriation, seafarer compensation for the ship's loss or foundering, manning levels, career and skill development and opportunities for employment.
- **Title 3 — Accommodation, Recreational Facilities, Food and Catering.** Onboard living conditions, recreational space, food provisioning, and catering standards.
- **Title 4 — Health Protection, Medical Care, Welfare and Social Security Protection.** Medical care on board and ashore, shipowner's liability, health and safety protection and accident prevention, access to shore-based welfare facilities, social security.
- **Title 5 — Compliance and Enforcement.** Flag-state responsibilities, port-state-control inspection, labour-supplying-state responsibilities, complaint-handling procedures.

## Key concepts

- **Maritime Labour Certificate (MLC):** required onboard ships of **500 GT or more** engaged in international voyages or operating from a port (or between ports) in another country; valid for up to **5 years**; issued by the flag state (or a recognized organization on its behalf) and subject to port-state-control inspection.
- **DMLC Part I:** the flag state's statement of national requirements implementing the convention's 14 inspection areas.
- **DMLC Part II:** the shipowner's statement of measures adopted to ensure ongoing compliance with the Part I requirements between inspections.
- **Substantial equivalence:** flag states may adopt national provisions that are *substantially equivalent* to Code Part A standards, rather than literal compliance, where strict literal implementation is impracticable.
- **Tacit acceptance:** Code amendments enter into force on a defined date unless a sufficient number of ratifying states formally object — the same rapid-amendment regime used by SOLAS and MARPOL Annexes, allowing the convention to evolve at industry pace without re-ratification.

## Major amendments

| Year | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 2014 | Financial security for repatriation and abandonment of seafarers (in force 2017) — mandatory shipowner financial-security certificate covering repatriation costs and outstanding wages where a seafarer is abandoned. |
| 2016 | Anti-harassment and anti-bullying provisions; clarifications to medical-certificate validity. |
| 2018 | Validity-period clarifications; requirement to keep onboard certified copies of MLC and DMLC documents. |
| 2022 | Anti-shore-leave-restriction provisions in the post-COVID environment; recognition of seafarers as key workers; expanded financial-security obligations. |

The 2022 amendments entered into force on 2024-12-23 under the tacit-acceptance procedure and are reflected in the **MLC Consolidated 2022** edition that this page is pinned to.

**Implementing-regulation national codes** *(illustrative, not exhaustive)*
