# LNG Project Shapes

## Scope

This page defines the canonical project-shape taxonomy and the terminology that distinguishes liquefaction, regasification, and storage-only configurations. It does **not** name or characterize any specific facility; it stays at the abstract project-shape level.

## Key Concepts

- **Onshore greenfield** — new-build liquefaction plant on a previously undeveloped site; capacity typically 3.0–8.0 MTPA per train
- **Onshore brownfield** — capacity addition adjacent to an existing plant; typically reuses common marine and utility infrastructure
- **FLNG (Floating Liquefied Natural Gas)** — vessel-mounted liquefaction; canonical capacity range 1.0–2.0 MTPA; relocatable
- **FSRU (Floating Storage and Regasification Unit)** — vessel-mounted regasification of imported LNG; faster to deploy than onshore regas
- **Mid-scale modular** — pre-fabricated trains in the 0.5–1.5 MTPA per train range; favored for first-cargo speed
- **Small-scale LNG (SS-LNG)** — bunkering, virtual pipeline, and remote-load distribution
- **Terminology boundary** — "LNG terminal" typically refers to receiving / regasification; "LNG plant" typically refers to liquefaction; "LNG facility" is a catch-all and should be qualified
