# LNG Liquefaction Processes

## Scope

This page surveys the liquefaction process families licensed by the major industrial-gas and oil-major licensors. It is a neutral overview only; it does **not** rank technologies, enumerate per-train MTPA capacity caps, or restate proprietary process diagrams. Vendor names appear as canonical industry references, not endorsements.

## Key Concepts

- **APCI C3-MR (propane pre-cooled mixed refrigerant)** — Air Products & Chemicals Inc. workhorse for large baseload trains
- **APCI AP-X** — APCI variant adding a nitrogen sub-cooling loop, targeting larger train capacities
- **ConocoPhillips Optimized Cascade** — three pure-component refrigerant loops (propane, ethylene, methane); favored for parallel-train modular designs
- **Shell Dual Mixed Refrigerant (DMR)** — two mixed-refrigerant loops; used in cold-climate baseload projects
- **Linde Mixed Fluid Cascade (MFC)** — three mixed-refrigerant loops; used in select onshore and floating projects
- **Single Mixed Refrigerant (SMR)** — single-loop variants (e.g. Black & Veatch PRICO) commonly used for mid-scale and FLNG service
- **Non-Western FLNG hulls** — e.g. Wison Offshore & Marine and CSSC Offshore & Marine Engineering have delivered FLNG/FSRU hulls; relevant for licensor-vs-hull distinction in capability surveys

## Standards / References

- [NFPA 59A](../standards/nfpa-59a.md) — design code for the liquefaction-train cold box, refrigerant containment, and onshore plant siting
- [FERC 18 CFR 153](../standards/ferc-18-cfr-153.md) — US LNG siting authority for liquefaction export terminals (Resource Reports + NEPA)
- [PHMSA 49 CFR 193](../standards/phmsa-49-cfr-193.md) — US LNG facility safety operations governing liquefaction plants in service
- IACS class rules for gas carriers and floating LNG units (process containment interfaces): <https://iacs.org.uk/>
- IGC Code (IMO) for marine carriage interfaces: <https://www.imo.org/en/OurWork/Safety/Pages/IGC-Code.aspx>
