# LNG Marine Transfer Systems

## Scope

This page surveys the marine-side transfer interfaces between LNG carriers, jetties, FSRUs, and FLNG units. It does **not** restate jetty design clauses, mooring-load envelopes, or specific environmental operability thresholds; those are reserved for standards-page or marine-engineering pages. Tandem mooring is named here by reference to OCIMF MEG4 and is fully covered in the engineering wiki — this page does not duplicate that content.

## Key Concepts

- **Conventional jetty + marine loading arms** — fixed berth with hard-arm (Chiksan-style) loading; the dominant onshore configuration
- **Ship-to-ship transfer (STS)** — side-by-side carrier-to-FSRU or carrier-to-carrier transfer; constrained by sea-state and fender envelope
- **Floating storage and regasification unit (FSRU)** — moored vessel that regasifies cargo into a downstream pipeline
- **FLNG offloading** — typically side-by-side at sheltered locations or tandem at exposed locations
- **Tandem mooring** — bow-to-stern offloading geometry; named by reference to OCIMF MEG4 and other industry guidance, not restated here
- **Marine breakaway couplings (MBC)** — emergency-release devices on transfer hoses
- **Quick-connect/disconnect couplers (QCDC)** — manifold-side couplings for STS and offshore service
