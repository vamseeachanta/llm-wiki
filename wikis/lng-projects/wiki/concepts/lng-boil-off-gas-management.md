# LNG Boil-Off Gas Management

## Scope

This page summarizes how boil-off gas (BOG) is generated, routed, and either reliquefied or consumed across LNG plants, storage, and marine assets. It does **not** restate BOG-rate design tables, compressor sizing curves, or specific make-up gas balances; those belong in vendor-specific or standards-page material.

## Key Concepts

- **Boil-off generation** — heat ingress through tank insulation, pump churn, and flash on filling produces a continuous vapor stream
- **Forced vs. natural BOG** — natural BOG tracks heat leak; forced BOG is added vapor produced to satisfy fuel-gas demand
- **BOG compressors** — reciprocating or centrifugal machines that raise BOG pressure for reliquefaction or fuel-gas use
- **Reliquefaction** — small refrigeration loop that returns vapor to the storage tank as liquid; common on LNG carriers and FLNG
- **Fuel-gas use** — BOG combusted in dual-fuel carrier propulsion or in plant utilities
- **Vapor return** — line that returns displaced vapor from receiving tank to the source vessel during transfer
- **Flaring** — last-resort vapor disposal during upset or commissioning
