# LNG Project Lifecycle

## Scope

This page summarizes the canonical phase taxonomy used by LNG project developers and EPC contractors from initial conceptualization through operations. It is project-agnostic — no facility-specific narrative — and does **not** enumerate gate criteria, sanction thresholds, or commercial terms; those belong in either the regulatory framework page or in project-bound source pages.

## Key Concepts

- **Conceptualization / Pre-FEED** — opportunity framing, capacity sizing, technology screening, location screening, order-of-magnitude cost (Class 5 / Class 4 estimates)
- **FEED (Front-End Engineering Design)** — typically 12–18 months; produces a Class 3 estimate, a frozen process basis of design, and the basis for FID
- **FID (Final Investment Decision)** — sanction milestone; commits offtake, financing, and EPC contract
- **EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction)** — lump-sum or convertible LSTK execution; multi-year duration scaled to capacity and shape (FLNG vs. onshore)
- **Commissioning and Startup** — mechanical completion, ready-for-startup, first cargo
- **Operations** — steady-state production; major turnarounds typically every 4–6 years
- **Decommissioning** — covered in a future dedicated page; abandonment-in-place vs. removal driven by host-government regime
