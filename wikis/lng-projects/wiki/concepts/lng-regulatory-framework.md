# LNG Regulatory Framework

## Scope

This page is a synthesis of the standards-publishing bodies and regulatory authorities that bind LNG project design, construction, and operation across the four interlocking layers — international maritime, jurisdictional onshore, classification societies, and industry-association best practice. It does **not** restate clause numbers, threshold values, or the relative authority of bodies in any given jurisdiction; that determination is project-specific and is captured on the corresponding standards-page entries linked below. This page also does not include the small-scale LNG-as-fuel ship-side framework (IGF Code) at depth — that is a sibling-page candidate.

## Why a layered framework matters

- **Onshore vs. ship-side authority split** — onshore LNG facilities are governed by national jurisdictional standards (NFPA 59A, EN 1473, CSA Z276) plus the corresponding regulators (PHMSA, national competent authorities, FERC for US export terminals). LNG carriers are governed by the IMO IGC Code and class-society type approval. Both layers apply at the ship-shore interface and project teams must clear both gates.
- **Classification societies as design-approval surface** — IACS member class societies (ABS, DNV, BV, LR, KR, ClassNK, RINA, RS, CCS, IRClass, PRS, CRS) review and stamp the design package for any floating LNG hull or gas carrier. Class approval is a precondition for IMO flag-state certification and is integrated into newbuild contracts.
- **Best-practice publishers as gap-fillers** — SIGTTO, OCIMF, and EEMUA publish operational guidance that fills gaps left by code-level standards. These are not enforceable on their own but are routinely incorporated by reference into terminal contracts, charter parties, and TUO/TMSA assurance regimes.
- **Methane-emission and ESG layer** — atmospheric-methane regulation (EU Methane Regulation 2024/1787, US EPA Subpart W, IMO MARPOL Annex VI methane provisions) is now a parallel layer on top of the safety-and-design framework above; methane-intensity reporting affects market access for LNG cargoes into the EU.

## Onshore-jurisdictional standards bodies

- **NFPA (National Fire Protection Association, US)** — publisher of [NFPA 59A](../standards/nfpa-59a.md), the US-jurisdiction primary standard for LNG production, storage, and handling. Adopted by reference into PHMSA 49 CFR 193 with edition-pinning.
- **CEN / CENELEC (Europe)** — publisher of [EN 1473](../standards/en-1473.md), the European harmonized standard for onshore LNG installation and equipment. EN 1473 allows risk-based methodology with peer review, distinct from NFPA 59A's deterministic methodology.
- **CSA Group (Canada)** — publisher of [CSA Z276](../standards/csa-z276.md), the Canadian national standard for LNG production, storage, and handling — NFPA 59A's cross-border parallel.
- **API (American Petroleum Institute, US)** — publisher of [API Std 625](../standards/api-std-625.md), tank systems for refrigerated liquefied gas storage; companion design code referenced by NFPA 59A and CSA Z276 for tank-system mechanical design.
- **GIIGNL (International Group of LNG Importers)** — industry-association publisher of the LNG Custody Transfer Handbook and LNG Information Paper series; widely used for cargo-quality, custody-transfer, and emission-reporting practice.

## International maritime framework

- **IMO (International Maritime Organization)** — UN agency that publishes the [IGC Code](../standards/igc-code.md) (international gas carrier construction and equipment), the [IBC Code](../standards/ibc-code.md) (chemical-tanker companion), the IGF Code (low-flashpoint ship's-fuel), and SOLAS (parent convention for the IGC Code as Chapter VII Part C subordinate code). IMO instruments are implemented through flag-state administration and port-state control.
- **MARPOL Annex VI** — air-pollution provisions including methane-related amendments developed under MEPC, addressing shipboard methane emissions and methane slip from gas-fueled engines.
- **MEPC (Marine Environment Protection Committee)** — IMO subcommittee responsible for environmental amendments including methane-slip, EEDI/EEXI/CII, and greenhouse-gas accounting frameworks affecting LNG carriers and LNG-as-fuel ships.

## Classification societies and IACS

- **IACS (International Association of Classification Societies)** — coordinating body for class rules covering gas carriers and floating LNG units. Publishes Unified Requirements (URs) that member societies adopt into their own rulesets.
- **Major class societies** — ABS, DNV, BV, LR, KR, ClassNK, RINA, CCS, RS, IRClass, PRS, CRS. Each publishes class rules for gas carriers and floating LNG units (FSRU, FLNG) with specific notations (e.g. ABS Gas Carrier Notation, DNV LNG Carrier class notation).
- **Type-approval surface** — class societies are the working-level interface for cargo-containment-system type approval under the IGC Code. GTT membrane-system qualification, sloshing-program updates, and material substitutions all flow through class-society review under IACS coordination.
- **FLNG and FSRU class notations** — separate notations and rules apply for offshore production and regasification units; class-society guidance fills the gap between IGC (carrier-side) and onshore design codes.

## Industry-association best-practice publishers

- **SIGTTO (Society of International Gas Tanker and Terminal Operators)** — publisher of operational guidance for LNG/LPG terminals and carriers. Coverage spans mooring equipment ([SIGTTO Mooring Equipment Guidelines](../standards/sigtto-mooring-equipment.md)), ship-shore interface, vapor-handling, ESD, transfer system safety, manifold arrangements, and incident lessons-learned.
- **OCIMF (Oil Companies International Marine Forum)** — publisher of MEG4 (mooring equipment), TMSA (tanker management self-assessment), JIP/MTQ (terminal management), and ship-inspection guidance (SIRE 2.0). MEG4 is the marine-mooring companion to SIGTTO mooring guidance.
- **EEMUA (Engineering Equipment and Materials Users Association)** — publisher of Pub 147 (in-service inspection of LNG storage tanks) and Pub 159 (inspection of above-ground storage tanks); widely used for terminal-tank in-service inspection planning.
- **API standards (beyond Std 625)** — API 521 (relief and depressuring systems), API 2000 (atmospheric and low-pressure storage tank venting), and API 14C (offshore safety systems) are routinely referenced into LNG facility and FLNG design.
- **ASME boiler and pressure vessel code** — Section VIII (pressure vessels) and B31.3 (process piping) referenced into LNG plant mechanical design alongside the LNG-specific codes.

## Atmospheric-methane and ESG regulation

- **EU Methane Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2024/1787)** — adopted 2024; mandates leak-detection-and-repair (LDAR) programs, restricts routine venting and flaring, and applies methane-intensity reporting to LNG imports into the EU. Affects market access for LNG cargoes from non-EU producers into EU regasification terminals.
- **US EPA Subpart W (40 CFR 98)** — Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program for petroleum and natural-gas systems; requires methane-emissions reporting for LNG facilities.
- **PHMSA, state regulators, air-permit programs** — address LNG-facility venting and flaring under siting and air-permit programs in the US.
- **IMO MARPOL Annex VI** — methane provisions affecting LNG carriers and LNG-as-fuel ships; methane-slip from dual-fuel engines is the primary regulatory target.
