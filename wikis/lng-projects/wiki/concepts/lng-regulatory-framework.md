# LNG Regulatory Framework

## Scope

This page is a synthesis of the standards-publishing bodies and regulatory authorities that bind LNG project design, construction, and operation across the four interlocking layers — international maritime, jurisdictional onshore, classification societies, and industry-association best practice. It does **not** restate clause numbers, threshold values, or the relative authority of bodies in any given jurisdiction; that determination is project-specific and is captured on the corresponding standards-page entries linked below. This page also does not include the small-scale LNG-as-fuel ship-side framework (IGF Code) at depth — that is a sibling-page candidate.

## Why a layered framework matters

- **Onshore vs. ship-side authority split** — onshore LNG facilities are governed by national jurisdictional standards (NFPA 59A, EN 1473, CSA Z276) plus the corresponding regulators (PHMSA, national competent authorities, FERC for US export terminals). LNG carriers are governed by the IMO IGC Code and class-society type approval. Both layers apply at the ship-shore interface and project teams must clear both gates.
- **Classification societies as design-approval surface** — IACS member class societies (ABS, DNV, BV, LR, KR, ClassNK, RINA, RS, CCS, IRClass, PRS, CRS) review and stamp the design package for any floating LNG hull or gas carrier. Class approval is a precondition for IMO flag-state certification and is integrated into newbuild contracts.
- **Best-practice publishers as gap-fillers** — SIGTTO, OCIMF, and EEMUA publish operational guidance that fills gaps left by code-level standards. These are not enforceable on their own but are routinely incorporated by reference into terminal contracts, charter parties, and TUO/TMSA assurance regimes.
- **Methane-emission and ESG layer** — atmospheric-methane regulation (EU Methane Regulation 2024/1787, US EPA Subpart W, IMO MARPOL Annex VI methane provisions) is now a parallel layer on top of the safety-and-design framework above; methane-intensity reporting affects market access for LNG cargoes into the EU.

## Four-layer architecture multi-criteria comparison

| Layer | Authority basis | Enforceability | Primary scope | Typical project touchpoint |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Onshore-jurisdictional (NFPA 59A / EN 1473 / CSA Z276 / API 625) | national regulator adoption | binding via national law / regulation | onshore liquefaction, storage, regas, peak-shaving | siting + permitting + EPC + commissioning + ops |
| International maritime (IMO IGC / IBC / IGF / SOLAS / MARPOL) | UN treaty (SOLAS); flag-state administration | binding via flag-state and port-state control | gas carriers; FLNG; FSRU; LNG-as-fuel ships | design + class survey + flag-state certification + port entry |
| IACS classification (ABS / DNV / BV / LR / KR / ClassNK / RINA / RS / CCS / IRClass) | classification contract; IACS Unified Requirements | binding via newbuild contract + class survey | hull, machinery, cargo containment type approval | design review + steel-cutting + commissioning + 5-yearly survey |
| Industry-association best practice (SIGTTO / OCIMF / EEMUA / GIIGNL / API beyond 625) | voluntary; routinely incorporated by reference | binding via contract reference (terminal, charter, lender) | operational guidance; gap-filling | terminal procedures + ship-vetting (SIRE) + insurance review |

## Onshore-jurisdictional standards bodies

- **NFPA (National Fire Protection Association, US)** — publisher of [NFPA 59A](../standards/nfpa-59a.md), the US-jurisdiction primary standard for LNG production, storage, and handling. Adopted by reference into PHMSA 49 CFR 193 with edition-pinning.
- **CEN / CENELEC (Europe)** — publisher of [EN 1473](../standards/en-1473.md), the European harmonized standard for onshore LNG installation and equipment. EN 1473 allows risk-based methodology with peer review, distinct from NFPA 59A's deterministic methodology.
- **CSA Group (Canada)** — publisher of [CSA Z276](../standards/csa-z276.md), the Canadian national standard for LNG production, storage, and handling — NFPA 59A's cross-border parallel.
- **API (American Petroleum Institute, US)** — publisher of [API Std 625](../standards/api-std-625.md), tank systems for refrigerated liquefied gas storage; companion design code referenced by NFPA 59A and CSA Z276 for tank-system mechanical design.
- **GIIGNL (International Group of LNG Importers)** — industry-association publisher of the LNG Custody Transfer Handbook and LNG Information Paper series; widely used for cargo-quality, custody-transfer, and emission-reporting practice.
- **Permit + EIA worked examples** —
  - *FERC siting (US)* — Sabine Pass: FERC 18 CFR 153 Resource Reports + NEPA Environmental Impact Statement led to siting order; subsequent train additions handled via siting amendment under the same parent docket. Canonical reference for the US export-terminal pathway.
  - *UK BEIS / DESNZ + HSE COMAH* — UK LNG facilities (e.g., Isle of Grain, South Hook, Dragon LNG) clear a stack of land-use planning (NSIP / Development Consent Order via the Planning Inspectorate), HSE Control of Major Accident Hazards (COMAH) assessment, and environmental permitting through the Environment Agency.
  - *Canada CEAA / IAA* — the federal Impact Assessment Act (formerly CEAA) governs environmental review for designated projects; provincial authorities (BC EAO for British Columbia LNG projects such as LNG Canada and Cedar LNG) parallel-track the provincial-jurisdiction review.
  - *Australia EPBC Act + state regimes* — federal Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act assessment plus state-level regimes (WA EPA for North West Shelf and Pluto, Northern Territory for Ichthys, Queensland for Curtis Island projects). The cumulative-impact assessment for clustered Curtis Island LNG (QCLNG, GLNG, APLNG) is a canonical reference for multi-project EIA coordination.
  - *Mozambique / Senegal / Mauritania* — host-government authorizations under petroleum-sector legislation (Mozambique INP, Senegal/Mauritania COS-A) for onshore-tied components of FLNG developments; environmental approvals through host-country environment ministries.

## International maritime framework

- **IMO (International Maritime Organization)** — UN agency that publishes the [IGC Code](../standards/igc-code.md) (international gas carrier construction and equipment), the [IBC Code](../standards/ibc-code.md) (chemical-tanker companion), the IGF Code (low-flashpoint ship's-fuel), and SOLAS (parent convention for the IGC Code as Chapter VII Part C subordinate code). IMO instruments are implemented through flag-state administration and port-state control.
- **SOLAS Chapter VII Part C** — the parent SOLAS chapter under which the IGC Code operates as a subordinate mandatory code; Part A (packaged dangerous goods), Part B (solid bulk dangerous goods), and Part C (liquefied gases in bulk) make the SOLAS-IGC linkage explicit. SOLAS Chapter II-2 (fire safety) further governs LNG-as-fuel ships under the IGF Code linkage.
- **MARPOL Annex VI** — air-pollution provisions including methane-related amendments developed under MEPC, addressing shipboard methane emissions and methane slip from gas-fueled engines. MEPC.346(78) (2022) introduced the energy-efficiency framework affecting CII / EEXI calculations for LNG carriers.
- **MEPC (Marine Environment Protection Committee)** — IMO subcommittee responsible for environmental amendments including methane-slip, EEDI/EEXI/CII, and greenhouse-gas accounting frameworks affecting LNG carriers and LNG-as-fuel ships. Future MEPC outputs on lifecycle GHG and on-board carbon-capture potential are tracked in lender ESG due-diligence.
- **Flag-state administration** — Panama, Marshall Islands, Liberia, Bahamas, Singapore, Hong Kong dominate LNG-carrier flags; the flag administration delegates statutory survey to recognized organizations (typically IACS class societies). Port-state control (Paris MoU, Tokyo MoU, USCG) inspects compliance at terminal arrival.

## Classification societies and IACS

- **IACS (International Association of Classification Societies)** — coordinating body for class rules covering gas carriers and floating LNG units. Publishes Unified Requirements (URs) that member societies adopt into their own rulesets. IACS members hold ~95% of the world fleet by tonnage; recognition by IMO is via the IMO Code for Recognized Organizations.
- **Major class societies** — ABS, DNV, BV, LR, KR, ClassNK, RINA, CCS, RS, IRClass, PRS, CRS. Each publishes class rules for gas carriers and floating LNG units (FSRU, FLNG) with specific notations (e.g. ABS Gas Carrier Notation, DNV LNG Carrier class notation, BV "Liquefied gas carrier" service notation).
- **Type-approval surface** — class societies are the working-level interface for cargo-containment-system type approval under the IGC Code. GTT membrane-system qualification, sloshing-program updates, and material substitutions all flow through class-society review under IACS coordination.
- **FLNG and FSRU class notations** — separate notations and rules apply for offshore production and regasification units; class-society guidance fills the gap between IGC (carrier-side) and onshore design codes. ABS Floating Liquefied Natural Gas (FLNG) Guide, DNV Offshore Standard DNV-OS-C103, BV NR542 cover the FLNG-specific design-approval surface.
- **Sloshing and motion analysis** — sloshing-load assessment for membrane-tank FLNG and partial-fill operation flows through class-society sloshing programs (GTT method, model-test campaigns, CFD validation) with IACS UR coordination on acceptable methodology.

## Industry-association best-practice publishers

- **SIGTTO (Society of International Gas Tanker and Terminal Operators)** — publisher of operational guidance for LNG/LPG terminals and carriers. Coverage spans mooring equipment ([SIGTTO Mooring Equipment Guidelines](../standards/sigtto-mooring-equipment.md)), ship-shore interface, vapor-handling, ESD, transfer system safety, manifold arrangements, and incident lessons-learned. SIGTTO publications routinely incorporated by reference into terminal procedures, charter parties, and lender ESG diligence.
- **OCIMF (Oil Companies International Marine Forum)** — publisher of MEG4 (Mooring Equipment Guidelines, 4th edition; 2018) — the marine-mooring companion to SIGTTO mooring guidance — TMSA 3 (tanker management self-assessment), JIP/MTQ (terminal management), and ship-inspection guidance (SIRE 2.0; 2024 rollout). MEG4 covers mooring-line MBL, factor-of-safety, fendering, and quick-release-hook practice.
- **EEMUA (Engineering Equipment and Materials Users Association)** — publisher of Pub 147 (in-service inspection of LNG storage tanks) and Pub 159 (inspection of above-ground storage tanks); widely used for terminal-tank in-service inspection planning. EEMUA coverage extends into Pub 154 (membrane-tank inspection) and Pub 154 Supplement.
- **IACS Unified Requirements (URs)** — IACS coordinates UR development across member societies; UR Z (machinery, hull) and UR P (piping for ships carrying liquefied gases) are particularly relevant to gas-carrier and FLNG hull approval.
- **API standards (beyond Std 625)** — API 521 (relief and depressuring systems), API 2000 (atmospheric and low-pressure storage tank venting), and API 14C (offshore safety systems) are routinely referenced into LNG facility and FLNG design.
- **ASME boiler and pressure vessel code** — Section VIII (pressure vessels) and B31.3 (process piping) referenced into LNG plant mechanical design alongside the LNG-specific codes.

## Atmospheric-methane and ESG regulation

- **EU Methane Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2024/1787)** — adopted 2024; mandates leak-detection-and-repair (LDAR) programs, restricts routine venting and flaring, and applies methane-intensity reporting to LNG imports into the EU. From 2027, LNG cargoes imported into the EU must be sourced from operators applying equivalent measurement, reporting and verification (MRV) practices. Affects market access for LNG cargoes from non-EU producers into EU regasification terminals.
- **US EPA Subpart W (40 CFR 98)** — Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program for petroleum and natural-gas systems; requires methane-emissions reporting for LNG facilities, including liquefaction terminals, LNG storage tanks, and import/export terminals. The 2024 final rule (Subpart W revisions) tightened reporting accuracy and expanded source-category coverage.
- **IMO MEPC.346(78)** — 2022 MEPC resolution on the energy-efficiency framework; methane-slip is now an in-scope emission category for the IMO GHG strategy, with eventual integration into life-cycle GHG metrics for marine fuels (the LCA Guidelines under MEPC.376(80) for marine fuels).
- **GIIGNL Methane / Carbon Intensity (CI) tagging** — voluntary cargo-level CI tagging framework allowing producers, traders, and buyers to track and report methane / carbon intensity per cargo. Increasingly referenced in offtake contracts and term sheets.
- **Voluntary frameworks** — OGMP 2.0 (Oil & Gas Methane Partnership, UNEP-led) reporting; MiQ certification (independent methane-intensity certification, third-party verified); GTI Veritas; SBTi targets; CDP disclosure. Lender and offtake-buyer due-diligence routinely references these.
- **PHMSA, state regulators, air-permit programs** — address LNG-facility venting and flaring under siting and air-permit programs in the US (Title V operating permits, NSPS OOOOa/b/c standards for new/modified sources).
- **IMO MARPOL Annex VI** — methane provisions affecting LNG carriers and LNG-as-fuel ships; methane-slip from dual-fuel engines is the primary regulatory target. MEPC.346(78) and forthcoming amendments anchor the marine-fuel LCA framework.
