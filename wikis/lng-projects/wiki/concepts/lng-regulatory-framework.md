# LNG Regulatory Framework

## Scope

This page is a one-paragraph-per-body synthesis of the standards organisations and regulatory bodies that bind LNG project design, construction, and operation. It does **not** restate clause numbers, threshold values, or the relative authority of bodies in any given jurisdiction; that determination is project-specific. Per the workspace-hub routing principle, lng-projects does not yet host `wiki/standards/<code-id>.md` pages — bodies are named here only.

## Key Concepts

- **NFPA (National Fire Protection Association, US)** — publisher of NFPA 59A, the US-jurisdiction primary standard for LNG production, storage, and handling
- **CEN / CENELEC (Europe)** — publisher of EN 1473, the European primary standard for onshore LNG installation and equipment
- **IMO (International Maritime Organization)** — publisher of the IGC Code governing the construction and equipment of ships carrying liquefied gases in bulk
- **SIGTTO (Society of International Gas Tanker and Terminal Operators)** — industry body publishing operational guidance for LNG/LPG terminals and carriers
- **OCIMF (Oil Companies International Marine Forum)** — publisher of MEG4 and other guidance on marine mooring, transfer, and tanker operations
- **EEMUA (Engineering Equipment and Materials Users Association)** — publisher of Pub 147 / Pub 159 for LNG storage tank in-service inspection
- **IACS (International Association of Classification Societies)** — coordinating body for class rules covering gas carriers and floating LNG units
- **API (American Petroleum Institute, US)** — publisher of API 625 covering tank systems for refrigerated liquefied gas storage
- **ABS / DNV (classification societies)** — class rules and guidance notes that govern design approval of floating LNG and gas-carrier hulls
