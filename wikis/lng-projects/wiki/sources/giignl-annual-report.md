# GIIGNL Annual Report

> Metadata-first source page for the International Group of Liquefied Natural Gas Importers (GIIGNL) Annual Report. Companion to IGU's *World LNG Report* with explicit importer-side framing.

## Source overview

The International Group of Liquefied Natural Gas Importers (GIIGNL) is a non-profit association of LNG importers headquartered in Levallois, France. Its *Annual Report* has been published since the early 2000s and is available as a free PDF via the GIIGNL publications portal (https://giignl.org/publications/) with no registration gate. The report's scope is deliberately importer-side: receiving-terminal capacity and utilization, importing-country breakdowns, spot vs. long-term trade-volume splits, contract-structure trends from the buyer perspective, regasification capacity, and short-term-market analytics. Publisher is GIIGNL. Cadence is strictly annual; the report is published in the spring of year N+1 and reflects calendar year N data.

## How to use this source

Cite GIIGNL when a page makes a **buyer-side or import-side claim** that IGU's all-tier framing does not specifically address: receiving-terminal capacity by country, regasification utilization rates, spot-market share evolution, importing-country diversification, or buyer-side contract restructuring (price-review clauses, destination flexibility, hub-indexation trends). GIIGNL is the canonical industry-association reference for the importer perspective; IGU is the canonical all-tier reference. The two reports are complementary, not redundant — citing both for the same claim is correct when the claim spans both perspectives. As with IGU, the contract is citation pointer plus page reference, not verbatim reproduction. Citation form: `(GIIGNL Annual Report YYYY, p.X)`.

## Coverage scope

**In scope**:
- Receiving-terminal capacity by country and per-terminal utilization metrics.
- Spot vs. long-term trade-volume split, year-on-year evolution.
- Importing-country breakdowns + regional flow patterns.
- Importer-side contract-structure trends (price reopener, destination flexibility, hub indexation, mid-term contracts).
- Regasification fleet (terminals + FSRUs) from the receiving-terminal operator's perspective.
- Short-term LNG market analytics (price arbitrage, reload activity, inter-basin flows).

**Not in scope** (defer to other sources):
- Liquefaction-side project lists, FID activity, exporter perspective — defer to [`igu-2024-lng-report`](igu-2024-lng-report.md) / [`igu-2025-lng-report`](igu-2025-lng-report.md).
- US export statistics, Henry Hub pricing, federal regulatory framing — defer to [`doe-eia-lng-outlook`](doe-eia-lng-outlook.md).
- US-terminal-specific docket detail — defer to [`ferc-lng-portal`](ferc-lng-portal.md).
- Vessel-side technical detail (cargo containment, BOG management) — defer to concept pages and IGC Code standards pages.

## Cross-wiki citation guidance

Cite this source when:

- A concept or standards page makes an importer-side, receiving-terminal, or spot-market claim. Examples: regasification utilization rate trends, FSRU fleet deployment from the importer perspective, mid-term contract activity, hub-indexation share.
- A page in a sibling wiki (marine-engineering, naval-architecture) cites importing-country flow data for design-context purposes (e.g., trade-route assumptions for FSRU charter-rate context).
- A page makes a claim about destination-flexibility or price-reopener trends that GIIGNL surveys explicitly.

Prefer IGU when the claim is global all-tier (liquefaction + regasification + trade) rather than importer-specific. Prefer DOE/EIA when the claim is US-jurisdiction specific.

**Do NOT** double-cite GIIGNL + IGU for the same data point — pick the source whose framing matches the argument, and cite both only when the argument explicitly bridges importer-side and global-industry framings.

## Updates + maintenance

- **Cadence**: Annual; published spring of year N+1 covering calendar year N.
- **Edition tracking**: GIIGNL editions are referenced by data-year; cite as `Annual Report YYYY` where YYYY is the data year.
- **Refresh trigger**: New edition typically lands April–May. The wiki should refresh "current state" GIIGNL citations once per year; trend citations may anchor on a specific prior edition and remain valid.
- **Stability**: GIIGNL has published the Annual Report continuously for two decades; URL stability is high (publications portal is the canonical landing page even if individual PDF URLs change between editions).
