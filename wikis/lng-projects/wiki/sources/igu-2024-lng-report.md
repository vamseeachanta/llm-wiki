# IGU 2024 World LNG Report

> Metadata-first source page for the International Gas Union's 15th annual World LNG Report (2024 edition, covering 2023 calendar-year data). Companion + prior-year sibling to the 2025 edition; useful for year-over-year trend continuity.

## Source overview

The International Gas Union (IGU) *World LNG Report* 2024 edition is the 15th in the series and covers the 2023 calendar year. Like every edition since 2010, it is published as a free PDF with no registration gate via the IGU resources portal (https://www.igu.org/resources/world-lng-report-2024/). Scope is the global LNG industry: trade flows, liquefaction capacity (operating, under-construction, sanctioned, proposed), regasification capacity and FSRUs, LNG bunkering and small-scale, contract structures and pricing, and project-level lists. Publisher is IGU (International Gas Union, Barcelona / Vevey). Cadence is strictly annual, published mid-year reflecting prior-year data.

## How to use this source

Treat the 2024 edition as the **prior-year companion** to [`igu-2025-lng-report`](igu-2025-lng-report.md) when a wiki page makes a year-over-year claim — capacity additions in 2023, FID activity in 2023, trade-flow shifts driven by the post-2022 European import surge, or year-on-year fleet growth. For static "current state" claims about installed capacity or operating projects, prefer the 2025 edition (it supersedes prior figures); for trend or delta claims that require an explicit prior-year anchor, cite the 2024 edition by edition + page reference. **Do not** reproduce IGU tables or charts verbatim into the wiki — citation pointer plus original-source page reference is the contract. Extraction is metadata-only on this page; concept pages cite IGU 2024 by `(IGU 2024, World LNG Report 15th ed., p.X)` form.

## Coverage scope

**In scope** for IGU 2024 specifically:
- 2023 trade-flow data (LNG trade volumes, origin and destination matrix, spot vs. long-term breakdown).
- Liquefaction capacity snapshot end-2023; project-by-project lists for operating and sanctioned facilities.
- Regasification capacity snapshot end-2023, including FSRU fleet additions during the European-supply pivot.
- 2023 contract structures, pricing benchmarks, and tender activity.
- Bunkering and small-scale LNG activity through 2023.

**Not in scope** (defer to other sources):
- 2024 calendar-year additions and forward 2025 outlook — those live in [`igu-2025-lng-report`](igu-2025-lng-report.md).
- US-jurisdiction project-docket detail — defer to [`ferc-lng-portal`](ferc-lng-portal.md).
- US export statistics + Henry Hub price history — defer to [`doe-eia-lng-outlook`](doe-eia-lng-outlook.md).
- Importer-side spot-market and receiving-terminal detail — defer to [`giignl-annual-report`](giignl-annual-report.md).

## Cross-wiki citation guidance

Cite the 2024 edition (this source page) when:

- A concept page in `concepts/` or a standards page in `standards/` makes a year-over-year claim and needs an explicit prior-year anchor distinct from 2025.
- A trend chart or comparison page needs a 2023-baseline reference (e.g., 2023 vs. 2024 capacity-addition delta).
- A retrospective claim about the post-2022 European import pivot needs primary IGU sourcing on 2023 numbers.

Prefer [`igu-2025-lng-report`](igu-2025-lng-report.md) when:
- The claim is "current state" rather than year-over-year — the 2025 edition supersedes prior figures.
- A single citation suffices and the 2024 anchor is not load-bearing for the argument.

**Do NOT** cite this source when the page is making a US-regulatory-docket argument (use FERC + DOE/EIA) or an importer-side spot-market argument (use GIIGNL).

## Updates + maintenance

- **Edition**: 15th edition, published 2024 (covers 2023 data).
- **Cadence**: Annual; the 2025 edition (16th) supersedes most "current state" citations but does not retire the 2024 edition for trend purposes.
- **Refresh trigger**: When a future IGU edition (2026, 2027) lands and the wiki rolls citations forward, the 2024 edition page remains valid as a year-over-year anchor and should not be deleted.
- **Version tracking**: Page metadata records edition number (15th) and data-year (2023) explicitly so future edition renumbering does not silently invalidate citations.
