# IGU 2025 World LNG Report

## Summary

The International Gas Union (IGU) publishes the *World LNG Report* annually as the gas industry's flagship state-of-the-market reference; the 2025 edition is the 16th in the series. Its ~168 pages cover global LNG trade flows, liquefaction capacity (operating, under-construction, sanctioned, and proposed), regasification capacity, FSRU and small-scale and LNG-bunkering markets, and project-level lists for operating, sanctioned, and speculative facilities. The report is distributed as a free PDF with no registration gate.

## Why this is foundational for the wiki

IGU's *World LNG Report* is the single most-cited LNG-industry annual data source. It is free, publicly available, globally trusted by operators, regulators, and analysts, and refreshed every year on a stable structure. Most concept pages in `lng-projects/wiki/concepts/` benefit from IGU citations whenever they make claims about installed capacity, fleet counts, trade volumes, or named projects — IGU page-references are reviewer-defensible without paywall friction, and the annual cadence lets the wiki track multi-year trends by re-citing successive editions rather than restating the data.

## Major sections of the 2025 report

- Executive Summary + 2024 trade data.
- Liquefaction Capacity (operating, under-construction, sanctioned-but-not-FID, proposed). Project-by-project in major regions.
- Regasification Capacity (terminals + FSRUs).
- LNG Trade Flows (origin + destination + spot vs. long-term).
- Pricing + Contract Structures.
- LNG Bunkering Market.
- Small-scale LNG.
- Technology Trends.

## Citation usage

When concept pages cite "global LNG capacity," "liquefaction project counts," "regasification terminal counts," "FSRU fleet size," or similar industry totals, they should cite IGU 2025 (or the then-current edition) by page reference rather than echoing the data verbatim into the wiki. This keeps the wiki audit-trail-defensible while respecting IGU's role as the canonical primary aggregator — and it keeps lng-projects pages cheap to refresh against future IGU editions by swapping the year/page reference rather than rewriting prose.
