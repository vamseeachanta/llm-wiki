# FERC 18 CFR Part 153 — Authorization for LNG Import/Export Facilities

> **Regulatory framing.** Title 18 of the US Code of Federal Regulations is a federal regulatory codification — not a private standard with editions. Revisions occur through Federal Register notice-and-comment rulemaking; the live authoritative text is the eCFR. This page is metadata-only synthesis; no regulatory text is reproduced.

## Scope

Federal regulations governing FERC's authority to authorize the construction, operation, or modification of LNG import/export facilities under the Natural Gas Act of 1938. Part 153 defines the application requirements for any party seeking to site, construct, or operate a facility used for the import or export of natural gas (including LNG).

Coverage includes:

- Pre-filing process (mandatory for LNG export terminals and most imports)
- Resource Reports (project description, environmental, engineering)
- NEPA compliance (EA or EIS depending on facility scale)
- Safety and cryogenic-design review (siting, impoundment, thermal radiation, vapor-cloud dispersion modeling)
- Environmental review (wetlands, fish/wildlife, cultural resources, air, noise)
- Public participation and Tribal consultation
- Post-authorization compliance (construction reports, in-service notification, annual reporting)

## Regulatory framework context

- **Natural Gas Act (NGA) Section 3** (15 U.S.C. § 717b) — authorizes FERC over LNG export and import facilities (siting, construction, operation). 18 CFR Part 153 implements this authority.
- **NGA Section 7** (15 U.S.C. § 717f) — pipeline-certification authority (18 CFR Part 157). More often invoked for the interstate pipeline lateral feeding an LNG project than for the liquefaction or regasification facility itself.
- **DOE / FECM (Office of Fossil Energy and Carbon Management)** — holds export-authorization authority separately for the **natural gas commodity** under NGA Section 3; FERC handles facility siting and construction. Two parallel authorizations are required for any US LNG export project (DOE for the molecules, FERC for the plant).
- **PHMSA 49 CFR Part 193** — companion safety regulation governing LNG facility safety standards (siting, design, construction, operation, maintenance, fire protection, security). FERC's safety review under Part 153 cross-references and incorporates Part 193 by reference. See `standards/phmsa-49-cfr-193.md` (forthcoming).
- **NEPA (42 U.S.C. § 4321 et seq.)** — drives the environmental review track under FERC's NEPA implementing regulations (18 CFR Part 380).

## Key requirements

- **Resource Reports 1–13** filed with the application:
  - RR1 — General Project Description
  - RR2 — Water Use and Quality
  - RR3 — Fish, Wildlife, and Vegetation
  - RR4 — Cultural Resources
  - RR5 — Socioeconomics
  - RR6 — Geological Resources
  - RR7 — Soils
  - RR8 — Land Use, Recreation, Aesthetics
  - RR9 — Air and Noise Quality
  - RR10 — Alternatives (including no-action and cumulative-impact)
  - RR11 — Reliability and Safety
  - RR12 — PCBs (where applicable to existing facilities)
  - RR13 — Engineering and Design Material (LNG-specific: cryogenic design, impoundment sizing, hazard modeling)
- **Pre-filing process** — mandatory for LNG export terminals and many import projects under FERC's Part 157 / Part 153 pre-filing rules; typically requires a minimum 6-month engagement before formal application is accepted.
- **NEPA review** — Environmental Assessment (EA) or Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) under FERC's NEPA Implementing Regulations (18 CFR Part 380); EIS is the norm for new greenfield LNG export terminals.
- **Cryogenic-design review** — siting evaluation, impoundment sizing, thermal-radiation-exposure modeling, and flammable-vapor-dispersion modeling drive the heat-flux distance and exclusion-zone calculations. Outputs determine the facility footprint relative to property lines and offsite receptors.
- **Public participation and Tribal consultation** — scoping meetings, comment periods, and government-to-government consultation under Section 106 (National Historic Preservation Act) and EO 13175.
- **Post-authorization compliance** — implementation plans, construction progress reports, FERC implementation-plan review, in-service authorization, and annual operational reporting through the project life.

## Major LNG project examples (US, FERC-authorized under Part 153)

- **Sabine Pass LNG (Cheniere Energy)** — first US LNG export terminal in the post-shale-gas era; Trains 1–6 (Louisiana). Set the procedural template for subsequent FERC export authorizations.
- **Cove Point LNG (Dominion Energy)** — converted from import to export terminal (Maryland); demonstrated the modification-authorization pathway under Part 153.
- **Plaquemines LNG (Venture Global)** — Phase 1 + Phase 2 phased authorization model (Louisiana); illustrates incremental-authorization sequencing for large-scale projects.
- **Commonwealth LNG (Commonwealth LNG)** — FERC-authorized; financing-dependent, illustrative of the gap between FERC authorization and FID.
- **Woodfibre LNG (Squamish, BC, Canada)** — although Canadian-jurisdiction (CER, not FERC), referenced here as a project-specific cross-link for the spinout's client work; the parallel Canadian framework is governed by NEB/CER instead of FERC. See concept page work in lng-projects.
