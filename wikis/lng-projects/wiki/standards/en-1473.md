# EN 1473 — Installation and Equipment for Liquefied Natural Gas — Design of Onshore Installations

> **code_id:** `en-1473` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **publisher:** CEN (European Committee for Standardization) &nbsp;·&nbsp; **revision:** not-on-disk; current published is **2021**

## Scope

EN 1473 is the European harmonized standard for the **design, construction, and operation of onshore LNG installations**. Its scope spans the full onshore-LNG facility taxonomy: import terminals, export / liquefaction terminals, peak-shaving plants, satellite installations, and LNG fuel-station installations. It is the European Union parallel to the US **NFPA 59A** and the Canadian **CSA Z276** national LNG-facility design codes, and is mandatory under the EU member-state harmonized regulatory framework as well as UK retained-EU-law (post-Brexit). EN 1473 acts as the *umbrella* standard for onshore LNG, with detailed sub-standards in the EN 14620 (cryogenic tanks) and EN 1474 (transfer systems) families covering the major component scopes by reference. ## Key clauses

- **Clause 4 — General Requirements.** Scope of application, key definitions, and the cross-cutting requirements that frame the rest of the standard.
- **Clause 5 — Site Selection.** Plant siting, thermal-radiation and flammable-vapor separation distances; aligned with NFPA 59A in form but uses EU-specific dispersion-modeling conventions (see EN 1473 vs NFPA 59A vs CSA Z276 below).
- **Clause 6 — Process Equipment.** Towers, heat exchangers, pumps, compressors, and other process equipment used in liquefaction and regasification trains.
- **Clause 7 — Storage.** Cross-references **EN 14620** for cryogenic tank design and **EN 1474-1/-2/-3** for transfer systems; EN 1473 itself sets the umbrella requirements rather than re-deriving the tank-system rules.
- **Clause 8 — Cargo Transfer.** Loading-arm and transfer-system requirements at the terminal interface; coordinates with EN 1474-1 (marine), EN 1474-2 (floating cryogenic), and EN 1474-3 (offshore).
- **Clause 9 — Vapor and Boil-Off Management.** BOG handling, flare and reliquefaction provisions, and vent-system design.
- **Clause 10 — Fire Protection.** Water-spray, water-curtain, dry-chemical, and high-expansion-foam systems; gas and flame detection.
- **Clause 11 — Operations and Maintenance.** Operating procedures, inspection programmes, recordkeeping, and personnel-qualification requirements.
- **Clause 12 — Cybersecurity.** Added in the 2021 revision; aligns onshore LNG installation cyber-physical safeguards with current EU NIS Directive expectations and IEC 62443 industrial-control-system practice.

## EN-family LNG standards

EN 1473 is the umbrella; the major component scopes are delegated to companion EN standards. These are the principal members of the EN LNG family:

- **EN 14620** — Cryogenic tank design (companion to API Std 625).
- **EN 1474-1** — Marine transfer systems.
- **EN 1474-2** — Floating cryogenic transfer systems.
- **EN 1474-3** — Offshore transfer systems.
- **EN 1532** — Ship-to-shore interface emergency-shutdown (ESD) requirements.
- **EN 12838** — LNG fueling-station installations.

## EU LNG project context

Most operating EU LNG installations are **import / regasification** terminals; liquefaction is rare in the EU itself, with Norwegian Hammerfest being the notable export-terminal exception. Representative projects governed by EN 1473 include Zeebrugge (Belgium), Dunkirk (France), Sines (Portugal), Mugardos (Spain), the Adriatic FSRU and Krk Island FSRU (Croatia), and Świnoujście (Poland). LNG sourced from Russian Yamal flows via Sabetta into EU regas terminals under the EN 1473 umbrella for the receiving-side scope.

## EN 1473 vs NFPA 59A vs CSA Z276

| Aspect | EN 1473 (EU) | NFPA 59A (US) | CSA Z276 (CA) |
|--------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| Distance criteria | Aligned with EU dispersion modeling (EFFECTS / FRED / ALOHA) | PHAST + FERC-LNG | PHAST + dispersion sub-aligned |
| Component standards | EN 14620 + EN 1474-1/-2/-3 | API Std 625 + API 620/650 | API Std 625 + CSA W47 |
| Cybersecurity provisions | 2021 (formal section) | 2023 edition | 2022 edition |
| Major projects governed | Zeebrugge, Sines, Krk, Dunkirk | Sabine Pass, Plaquemines | LNG Canada, Woodfibre |
