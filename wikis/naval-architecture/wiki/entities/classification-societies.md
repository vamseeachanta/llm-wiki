# Classification Societies

## Scope

This page is a directory of the principal classification societies that issue ship rules and certify hulls and machinery. It is the first entity-page in the naval-architecture wiki and is intentionally aggregate (per `wiki/standards/<code-id>.md` routing, per-society standards pages are reserved for downstream calc-citation needs). It does NOT enumerate specific rule-clause numbers and does NOT compare scope coverage between societies.

## Key Concepts

- **Classification society** — independent body that publishes structural and machinery rules, surveys ships during construction and in service, and assigns class notation.
- **IACS (International Association of Classification Societies)** — federation that coordinates Unified Requirements (URs), Unified Interpretations (UIs), and Procedural Requirements across member societies.
- **Recognized organizations (ROs)** — class societies authorized by flag states to act on the state's behalf for statutory certification.
- **Class notation** — encoded summary of a ship's classed scope, e.g. hull, machinery, navigation, and additional service or operational notations.
- **Annual / intermediate / special surveys** — periodic in-service inspections required to maintain class.
