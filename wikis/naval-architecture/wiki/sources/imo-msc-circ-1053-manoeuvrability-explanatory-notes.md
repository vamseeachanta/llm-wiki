# IMO MSC/Circ.1053 Explanatory Notes to the Standards for Ship Manoeuvrability

Raw path: `/mnt/ace/acma-codes/IMO/Maneouvrability/2002 MSC Circ.1053 Explanatory Notes to Manoeuvrability.pdf`

## Relevance

Primary explanatory source for maneuverability metrics, yaw-rate/rudder-angle relation, dynamic instability tests, and standard-condition caveats.

## Key extracted content

- Steady turn relation: `R = V / psi_dot` at fixed engine output and rudder angle.
- Equilibrium yaw-rate/rudder-angle relation (“spiral loop”) diagnoses dynamic instability.
- Reverse spiral and pull-out tests are used when standard maneuvers indicate instability.
- `L/V` functions as a maneuvering time constant for overshoot criteria.
- Standard conditions require deep/unconfined/sheltered water, near full-load/even-keel condition, and calm-weather corrections.
