# USNA EN400 — Principles of Ship Performance (Course Notes)

## Summary

Course notes covering hull form, hydrostatics, stability, structures, resistance, seakeeping, maneuvering, and submarines.

## Yaw/rudder extraction for #2564

- §1.8 defines ship axes and DOF: `x` longitudinal/forward, `y` starboard, `z` vertical; yaw is rotation about `z`. Locator: PDF p.35 / printed p.1-25.
- §9.2.2 states maneuvering response depends on rudder dimensions, rudder angle, and ship speed. Locator: PDF p.330 / printed p.9-3.
- Rudder area ratio: `Rudder area / (LPP * T)`, with cited range about 0.017 for cargo ships to 0.025 for destroyers.
- Standard rudder angle context: USN “standard rudder” is 20° of wheel.
- Greater speed reduces response time and increases overshoot because rudder force at a given angle increases with speed.
- §9.3.1 describes rudder span/chord constraints and notes span is often approximately propeller diameter.
- §9.3.3.1 notes typical rudder range about ±35° and stall near 45°.
- §9.4.1 explains low-speed steering limits and propeller/rudder thrust-vectoring benefit.
