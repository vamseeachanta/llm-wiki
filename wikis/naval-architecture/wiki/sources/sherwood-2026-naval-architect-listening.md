# Sherwood (2026) — Naval Architect Role and Stakeholder Listening

Raw URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/david-sherwood-1a446649_navalarchitecture-marinedesign-commercialmarine-share-7457012721419575296-CsDU

Author: David Sherwood. Format: LinkedIn post (industry-practitioner essay). Date observed: 2026-05-07.

## Relevance

A practitioner enumeration of the naval architect's scope of discipline split into **core** versus **adjacent / supporting** expertise, paired with an argument for stakeholder-listening discipline during shipbuilding execution. Useful as a scope-of-discipline lens that complements the process-oriented [Ship Design Process](../concepts/ship-design.md) page.

## Core naval-architect domains (per Sherwood)

- Arrangements and capacities
- Hydrodynamics
- Propulsion
- Stability
- Structural design

## Adjacent / supporting expertise the naval architect must integrate

- Electrical systems
- Mechanical systems
- HVAC
- Fitout
- Vessel operations — crewing, maintenance, operating costs
- Regulations and compliance

## Practitioner thesis

Sherwood's central claim is that technical expertise alone is insufficient: missing critical input from other disciplines at the start of, and during, execution forfeits opportunities to improve the product. He frames this against the shipyard caricature of the naval architect "looking over the project like a god" — acknowledging the architect should be a front-line expert, but arguing the expertise must be paired with active listening and stakeholder integration.
