# Intact Stability Criteria

## Scope

This page NAMES the categories of intact-stability criteria used to qualify ships in the upright (undamaged) condition. It defers all numeric thresholds, formula constants, and code clauses to a future `wiki/standards/imo-is-code.md` codified-standards page. It does NOT duplicate the GZ-curve mechanics page (`concepts/stability.md`) and does NOT cover post-damage flooded conditions (`concepts/damage-stability.md`).

## Key Concepts

- **General GZ-curve criteria family** — minimum righting-arm magnitude, minimum range of positive stability, area under the GZ curve up to specified angles, minimum angle of maximum righting arm.
- **Severe-wind / heavy-rolling criterion (weather criterion)** — energy balance comparing wind heeling moment to residual righting energy after a roll-back excursion; applies to most ship types.
- **Free-surface correction** — partially filled tanks reduce effective metacentric height; a virtual rise of G is added per tank.
- **Special-class criteria** — additional rules for passenger ships, container ships, offshore-supply vessels, and high-speed craft.
- **Operational loading conditions** — criteria are evaluated across departure / mid-voyage / arrival / partial-cargo loading combinations.
- **Margin-line and downflooding angle** — geometric limits that cap allowable heel separately from the GZ-energy test.
- **Second-Generation Intact Stability (SGISC)** — performance-based criteria for vulnerable failure modes (parametric roll, pure loss, dead-ship, surf-riding, excessive accelerations).
