# Ship Structural Strength

## Scope

This page NAMES the structural-strength concepts and the standards bodies that govern hull-girder design. It defers all numeric thresholds, section-modulus formulas, and clause-specific rule values to a future `wiki/standards/iacs-ur-s.md` codified-standards page. It is the strength-side companion to `concepts/ship-structures.md` (which surveys structural arrangements) and to `concepts/ship-weights-and-loading.md` (which covers the load side).

## Key Concepts

- **Hull girder** — the ship treated as a beam in longitudinal bending; the dominant primary structural model.
- **Still-water bending moment (SWBM)** — bending caused by the longitudinal mismatch between weight and buoyancy distributions in calm water.
- **Wave-induced bending moment (VWBM)** — additional sagging or hogging contribution from a design wave or rule wave.
- **Hogging vs sagging** — convex-up vs concave-up curvatures; both must be checked.
- **Section modulus** — geometric stiffness of the hull cross-section about its neutral axis; primary strength index.
- **Hull-girder ultimate strength** — collapse moment under combined bending and local plate buckling.
- **Buckling and ultimate-strength of stiffened panels** — secondary and tertiary structural checks.
- **Fatigue strength** — cumulative damage under cyclic stress ranges from waves; S-N-curve based.
- **Yielding criterion** — von Mises or equivalent stress check against material yield.
- **Categories of structural members** — primary (hull-girder), secondary (stiffeners on panels), tertiary (plating between stiffeners).

## Standards / References

- IACS — *Unified Requirements S series* on longitudinal strength (named only; clauses reside in the future `wiki/standards/iacs-ur-s.md`); see https://iacs.org.uk/ for the public list.
- IACS — *Common Structural Rules (CSR)* for bulk carriers and oil tankers (referenced by member societies).
- SNAME — *Principles of Naval Architecture*, Volume I (Second Revision), hull-structural-design chapter.
