# Anode (Sacrificial)

A sacrificial anode is a more electrochemically active metal connected to a protected structure
to prevent corrosion through galvanic action. Used extensively in offshore cathodic protection systems.

## Anode Materials

| Material | Potential (V vs Ag/AgCl) | Typical Use |
|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| Aluminum-Zinc-Indium (Al-Zn-In) | -1.05 to -1.12 | Primary offshore anode |
| Aluminum-Zinc-Mercury (Al-Zn-Hg) | -1.05 to -1.10 | Older design, being phased out |
| Zinc | -1.00 to -1.05 | Brackish/mud zone, older platforms |
| Magnesium | -1.50 to -1.70 | Freshwater, not typical offshore |

## Key Design Parameters (per DNV-RP-B401)

- **Electrochemical capacity**: Minimum 2600 Ah/kg (Al-alloy), 780 Ah/kg (Zn)
- **Utilization factor**: Typically 0.85–0.90 depending on anode configuration
- **Driving potential**: Difference between anode operating potential and structure protection potential
- **Anode resistance**: Dwight or McCoy formulas for individual anodes

## Design Life

Typical design life for offshore structures: 20–30 years, determined by total anode mass required
and coating breakdown factor over the design period.
