# Semisubmersible

A semisubmersible is a floating offshore unit with buoyant columns and pontoons arranged to reduce wave-induced motions compared with ship-shaped forms. Semisubmersibles are used for drilling, production, construction, accommodation, and specialist marine operations.

## Engineering Role

Semisubmersible engineering commonly involves:

- low-frequency and wave-frequency motion assessment
- column/pontoon hydrodynamic modeling
- station-keeping by mooring or dynamic positioning
- air-gap, deck-wetness, and green-water checks
- riser, drilling, or construction-equipment interface limits
- transit, installation, and survival-condition planning
