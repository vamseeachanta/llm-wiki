# Jack-Up

A jack-up is a mobile offshore unit with a buoyant hull and extendable legs. It floats during transit, then lowers its legs to the seabed and elevates the hull above the waterline for drilling, construction, accommodation, or maintenance work.

## Engineering Role

Jack-up engineering commonly includes:

- site-specific metocean and geotechnical screening
- leg, spudcan, and foundation interaction checks
- preload, punch-through, and leg-penetration planning
- elevated-storm condition assessment
- transit, tow, jacking, and installation procedures
- collision, dropped-object, and nearby-asset interface management
