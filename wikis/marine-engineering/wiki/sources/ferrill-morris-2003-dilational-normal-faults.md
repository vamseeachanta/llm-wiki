# Dilational normal faults (Ferrill & Morris 2003)

## Citation

> Ferrill, D.A., & Morris, A.P. (2003). Dilational normal faults.
> *Journal of Structural Geology*, 25(2), 183-196.
> doi:[10.1016/S0191-8141(02)00029-9](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0191-8141(02)00029-9)

## Summary

Develops **dilation tendency** Tₐ = (σ₁ − σ_n) / (σ₁ − σ₃) as a per-orientation
scalar quantifying how close a fault surface is to opening (dilating) under a
given stress state. Establishes the operational complementarity between slip
tendency and dilation tendency: a fault may be critically stressed for slip
yet poorly oriented for fluid transmission, and vice versa. Field-application
emphasis is on normal-faulting regimes, where dilational segments preferentially
host fluid flow.

## Key Contributions

- Defines dilation tendency on the [0, 1] interval, bounded by σ₁-aligned
  surfaces (least dilatant) and σ₃-aligned surfaces (most dilatant).
- Shows that dilation tendency varies with both fault orientation and the
  σ₂ value within the principal-stress envelope (Lode parameter sensitivity).
- Documents geometric and mineralogic indicators of dilational fault segments
  in natural normal-fault systems.
- Provides the standard reference for fluid-pathway prediction in fractured
  reservoirs and CO₂-storage caprock integrity workflows.
