# A note on fault reactivation (Sibson 1985)

## Citation

> Sibson, R.H. (1985). A note on fault reactivation. *Journal of Structural
> Geology*, 7(6), 751-754.
> doi:[10.1016/0191-8141(85)90150-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/0191-8141(85)90150-6)

## Summary

Establishes the frictional reactivation criterion for cohesionless pre-existing
faults under a given stress state. For a planar surface with cohesion ≈ 0, the
Coulomb failure criterion τ ≥ μ σ_n reduces to a friction-coefficient threshold
on the slip-tendency ratio τ/σ_n. The paper's central observation is that
**pre-existing faults can slip at orientations that would not form new faults**,
because they lack intact-rock cohesion — making reactivation the dominant
failure mode in basins with structural inheritance.

## Key Contributions

- Derives the orientation-window for reactivation as a function of friction
  coefficient and the stress ratio R = (σ₁ − σ₂) / (σ₁ − σ₃).
- Identifies "lock-up" angles beyond which reactivation is geometrically
  impossible regardless of differential stress.
- Provides the canonical reference for distinguishing reactivation
  (cohesionless surface + friction) from new-fracture formation
  (intact-rock + tensile strength + cohesion).
- Underpins induced-seismicity risk assessments where fluid injection into
  basement faults is the failure pathway.
