# NOV (2026) — Motion Compensation Systems Product Family

Raw URL: https://www.nov.com/products/motion-compensation-systems

Publisher: National Oilwell Varco (NOV). Format: vendor product family page with linked technical brochures. Date observed: 2026-05-12.

## Relevance

A vendor inventory of the four principal motion-compensation product families that NOV offers for offshore drilling — useful as a real-world product-naming anchor for the [Motion Compensation Systems](../concepts/motion-compensation-systems.md) concept page. The brochures are vendor marketing literature; **none of their content is reproduced in this wiki**. The concept page anchors on API specifications, DNV standards, and public peer-reviewed literature; the NOV documents serve only as an existence proof that each product family is commercially supplied and as a pointer for further reading outside this wiki.

## Product families catalogued on the NOV page

| Product family | Brief description (vendor-page paraphrase) | Local archive filename |
|---|---|---|
| Crown Mounted Compensation (CMC) | Drillstring motion compensator mounted at the top of the derrick to isolate the drillstring from vessel heave. | `NOV_Crown_Mounted_Compensation_2026.pdf` |
| Riser Tensioners | Direct-acting or wireline tensioners that maintain constant tension on the marine drilling riser through vessel heave. | `NOV_Riser_Tensioners_2026.pdf` |
| Active Heave Drilling Drawworks (AHD) | Drawworks-integrated active heave compensation: servo-controlled drum motion offsets vessel heave at the hook. | `NOV_Active_Heave_Drilling_Drawworks_AHD_2026.pdf` |
| Active Heave Dual Drilling Drawworks (AHDD) | Dual-drum variant of the AHD architecture for higher continuous duty or redundancy. | `NOV_Active_Heave_Dual_Drilling_Drawworks_AHDD_2026.pdf` |
| Offshore Product Reference Guide | NOV's broader offshore-product catalogue document; contains the above four families plus adjacent surface and subsea hardware. | `NOV_Offshore_Product_Reference_Guide_2026.pdf` |

PDFs were publicly downloadable from `assets.nov.com` at the time of observation (2026-05-12) and have been archived locally for offline reference; they are **not** committed to this repository under the project's vendor-PDF governance.

## Named application contexts (per the NOV page)
