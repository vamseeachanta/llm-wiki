# Slip-tendency analysis and fault reactivation (Morris, Ferrill, Henderson 1996)

## Citation

> Morris, A.P., Ferrill, D.A., & Henderson, D.B. (1996). Slip-tendency
> analysis and fault reactivation. *Geology*, 24(3), 275-278.
> doi:[10.1130/0091-7613(1996)024<0275:STAAFR>2.3.CO;2](https://doi.org/10.1130/0091-7613(1996)024%3C0275:STAAFR%3E2.3.CO;2)

## Summary

Foundational paper introducing **slip tendency** Tₛ = τ/σ_n as a quantitative
indicator of how close a planar surface is to frictional failure under a given
effective stress tensor. The authors demonstrate that mapping Tₛ over fault-
orientation space identifies critically stressed populations whose geometry is
consistent with observed seismicity, and that the method extends to forecasting
reactivation of pre-existing faults under perturbed stress states.

## Key Contributions

- Defines slip tendency as the ratio of resolved shear stress to effective
  normal stress on an arbitrarily oriented surface.
- Establishes the threshold criterion Tₛ ≥ μ (friction coefficient) for
  predicted slip on cohesionless pre-existing surfaces.
- Demonstrates the method on natural fault populations and a Yucca Mountain
  case study, correlating high-Tₛ orientations with observed micro-seismicity.
- Provides the methodological foundation for downstream reservoir-geomechanics
  workflows (induced-seismicity screening, CO₂-storage caprock integrity,
  fault-seal evaluation).
