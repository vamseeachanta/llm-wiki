# The Dynamics of Faulting and Dyke Formation, 2nd ed. (Anderson 1951)

## Citation

> Anderson, E.M. (1951). *The Dynamics of Faulting and Dyke Formation*,
> 2nd ed. Oliver & Boyd, Edinburgh.

No DOI — book reference predates DOI registration.

## Summary

The canonical classification of fault styles by the orientation of the
principal stresses relative to gravity. Anderson observed that, because the
Earth's surface is a free surface (zero shear traction), one principal stress
must be vertical at shallow depth; the orientation of the *vertical* principal
stress (σ₁, σ₂, or σ₃) then determines which fault style is mechanically
favored. The 1951 second edition consolidates and extends Anderson's earlier
1905 paper.

## Key Contributions

- **Andersonian regime classification:**
  - σ₁ vertical → normal-faulting regime, faults dip ~60°
  - σ₂ vertical → strike-slip regime, vertical strike-slip faults
  - σ₃ vertical → reverse / thrust regime, faults dip ~30°
- Mechanical reasoning grounded in the free-surface boundary condition
  and Mohr-Coulomb failure under principal-stress states.
- Foundational vocabulary still used in modern reservoir geomechanics,
  basin analysis, and induced-seismicity literature.
- Limitations (acknowledged in modern literature): assumes principal-stress
  alignment near a flat free surface; rotation occurs near topographic relief,
  faults, and pore-pressure heterogeneity.
