## Summary

The LNG2026 programme confirms that marine terminal and port-side engineering remain active, industry-visible problem areas within the LNG value chain. The programme does not provide detailed terminal design data, but it does explicitly identify a dedicated technical session on shipping, marine, and port operations that includes LNG bunkering, ship-to-ship transfer, and LNG port and terminal infrastructure.

This makes the programme useful as a signal source for topic prioritization, capability mapping, and research direction, even though it is not a substitute for design standards, terminal studies, or execution procedures.
