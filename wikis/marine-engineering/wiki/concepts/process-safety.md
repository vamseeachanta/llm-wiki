# Process Safety

The management of hazards associated with oil and gas processing operations — preventing and
mitigating fire, explosion, toxic release, and loss of containment in processing equipment.

## Key Elements (DNVGL-OS-E201)

- **Hazard identification**: HAZID, HAZOP, FMEA
- **Safety-critical elements**: Equipment whose failure could lead to loss of containment
- **Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS)**: Protection layers with defined SIL ratings
- **Pressure safety**: Relief valve sizing, flare capacity, blowdown
- **Fire and explosion protection**: Deluge, firewalls, hazardous area classification

## Protection Layers

1. **Process design**: Inherent safety (minimize inventory, reduce pressure if possible)
2. **Basic Process Control System (BPCS)**: Automatic control loops
3. **Alarm management**: Operator intervention at defined thresholds
4. **Safety Instrumented Functions (SIF)**: Automatic shutdown at defined setpoint
5. **Pressure relief devices**: Last line of defense against overpressure
6. **Physical protection**: Blast walls, fireproofing, dikes
