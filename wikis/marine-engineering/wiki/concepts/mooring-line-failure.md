# Mooring Line Failure

Catastrophic failure of a vessel's mooring line during berthed operations. Results in snap-back hazards (fatalities), production disruption, cargo delay, and potentially vessel breaking away from jetty.

## Types of Mooring Lines

| Material | Abbreviation | Key Failure Mode |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| High-Modulus Polyethylene | HMPE (Dyneema) | Creep, abrasion, UV degradation |
| Nylon | Nylon | Elastic elongation, fatigue |
| Polyester | Polyester | Abrasion, UV |
| Steel wire | Wire rope | Corrosion, fatigue |

## Industry-Wide HMPE Failure Pattern

Between 2007 and 2011, multiple HMPE mooring line failures were reported across LNG terminals including:
- **South Hook LNG (Milford Haven, 2015)** — LNG Zarga, HMPE line failure, severe crew injury
- **Prelude FLNG (2018)** — LNG carrier Gallina nylon line failure
- **BW Fleet Management** — 7 HMPE lines failed simultaneously in Spain
- **Veracruz Port (2024-2025)** — 5 mooring failures in 6 months
- **Golar Freeze at Elba Island (2006)** — passing vessel wake breakaway
- **Summit FSRU Bangladesh (2021)** — mooring line tear, 3 months out of service

## Fatal Snap-Back Incidents

- **Ansac Splendor at Longview (2018)** — spring line snap-back, 2 fatalities
- **MV Asterix at Busan (2023)** — HMPE snap-back injury, TSB Canada M23F0012

## Monitoring & Mitigation

Port Hedland (BHP): 35% reduction in mooring line failures via active monitoring program.
