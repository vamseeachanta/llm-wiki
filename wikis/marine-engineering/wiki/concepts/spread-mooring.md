# Spread Mooring

Spread mooring is a passive station-keeping arrangement where multiple mooring lines restrain a floating unit from several directions. The system provides horizontal restoring force and yaw restraint through line geometry, pretension, line stiffness, seabed interaction, and anchor capacity.

## Engineering Role

Spread moorings are common for floating production units, construction vessels, barges, and terminals where a vessel or offshore unit must stay within an operating envelope without full weathervaning freedom. The design problem is coupled: environmental loads, vessel motions, line tensions, fatigue, anchor capacity, clearances, risers, and operations criteria all interact.

## Key Design Questions

- What intact and damaged offsets are acceptable for the operation?
- Which environmental headings govern line tension, vessel excursion, and riser/umbilical clearance?
- How sensitive is the response to long-period swell, low-frequency drift, or local harbour effects?
- Are anchors, fairleads, chain/wire/polyester segments, and seabed conditions compatible with the required design life?
- What operational limits trigger cargo stop, standby, disconnection, or emergency release?
