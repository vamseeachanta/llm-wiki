# Corrosion Control

Strategies and techniques to prevent or mitigate the degradation of metallic structures in marine
and process environments.

## Primary Methods

| Method | Principle | Typical Application |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| Cathodic Protection | Electrochemical potential control | Submerged marine structures |
| Protective Coatings | Barrier between metal and corrosive environment | All zones |
| Material Selection | Use corrosion-resistant alloys | Critical piping, vessels |
| Corrosion Allowance | Extra wall thickness for corrosion loss | Pipeline, riser design |
| Inhibitors | Chemical addition to process stream | Internal pipeline, flowlines |

## Offshore Corrosion Environment

- **Atmospheric zone**: Above splash zone; UV, salt spray, humidity
- **Splash zone**: Most aggressive zone; cyclic wetting/drying, oxygen availability
- **Submerged zone**: Continuous immersion; dissolved O2, temperature, velocity dependent
- **Soil/mud zone**: Buried structures; anaerobic conditions, SRB activity
- **Internal process**: H2S, CO2, chlorides, temperature, flow velocity

## Scope distinction — marine-engineering external structural corrosion vs production-engineering internal production-system corrosion

This page covers corrosion control of **submerged and atmospheric external structural surfaces** in the marine environment: offshore-platform jackets and topside structural members, ship hulls, mooring hardware, riser external surfaces, subsea-tree and subsea-hardware external surfaces. The protective strategies that this page covers (cathodic protection, protective coatings, material selection for marine atmospheric and seawater service, corrosion-allowance design) are oriented to the external-structural-exposure environment.

The **internal corrosion of production-fluid-carrying tubulars and piping** is a complementary scope owned by the production-engineering wiki. Production tubing inside the well, production casing on its inside surface, internal-flowline and internal-piping surfaces of the production system are exposed to a chemistry environment (CO2, H2S, microbiologically-influenced corrosion, oxygen-ingress) that is fundamentally different from the seawater-and-atmospheric environment that this page addresses. The corrosion-control strategies are correspondingly different (internal-corrosion inhibitor injection, sour-service material qualification, internal coatings and linings, oxygen-scavenging chemistry).

The two scopes meet at the **wall of the tubular**: external structural corrosion attacks one side, internal production-system corrosion attacks the other. Both must be managed for the tubular to remain fit-for-service across its operating life; both contribute to wall-thickness loss that integrity-monitoring programs track. A given subsea tree, riser, or flowline therefore has corrosion-control coverage on both wikis — external structural per this page, internal production-system per the production-engineering [Corrosion Management](../../../production-engineering/wiki/concepts/corrosion-management.md) and [Well Integrity During Production](../../../production-engineering/wiki/concepts/well-integrity-during-production.md) cluster.

For production-system internal-corrosion management, see production-engineering [Well Integrity During Production](../../../production-engineering/wiki/concepts/well-integrity-during-production.md), [Corrosion Management](../../../production-engineering/wiki/concepts/corrosion-management.md), [Integrity Monitoring](../../../production-engineering/wiki/concepts/integrity-monitoring.md), [Intervention Triggers](../../../production-engineering/wiki/concepts/intervention-triggers.md), and the system-level standards anchors [ISO 21457](../../../production-engineering/wiki/standards/iso-21457.md) (materials selection and corrosion control for oil and gas production systems) and [NACE SP0106](../../../production-engineering/wiki/standards/nace-sp0106.md) (internal corrosion control in steel pipelines and piping systems).
