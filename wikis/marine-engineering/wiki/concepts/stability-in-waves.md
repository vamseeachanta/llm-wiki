# Stability in Waves

Stability in waves covers how a floating vessel or offshore unit maintains acceptable heel, trim, reserve stability, and dynamic behavior when exposed to wave excitation. It connects hydrostatics, seakeeping, loading condition management, free-surface effects, green-water exposure, and operational criteria.

## Engineering Use

Marine-engineering workflows use stability-in-waves reasoning to:

- screen floating units for operating envelopes rather than only calm-water equilibrium
- connect RAOs and motion response to deck, cargo, riser, and transfer limits
- identify headings or sea states where roll, pitch, or coupled motions become limiting
- distinguish survival checks from day-to-day operability checks
- route stability questions to naval-architecture pages when hydrostatic detail is needed
