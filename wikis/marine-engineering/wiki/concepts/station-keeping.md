# Station-Keeping

Station-keeping is the engineering discipline that keeps a floating unit within an acceptable position and heading envelope while it performs its operation. It can be passive, active, or hybrid: spread moorings, turret systems, dynamic positioning, thruster assistance, anchors, lines, and operating procedures all sit inside the station-keeping boundary.

## Engineering Use

Station-keeping is central to:

- floating production systems and export operations
- mobile offshore drilling units
- LNG carrier and FSRU berth behavior
- riser, umbilical, and transfer-system interface management
- marine operations where excursion limits affect safety or operability

## Main Subsystems

| Subsystem | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Mooring lines and anchors | Passive restoring force and position envelope control |
| Thrusters and controls | Active position and heading correction |
| Environmental load model | Wind, wave, current, and low-frequency forcing basis |
| Vessel response model | Motions, drift, damping, offsets, and heading behavior |
| Operations criteria | Watch circles, disconnection triggers, cargo stop rules, and degraded-state limits |
