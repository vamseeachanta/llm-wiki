# API RP 10B-4 — Testing of Foamed Cement

## Scope

API RP 10B-4 standardizes the additional test procedures for foamed-cement slurries (gaseous-foam-stabilized low-density cement). Foamed cement is used where conventional slurries are too heavy for weak formations (lost-circulation zones, shallow formations with low fracture gradient).

## Foamed-cement application

- **Use case** — when slurry density must be lower than the lightest conventional cement can deliver (typically < 12 ppg)
- **Mechanism** — base slurry (typically Class G or H) is foamed with nitrogen gas at the cementing-pump intake; foam stability maintained by surfactant additives
- **Limitations** — density-uniformity along the column, foam stability over time, compressive-strength development is more variable than conventional

## Test additions over RP 10B-2

- **Foam quality** — gas-volume fraction at standard conditions; typical 10-40% by volume
- **Base-slurry stability** under foam mixing
- **Compressive strength** vs foam quality (lower CS than conventional at same nominal density)

## Where it applies

Foamed-cement service companies and operators specifying foamed cement for weak-formation applications.
