# IADC Drilling Manual

## Scope

The industry-canonical practitioner reference for drilling operations published by the International Association of Drilling Contractors. Organized as a multi-volume technical compendium covering rig equipment, drilling fluids, well control, casing and cementing, drill string, bits, directional drilling, hole problems, completions, and rig safety. Used as the foundational reference text for drilling-engineer training, certification (WellCAP, IWCF), and as the citable authority for IADC-defined classification systems (bit classification, daily-drilling-report format).

## Why it sits in standards/ rather than sources/

The IADC Drilling Manual is the published reference document underlying multiple IADC-canonical classification systems and procedures. Cite it as the standard (with `publisher=IADC`, `code_id=iadc-drilling-manual`) for any wiki content that depends on IADC-defined nomenclature (rig-class definitions, bit codes, DDR format, well-control terminology).

## Structural-overview-vs-detail caveat

The wiki cites the IADC Manual as authority for **classification and nomenclature**; specific procedural detail, dimensional tables, and worked examples are paraphrased from open textbooks (Bourgoyne, Mitchell-Miska, Lyons) where possible to avoid transcribing the IADC publication. The Manual itself is a paid publication; vendor-derivative summaries of it are deny-listed.

## Where it applies

Drilling-engineer training programs; IADC certification (WellCAP, RigPass); rig-floor reference; drilling-tender response preparation and evaluation.
