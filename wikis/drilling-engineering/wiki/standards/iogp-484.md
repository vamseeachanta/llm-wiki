# IOGP 484 — Wellbore Plug and Abandonment Guidelines

## Scope

IOGP 484 is the international-industry-consensus guidance for plug-and-abandonment (P&A) operations developed by the International Association of Oil & Gas Producers. Codifies the **two-barrier principle** as the load-bearing P&A safety framework + abandonment-class definitions + lifecycle responsibilities for operator P&A planning.

## Two-barrier principle

The core IOGP 484 framework: every flow path from a formation hydrocarbon source to surface (or to another formation) must be isolated by **two independent verified barriers**. Primary barrier = first containment element; secondary barrier = independent backup. Both must be verified before the well is considered abandoned.

## Abandonment classes

- **Permanent abandonment** — well is permanently isolated; no future re-entry planned
- **Temporary suspension (TA)** — well is isolated but designed for future re-entry; lower barrier requirements vs permanent
- **Rig-skid abandonment** — temporary while rig moves between wells on a pad; lowest barrier requirements

## Where it applies

International operators conducting P&A operations; regulators referencing industry consensus; class-society review for offshore P&A safety case.
