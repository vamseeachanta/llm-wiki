# API RP 10B-2 — Testing of Well Cements

## Scope

API RP 10B-2 standardizes the laboratory test methods for well-cement slurries — thickening time (consistometer), rheology, compressive strength (UCA or destructive cube test), free water, fluid loss (HTHP filter press), and static gel-strength development. The lab test protocol that runs before the cement formulation is approved for the well.

## Key tests

- **Thickening time** — time for slurry to reach 70 or 100 Bc consistency (Bearden units) at simulated bottomhole pressure and temperature. Drives pumpability window.
- **Compressive strength** — UCA (ultrasonic cement analyzer) for non-destructive monitoring; cube-press for destructive. Compressive strength governs casing-support timing.
- **Free water** — fluid separating from slurry during static hold. High free water causes channels and shoe-track failures.
- **HTHP fluid loss** — filtrate volume in 30 min at simulated bottomhole conditions. Lower is better for protecting formation.
- **Rheology** — for displacement-design calculations.

## Where it applies

Cement service-company labs (Halliburton, Schlumberger, BJ Services / Baker Hughes) qualifying cement formulations for specific wells. Operator audit verifying QA / QC.
