# Semi-Submersible Rig

## Scope

Column-stabilized offshore drilling rig — a deepwater floater with two ballasted pontoons connected by vertical columns supporting the main deck. Pontoons are ballasted to a draft below the wave-zone, leaving the columns intersecting the waterplane. The small waterplane area and large pontoon volume produce low heave / pitch / roll response, making semis the deepwater rig of choice for harsh-environment operations.

## Class characteristics

- **Pontoon-and-column geometry** — two pontoons (typically) joined by 4-8 columns
- **Operating draft** — pontoons submerged ~70 ft to put waterplane in the column-only zone
- **Water-depth rating** — 1,500-10,000 ft depending on generation
- **Station-keeping** — mooring (8-12 lines, anchor or pre-set) for benign-to-moderate environments; dynamic positioning (DP-2 or DP-3) for deepwater and harsh conditions where mooring is impractical
- **Heave compensator** — required for floating rigs; absorbs vessel heave to keep WOB constant on the bit

## Generation taxonomy

Industry uses a generation numbering (Gen 1 through Gen 7+) reflecting build year and capability:

- **Gen 1-3** (1962-1985) — early floaters; mostly retired or scrapped
- **Gen 4** (1985-1999) — moored deepwater
- **Gen 5** (1998-2004) — DP-capable, ultra-deepwater
- **Gen 6** (2007-2013) — modern dual-derrick, high-spec
- **Gen 7+** (2013-present) — harsh-environment-capable, 12,000+ ft water depth

## Station-keeping options

- **Mooring** — anchor-based mooring for moderate water depths and benign environments
- **Dynamic positioning (DP)** — thruster-based active position-keeping; DP-2 (fault-tolerant) or DP-3 (redundant) class
- **Hybrid** — pre-set mooring with DP-assist common in 1,500-3,000 ft water depth

## Cross-domain interaction

Semi-sub hull form, intact / damage stability, and mooring design are [naval-architecture](../../../naval-architecture/wiki/index.md) and [marine-engineering](../../../marine-engineering/wiki/index.md) topics.
