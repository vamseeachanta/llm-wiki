# Coiled Tubing Overview

## Scope

Coiled tubing (CT) — continuous-length seamless steel tube deployed and retrieved from a reel via a hydraulic injector head. The CT unit replaces the jointed-pipe approach for many well-intervention and stimulation operations, enabling continuous deployment under pressure (live-well operations) and faster trip times.

## CT unit anatomy

1. **Reel** — large drum holding 5,000-30,000 ft of continuous coiled tubing
2. **Injector head** — hydraulic grippers and drive chains; forces tube into well or pulls it out
3. **Stripper / pack-off** — pressure-sealing element around the tube at the well entry
4. **CT BOP** — dedicated BOP for CT operations (shear, blind, pipe, slip rams in a 4-ram stack)
5. **Power pack / control cabin** — hydraulic and operator station
6. **Pumping unit** — for circulating fluid down the CT (separate from rig mud pumps)
7. **Mast** — supports injector head over the wellhead

## CT applications

- **Wellbore cleanout** — removing sand, scale, debris from production tubing
- **Acid stimulation** — placing acid in a target zone via CT bottom-hole assembly
- **Fishing** — retrieving lost tools or junk from the wellbore
- **CT drilling** — drilling slim-hole laterals from existing wells
- **Nitrogen unloading** — gas-lifting fluid out of a loaded well
- **Logging** — running wireline tools via CT in deviated / horizontal sections

## Fatigue management

CT undergoes plastic deformation each time it bends around the reel and the gooseneck guide — typical CT life is 50-200 trips before fatigue retirement. CT software (commercial vendor tools) tracks per-trip fatigue accumulation and retires the tubing when the accumulated-damage threshold is reached.
