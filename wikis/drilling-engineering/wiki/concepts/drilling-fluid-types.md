# Drilling-Fluid Types

## Scope

The major drilling-fluid (mud) families and the operational regimes that favor each. Selection driven by formation reactivity, target depth and temperature, environmental restrictions (cuttings disposal), and economics.

## Major families

### Water-based mud (WBM)

- **Spud / native mud** — water with formation clay buildup; cheap; routine top-hole
- **Bentonite mud** — water + bentonite; basic dispersed mud; routine
- **Inhibitive WBM** — bentonite + KCl, polymer, or glycol additives for shale inhibition; alternative to OBM in some regions
- **Saltwater / saturated brine** — through salt sections; uses attapulgite (salt-tolerant) instead of bentonite

### Oil-based mud (OBM)

- **Diesel-based** — historical; banned in many offshore jurisdictions on environmental grounds
- **Mineral-oil-based** — lower-toxicity hydrocarbon base; common until ~2000
- **Largely superseded by SBM in OBM regulatory environments**

### Synthetic-based mud (SBM)

- Synthetic base fluid (esters, olefins, paraffins) replaces mineral oil
- Lower toxicity, biodegradable cuttings
- Modern offshore deepwater standard for HPHT and ERD wells
- Significantly more expensive than WBM per barrel

### Foam / mist / air

- Air or nitrogen as primary circulating medium
- Used for underbalanced drilling, lost-circulation zones, hard-rock arid-region wells
- Specialized; not covered in detail here

## Selection drivers

| Driver | Favored type |
|---|---|
| Shale-rich formation | Inhibitive WBM or SBM |
| HPHT, deep | SBM |
| Environmental restriction | WBM or SBM (water-discharge-permit cuttings) |
| Cost-constrained | WBM |
| Lost circulation tendency | Foam or specially formulated WBM |
| Sour-service H2S | Iron-sulfide-controlled WBM or SBM |
