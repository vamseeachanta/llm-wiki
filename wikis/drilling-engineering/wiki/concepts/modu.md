# MODU — Mobile Offshore Drilling Unit

## Scope

MODU is the IMO and class-society umbrella term covering all mobile offshore drilling rigs classed as marine vessels for transit, station-keeping, and offshore-personnel safety. Includes [jackups](jackup-rig.md), [semi-submersibles](semi-submersible-rig.md), and [drillships](drillship.md). Excludes [platform rigs](platform-rig.md) (fixed installations) and [land rigs](land-rig.md) (not offshore-marine).

## Why the MODU collective matters

MODU is the integrating term for the **marine-vessel side** of a drilling rig. It governs:

- **Class certification** — ABS, DNV, Lloyd's Register, BV, ClassNK, KR, RINA, CRS, RS, IRS, PRS, and CCS each have MODU rules
- **Flag-state registration** — MODUs register under flags like a ship (Marshall Islands, Liberia, Panama for most international MODUs)
- **IMO compliance** — MODU Code (IMO Resolution A.1023(26) and successors) sets the international safety baseline
- **SOLAS applicability** — MODUs comply with SOLAS chapters relevant to manned marine units

The drilling package on top of the MODU is regulated separately under national petroleum-safety authorities (BSEE in US, HSE in UK, PSA in Norway, etc.).

## MODU vs platform rig vs land rig

| Category | MODU? | Class society? | Flag? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jackup | yes | yes | yes |
| Semi-submersible | yes | yes | yes |
| Drillship | yes | yes (with double-up as vessel) | yes |
| Platform rig | no | no (platform is the regulated unit) | n/a |
| Land rig | no | no | n/a |
