# Jackup Rig

## Scope

Bottom-supported offshore drilling rig characterized by a buoyant hull with three (most common) or four jackable legs. The hull floats to location, the legs jack down to the seabed, and the hull is then jacked up clear of the wave zone to drill on a stable bottom-supported platform.

## Class characteristics

- **Hull-and-legs configuration** — triangular three-leg arrangement is dominant; X-Y rectangular four-leg layouts on some larger units.
- **Leg type** — open-truss lattice (most modern jackups) or columnar / cylindrical (older / heavy-lift utility units).
- **Footings** — independent spudcans (most common) penetrating soft seabed by self-weight; mat foundation on rare older units for very-soft seabed.
- **Water-depth rating** — typically 300-400 ft; harsh-environment HE class to 500-700 ft.
- **Drilling depth rating** — typically 25,000-30,000 ft hookload-equivalent.
- **Cantilever** — modern jackups carry the drill floor on a skid-mounted cantilever that translates and skews aft of the hull, enabling drilling over pre-installed wellhead platforms.

## Deployment

1. **Wet tow** to location (rig hull floating, legs raised vertically). Tug-towed at low speed; long inter-region moves use heavy-lift transport vessel.
2. **Preload** — sea water pumped into preload tanks to load the spudcans into the seabed to a depth that simulates worst-case operating + storm load, then drained. Confirms spudcans won't punch through during operations.
3. **Jacking up** — hull lifted above wave zone, typically 50-60 ft air gap.
4. **Cantilever skidding** — drill floor moved aft over wellhead.
5. **Operations**.
6. **Reverse** to mobilize off location.

## Harsh-environment subclass

HE jackups are purpose-built for North Sea / Norwegian Sea / Barents Sea conditions: heavier legs, deeper-water-rating, dual derricks, winterized equipment, cantilever ratings for the heaviest BOP stacks. Examples: Maersk Inspirer (named), Noble Lloyd Noble (named) — see [Noble Corporation fleet](../entities/noble-corporation-fleet.md) and [Maersk Drilling fleet](../entities/maersk-drilling-fleet.md) where present.
