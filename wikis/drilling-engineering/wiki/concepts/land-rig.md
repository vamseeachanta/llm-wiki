# Land Rig

## Scope

Onshore drilling rig — truck-mounted, trailer-mounted, or pad-mounted skid-system depending on mobility and rated depth. Modern land-rig fleets are dominated by pad-style "walking rigs" that can move between wellheads on a single multi-well pad without rig-down / rig-up.

## Class characteristics

- **Mobility class**:
  - **Truck-mounted single-axle** — shallow workover / drilling rigs, ~3,000-5,000 ft rated
  - **Trailer-mounted** — intermediate-depth rigs, ~6,000-12,000 ft
  - **Skid / walking-system** — modern pad-drilling rigs, 15,000-25,000+ ft rated; designed to walk between wells on a pad
- **Hookload rating** — 100,000 lbf to 1,500,000 lbf depending on class
- **Power** — diesel-mechanical, diesel-electric (AC or DC), or grid-electric for hybrid pad rigs
- **Substructure** — varies from box-on-box (older) to fast-erect hydraulic raise systems (modern)

## FlexRig and the modern pad rig

Helmerich & Payne FlexRig is the canonical modern pad-rig product family — AC-drive, walking-system, high-spec — and has become an industry archetype. See [Helmerich & Payne FlexRig](../entities/helmerich-payne-flexrig.md) entity stub.

## Pad drilling

Multiple wells from one surface pad, drilled sequentially. Rig walks 20-60 ft between wellheads without rig-down. Pad drilling is the dominant unconventional / shale workflow (Bakken, Eagle Ford, Permian Basin, Marcellus, Haynesville) — slots-per-pad has climbed from 2-4 (early shale) to 8-16+ (modern multi-well pads).
