# Plug and Abandonment (P&A) Overview

## Scope

Plug and abandonment is the **lifecycle-terminal operation** for every well — the engineering deliverable that permanently isolates the wellbore once production has ended. P&A is the most regulator-attended operation in a well's life because the goal (eternal containment of fluids) is harsh: the barriers placed today must hold for centuries.

## Objectives

1. **Permanent isolation** of all flow paths from formation to surface
2. **Restoration of the cap rock seal** — placing cement across the cap-rock interval reseals the geological barrier the wellbore breached
3. **Removal of surface infrastructure** — wellhead + tree removed; site restored

## Regulatory drivers by jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Primary regulator | Reference standard |
|---|---|---|
| US OCS | BSEE | 30 CFR Part 250 Subpart Q |
| UK / UKCS | HSE / OGUK | Oil & Gas UK Well Decommissioning Guidelines |
| Norway / NCS | PSA | NORSOK D-010 |
| Other | National operator-association | IOGP 484 typically referenced |

## Cost driver

P&A is a real cost burden — modern offshore P&A can cost $1-10 million per well, with deepwater wells running $20-50+ million each. Operators carry P&A liabilities on their balance sheets as decommissioning provisions. Mature basins (North Sea, GoM Shelf) have massive P&A inventories.

## P&A timing

- **End-of-life P&A** — well production has terminated; abandonment is irrevocable
- **Suspended abandonment (TA)** — temporary; future re-entry possible; lower barrier set
- **Field-decommissioning campaign** — multiple wells P&A'd together at field abandonment
- **Mid-life P&A** — rare; well abandoned before end of production due to integrity issues
