# Underbalanced Drilling (UBD)

## Scope

Underbalanced drilling deliberately keeps bottomhole pressure (BHP) **below** formation pore pressure so that formation fluid flows continuously into the wellbore during drilling. The two primary benefits: (1) **reservoir protection** — fluid-loss into formation is eliminated, protecting depleted or damage-sensitive reservoirs from skin formation, (2) **productivity-testing-while-drilling** — formation deliverability is observable as it's drilled.

## When UBD is used

- **Depleted reservoirs** — conventional overbalanced drilling can damage the residual production
- **Carbonate reservoirs** — naturally fractured carbonates with high productivity but loss-prone
- **Reservoir-characterization drilling** — productivity testing during drilling provides real-time data
- **Hard-rock arid wells** — air-drilling for very hard formations where conventional mud is inefficient

## When UBD is NOT used

- Wells with strong H2S (toxic) or substantial gas (flammable) production — surface handling of gas / H2S during drilling exceeds risk threshold
- Unstable wellbore formations — underbalance reduces wellbore-stabilization fluid pressure
- Wells where well-control is the primary concern — UBD inverts the overbalance safety margin

## Surface equipment additions over conventional

- **Rotating control device (RCD)** — top of drill string, same as MPD
- **Separator system** — surface 4-phase separator for oil / gas / water / cuttings
- **Closed-loop circulation** — continuous flow even during pipe connections
- **Gas-handling** — flare or gas-recovery infrastructure
- **H2S monitoring** — continuous H2S sensors + alarms + scrubber if produced
