# P&A Cement Plug Design

## Scope

Cement plugs are the most common P&A barrier element. Designing one involves selecting depth (against formation strength), length (against pressure-test capability), volume (to fill the geometry with margin), slurry recipe (against downhole conditions), and the cement-column-behind-casing context that supports the plug.

## Typical plug specification (NORSOK D-010 baseline)

- **Length**: typically 100 m for primary (load-bearing) plug; 50 m for secondary
- **Depth**: across the formation interval being isolated, with safety margin into surrounding zones
- **Formation behind plug**: the casing OD across the plug interval must have verified cement-to-formation behind it (CBL/VDL evidence required)
- **Slurry density**: matched to formation pressure (over-balanced) but not exceeding fracture pressure
- **Compressive strength target**: typically 500-1,000 psi at test time

## Plug-placement methods

### Balance plug method (most common)

- Cement slurry is pumped down drill pipe; displacement fluid above maintains balance
- When pipe is pulled, slurry settles into the planned cement plug volume + surface above
- Requires careful displacement-volume calculation to avoid contamination

### Cement squeeze (with mechanical plug)

- Mechanical bridge plug or packer set below target depth
- Cement squeezed through perforations or via piping against the mechanical plug as base
- Used where balance plug is impractical (e.g., across casing-shoe perforations)

### Plug-set-on-balanced-mud-cap

- For underbalanced or marginal-stability conditions
- Cement slurry placed on top of a mud cap that maintains pressure balance

## Common design factors

- **Maximum anticipated pressure**: plug must hold against this with margin
- **Pressure-test capability**: top-of-plug surface must be accessible for positive test
- **Formation behind plug**: critical — without cement-to-formation behind the casing, the "plug" only seals inside the casing; formation fluid can still migrate vertically behind the casing wall
- **Slurry composition for service**: salt-saturated for salt-section plugs; sulfate-resistant where required
