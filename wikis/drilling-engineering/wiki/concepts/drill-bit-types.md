# Drill-Bit Types

## Scope

Physical taxonomy of drill bits used in rotary drilling operations. The IADC classification codes (covered separately on [iadc-roller-cone-code](iadc-roller-cone-code.md) and [iadc-pdc-code](iadc-pdc-code.md)) overlay this physical taxonomy.

## Primary types

### Roller-cone (tricone)

Three rotating cones mounted on the bit body, each with teeth or tungsten-carbide buttons. Cones rotate about their own axes as the bit rotates; teeth crush and chip rock. Two cutter types:

- **Milled-tooth** — integral teeth machined from the cone steel. Soft / medium formations.
- **Tungsten-carbide insert (TCI)** — pressed-in carbide buttons. Medium / hard / abrasive formations.

Bearing types:

- **Open-roller** — older, less common today
- **Sealed-roller** — bearing-grease retained by O-ring seal
- **Sealed-journal (friction)** — journal-bearing slider; longest life, dominates modern tricone bits

### Fixed-cutter (PDC)

PDC = Polycrystalline Diamond Compact. Synthetic-diamond cutters bonded to a tungsten-carbide substrate, mounted in fixed pockets on a steel or matrix-body bit. No rotating cones — the entire bit rotates as a unit and the cutters shear rock by ploughing-style cutting action. Dominant bit type in soft / medium / firm formations; rapid market-share growth since 1990s.

### Natural-diamond

Industrial diamonds set in matrix-body bits. Used in very hard / abrasive formations (chert, quartzite) where PDC cutter wear is excessive. Less common since PDC technology improved; specific niches.

### Hybrid

Combines roller-cone and PDC cutters on the same bit body. Roller cones handle hard interbeds; PDC cutters handle the bulk-section soft / medium intervals. Used in interbedded formations where neither pure tricone nor pure PDC is optimal.

### Bicenter / eccentric

Asymmetric cutter pattern that drills a hole larger than the bit pass-through diameter. Used to drill a larger hole below a casing shoe than the casing ID allows — alternative to underreaming.

## Selection drivers (high level)

- **Soft / medium formation, high ROP target** → PDC
- **Hard / abrasive formation** → TCI roller-cone or natural-diamond
- **Interbedded hard / soft sections** → hybrid
- **Below-casing-shoe larger hole** → bicenter
- **Air / underbalanced drilling** → specialized PDC or roller-cone with air-circulation features
