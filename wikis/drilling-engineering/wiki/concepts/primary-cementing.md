# Primary Cementing

## Scope

The cement-placement operation that seals the casing-to-formation annulus after running casing — the engineering deliverable that determines whether the casing string isolates formations as designed. Primary cementing is the most-leveraged operational variable for long-term well integrity.

## Standard job sequence

1. **Run casing to setting depth** with centralizers spaced for adequate stand-off
2. **Circulate bottoms-up** through the new casing to clean filter cake and condition the mud
3. **Pump spacer** ahead of cement — fluid intermediate between mud and cement; mechanically scrubs and chemically separates the two
4. **Pump lead cement** — typically lower-density lightweight slurry filling the bulk of the annulus
5. **Pump tail cement** — denser high-strength slurry covering the shoe area and any zone of interest
6. **Bump top plug** against landing collar — wipes the casing ID and signals end-of-displacement (pressure rise)
7. **Wait on cement (WOC)** until compressive strength threshold is reached
8. **Drill out** float collar and shoe-track cement
9. **Pressure test** — FIT or LOT at new shoe per the [Casing Shoe Track](casing-shoe-track.md) procedure

## Mud-removal mechanisms

Achieving good cement bond requires the cement to **displace** the mud cleanly. The four mud-removal mechanisms:

1. **Annular velocity** — sufficient to maintain turbulent or near-turbulent flow
2. **Pipe rotation / reciprocation** — disrupts gelled mud
3. **Spacer chemistry** — incompatible with mud, compatible with cement
4. **Centralizer stand-off** — ≥ 70% recommended; concentric annulus prevents channeling

## Failure signatures

- **Lost circulation** during job — formation fractures; cement doesn't reach planned top
- **Premature plug bump** — cement set early; partial displacement
- **Late or no plug bump** — over-displacement or plug bypass; cement may be in wrong location
- **Poor CBL log** — channels and free water identified post-WOC
