# Platform Rig

## Scope

Drilling rig installed on a fixed offshore production platform — typically a jacket (steel-truss), gravity-base (concrete), or compliant tower. Platform rigs drill development wells from the platform's pre-determined slot pattern and perform workovers / interventions on existing platform wells. Distinct from MODUs in that the rig is platform-fixed, not platform-mobile.

## Class characteristics

- **Configuration** — purpose-built rig package designed for the specific platform; or rented modular rig package skidded onto the platform deck for a campaign
- **Slot pattern** — fixed in the platform jacket; well count typically 12-40 conductors with directional / extended-reach drilling from a single platform
- **Drill-floor mobility** — skid-base allows the rig to traverse the slot pattern
- **Footprint** — smaller than any MODU drill floor due to platform deck space constraints
- **Logistics** — supply from beach (helicopter / vessel) rather than rig hull

## Platform types hosting drilling rigs

- **Steel jacket platforms** — most common; rig deck on the top of the jacket
- **Gravity-base structures (GBS)** — concrete substructure (Hibernia, Troll A); rig integrated with the GBS
- **Compliant towers** — slender steel towers compliant in surge / sway; rig integrated topside (Bullwinkle, Petronius)
- **Spar platforms** — vertical cylinder; rig integrated topside (Perdido, Holstein, Mad Dog)
- **TLP — tension-leg platform** — taut-leg moored floater; rig integrated topside (Ursa, Magnolia, Brutus)

## Where the drilling-engineering scope cuts

Drilling-engineering scope covers the rig drilling package, the operational drilling program, and the conductor-to-bottomhole well design. Platform structural design (jacket fatigue, GBS gravity loads, compliant-tower dynamics) and topside structural integration are out of scope for drilling-engineering and live in marine-engineering / [engineering-standards](../../../engineering-standards/wiki/index.md).
