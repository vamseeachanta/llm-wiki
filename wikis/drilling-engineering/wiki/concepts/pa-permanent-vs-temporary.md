# P&A Permanent vs Temporary

## Scope

Wells can be plugged and abandoned permanently OR temporarily suspended. The distinction matters for: (1) barrier-set requirements (permanent stricter), (2) regulatory reporting status, (3) operator's balance-sheet liability provision, (4) future-re-entry option.

## Permanent abandonment

- **Intent**: well is permanently isolated; no future re-entry planned
- **Barriers**: full two-barrier set per [IOGP 484](../standards/iogp-484.md) / [NORSOK D-010](../standards/norsok-d-010.md)
- **Surface**: wellhead removed (offshore: cut below seabed; onshore: cut + buried below frost line); Christmas tree removed
- **Reservoir restoration**: cap-rock seal restored by cement plug across cap-rock interval
- **Regulatory status**: "permanently abandoned" — closed file
- **Balance sheet**: liability provision retired (with potential residual environmental-remediation liability)

## Temporary suspension (TA)

- **Intent**: well isolated for an indefinite period; future re-entry possible
- **Barriers**: at minimum, one verified barrier above the reservoir; usually less rigorous than permanent
- **Surface**: wellhead retained; tree may be retained or temporarily plugged
- **Reservoir restoration**: not full cap-rock plug; reservoir-side isolation only
- **Regulatory status**: "temporarily abandoned" — file remains open; periodic integrity reporting may be required
- **Balance sheet**: liability provision retained; eventual full P&A still required

## Why TA exists as a category

- **Pre-workover suspension** — well taken offline pending workover decision; TA is the safe interim
- **Field-shut-in pending price recovery** — economic abandonment with hope of future re-development
- **Equipment migration** — well goes offline while production system is upgraded
- **Pre-decommissioning queueing** — operator prefers to batch P&A operations; TA holds wells until campaign

## Conversion between states

- **TA → Permanent** — straightforward; add required additional barriers + remove surface
- **Permanent → re-active** — not formally allowed in most jurisdictions; requires regulatory approval as new well
