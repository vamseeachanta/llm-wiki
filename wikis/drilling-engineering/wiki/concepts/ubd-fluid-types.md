# UBD Fluid Types

## Scope

Underbalanced drilling requires a continuous-phase fluid lighter than conventional mud to achieve below-pore-pressure BHP at downhole depth. Five primary UBD fluid types exist along a spectrum from lightest (pure air) to heaviest (aerated mud). Selection drives both bottomhole-pressure target and operational practicality (hole cleaning, equipment compatibility).

## Five fluid types

### 1. Pure air / nitrogen

- Lowest BHP achievable
- For hard-rock arid environments (no formation water complications)
- Cuttings transport via high-velocity air; works only with sufficient annular velocity
- **Application**: hard-rock drilling, surface holes in arid regions

### 2. Mist

- Water droplets injected into air stream
- Helps with hole cleaning when fine fines are present
- Slightly higher BHP than pure air
- **Application**: same as air but with minor formation moisture

### 3. Foam

- Surfactant-stabilized air-water mixture (~ 80-95% air, 5-20% water by volume)
- Excellent cuttings transport via high-viscosity stable bubble structure
- **Standard UBD fluid for most applications**
- BHP ~25-50% of equivalent water column at the same depth

### 4. Gasified mud

- Conventional drilling mud with nitrogen injection at surface
- Lower BHP than mud alone but maintains some mud-like properties (filter cake, fluid loss control)
- Bubble distribution can be irregular → BHP variability
- **Application**: depleted reservoirs where some filter-cake formation is OK

### 5. Aerated mud

- Discrete air bubbles in continuous mud phase
- Smaller air-fraction than gasified mud
- Easier to handle at surface but smaller BHP reduction
- **Application**: marginal underbalance applications

## Surface separation requirements

UBD returns contain a mixture of:
- Continuous-phase fluid (air / foam / mud)
- Formation fluid (oil / gas / water) — the productivity signal
- Cuttings

Surface 4-phase separator separates these; gas is flared (or recovered to surface facilities); oil + water are processed; cuttings go to drilling-waste disposal.
