# Rig Classes Overview

## Scope

Taxonomy of drilling rigs by deployment mode and structural configuration. This router page links to the dedicated concept pages for the five primary rig classes plus the [MODU](modu.md) collective term for offshore mobile drilling units.

## The five primary classes

| Class | Deployment | Water-depth range | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Jackup Rig](jackup-rig.md) | Bottom-supported, three legs jacked down to seabed | 0 - 500 ft typical; harsh-environment to 700 ft | Continental-shelf development drilling |
| [Semi-Submersible Rig](semi-submersible-rig.md) | Column-stabilized floater, ballasted pontoons | 200 - 10,000 ft | Deepwater exploration, harsh-environment development |
| [Drillship](drillship.md) | Ship-shaped DP-station-kept floater | 1,000 - 12,000+ ft | Ultra-deepwater exploration, long-reach development |
| [Platform Rig](platform-rig.md) | Permanent / temporary on production platform | Platform jacket water depth | Platform-development drilling, infill, workover |
| [Land Rig](land-rig.md) | Truck / trailer / pad-mounted | N/A (onshore) | Onshore drilling — conventional, unconventional, shale |

## The MODU collective

[MODU](modu.md) — Mobile Offshore Drilling Unit — is the regulatory and class-society umbrella covering jackups, semis, and drillships when classed as marine vessels for transit and station-keeping. Platform rigs are not MODUs (they're fixed installations). Land rigs are not MODUs (they're mobile but not offshore-marine-vessel).

## Selection drivers (high-level)

- **Water depth** — the gating constraint. Jackups for shallow, semis for mid, drillships for ultra-deep.
- **Environment severity** — harsh-environment (North Sea, Arctic, Barents) demands purpose-built rigs; benign-environment (Gulf of Mexico routine, West Africa) accepts standard-spec rigs.
- **Operation type** — exploration favors high-capability floaters; development from a fixed platform favors platform rigs; routine onshore favors fit-for-purpose land rigs.
- **Day-rate vs efficiency** — high-spec rigs cost more per day but drill faster; total-well-cost optimization rarely picks the lowest day rate.
